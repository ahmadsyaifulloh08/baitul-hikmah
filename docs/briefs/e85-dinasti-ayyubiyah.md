## Character Lock
- **Saladin**: Kurdish military commander with neat beard, chain mail under green robes, noble leadership presence
- **Ayyubid princes**: Saladin's relatives in fine military dress, governing different regions
- **Mamluk soldiers**: Elite cavalry in armor with curved swords, disciplined formation
- **Christian Crusaders**: Knights in metal armor with red crosses, opposing army

## Slides

### slide-01 — Saladin mendirikan Dinasti Ayyubiyah di Cairo
Establishing shot of Cairo Citadel with Saladin on horseback reviewing troops. Kurdish-origin Saladin in chain mail and green robes establishing new dynasty after overthrowing Fatimids. Mamluk cavalry in formation. Cairo skyline with Nile River visible. Colors: brown citadel stone, green Saladin's banner, gold Cairo architecture, blue Nile waters, white horse. Mood: military authority, new dynasty rising, strategic leadership.

### slide-02 — Sistem pemerintahan federasi keluarga Ayyubiyah
Medium shot map-style view showing Ayyubid territories across Syria, Egypt, and Yemen with different family members ruling. Saladin's brothers and sons governing Damascus, Aleppo, Hama. Administrative efficiency and family cooperation. Trade routes connecting regions. Colors: blue territorial boundaries, green Ayyubid banners, brown trade caravans, gold prosperous cities, white administrative documents. Mood: organized governance, family unity, territorial management.

### slide-03 — Peperangan dengan Tentara Salib di Hattin
Wide shot of Ayyubid forces facing Crusader army at Horns of Hattin. Saladin strategically positioned on hills with disciplined Muslim cavalry below. Crusader knights in heavy armor struggling in heat. Decisive moment before major victory. Colors: brown battlefield terrain, green Muslim banners, red Crusader crosses, blue military strategy, gray armor glinting. Mood: strategic confrontation, military genius, historical turning point.

### slide-04 — Transisi ke Mamluk setelah perpecahan internal
Close-up of later Ayyubid princes in palace council showing family disagreements. Mamluk military commanders gaining influence in background. Documents showing territorial divisions. Political fragmentation leading to eventual Mamluk takeover. Colors: red conflict tension, brown palace interior, gold weakening authority, green Mamluk rising power, white diplomatic papers. Mood: political decline, family division, changing power structure.
