# Translation Movement: Bridge of Civilizations 🌉

---

## 🏛 Do You Know?

When Muslims spread from Arabia to Syria, Egypt, and Persia, they discovered **treasures of knowledge** from ancient civilizations. In Alexandria, there was a great library with Greek books about medicine. In India, there was an advanced mathematical system with the number zero. In Persia, there were amazing works on astronomy.

But all this knowledge was written in foreign languages: Greek, Sanskrit, Persian. Muslims couldn't read them! So began the **Translation Movement** — a massive project to translate all the world's knowledge into Arabic.

**Khalid ibn Yazid** (685-704 CE), an Umayyad prince, became the first pioneer. He brought translators from Alexandria to translate books on alchemy and astronomy. Khalid even learned Greek himself!

## 🔬 What Is the Translation Movement?

During **Caliph Al-Ma'mun's** time (813-833 CE), this movement reached its peak. Al-Ma'mun established **Bayt al-Hikmah** (House of Wisdom) in Baghdad — the greatest translation center in history!

**Hunayn ibn Ishaq** became the master translator extraordinaire. He didn't just translate, but also collected many manuscripts for comparison to ensure accuracy. Hunayn mastered four languages: Arabic, Greek, Syriac, and Persian.

Hunayn's working method was very meticulous: first he translated from Greek to Syriac (his mother tongue), then from Syriac to Arabic. With this method, he successfully translated **129 works of Galen** on medicine — even more than what survived in the original Greek!

## 🌍 Impact on the World

The translation movement changed the world! **Banu Musa** — three genius brothers — not only translated books on mathematics and mechanics, but also created the first **automatic robots** in history.

**Al-Kindi**, the "Arab Philosopher," translated Aristotle's works while developing Islamic philosophy. **Al-Khwarizmi** translated Indian mathematical books and created **algebra** — a science whose name comes from his book title "Al-Jabr".

From Baghdad, knowledge spread to **Cordoba**, **Cairo**, and **Samarkand**. Librarian Al-Hakam II in Cordoba collected 400,000 manuscripts! When Europe was still in darkness, the Islamic world became a **lighthouse of civilization** illuminating the entire world.

## 📖 Islam & Knowledge

The Quran teaches: "Read in the name of your Lord... He taught man what he did not know." The translation movement embodied this verse — Muslims sought knowledge wherever it was, from whatever civilization.

Amazingly, **translators from various religions** worked together: Muslims, Christians, Jews, and Zoroastrians. They believed **truth knows no boundaries of religion or ethnicity**. Al-Ma'mun said: "We must seek truth wherever it may be."

Thanks to this movement, we have scientific terms still used today: **alchemy** (chemistry), **algebra**, **algorithm**, **alcohol**. Even the numbers we use daily (1, 2, 3...) come from the Indian system translated into Arabic, then to Europe as "Arabic numerals"!
