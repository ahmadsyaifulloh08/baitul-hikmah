# Standardization of the Quran Mushaf 📖

---

## 🏛 Do You Know?

During Caliph Uthman ibn Affan's time, a big problem arose. **Muslim soldiers** from different regions were arguing about how to read the **Quran**. Soldiers from Syria read differently from those from Iraq. They kept saying: "Our way of reading is correct!"

**Hudhaifah ibn al-Yaman** witnessed this conflict and worried that Muslims would split apart. He quickly went to Caliph Uthman in Medina and said: "O Commander of the Believers, save this nation before they quarrel about their book like the Jews and Christians did!"

Uthman understood this danger. Muslims needed to have **one identical mushaf** throughout the world.

## 🔬 What Is Mushaf Standardization?

Caliph Uthman formed a **special team** to create identical mushaf copies for everyone. This team was led by **Zaid ibn Thabit** — the same person who had compiled the Quran during Abu Bakr's time.

The other team members were **Abdullah ibn al-Zubair**, **Sa'id ibn al-'As**, and **Abdurrahman ibn al-Harith**. They worked very carefully. Every verse was checked against Hafsa's mushaf (daughter of Umar) and with the memorization of **Quran reciters** who were still alive.

Uthman instructed: "If you disagree about spelling, use the **Quraish dialect** because the Quran was revealed in their language."

## 🌍 Impact on the Islamic World

After the new mushaf was completed, Uthman sent **official copies** to major cities: Mecca, Basra, Kufa, Syria, and Yemen. Each mushaf was accompanied by a **Quran teacher** skilled in teaching the correct recitation.

Then Uthman made a bold decision: **all different old mushaf copies must be destroyed**. Some were burned, others washed with water. The purpose was so there would be no more arguments about Quran recitation.

Some companions initially objected, like Abdullah ibn Mas'ud who had his personal mushaf. But the majority of companions supported Uthman because they knew this was important for **Muslim unity**.

## 📖 Islam & Knowledge

This mushaf standardization became a **historical miracle**. Imagine if Uthman hadn't acted — Muslims in different countries would continue fighting about the Quran. Over time, small differences could become major splits.

The **Uthmani Mushaf** created 1,400 years ago remains the standard today. Every Quran printed in Medina, Cairo, Jakarta, or any city in the world uses **identical script** — all referring to the wise decision of Caliph Uthman.

This proves Allah's promise in the Quran: "Indeed, We have sent down the reminder, and We will preserve it." Allah preserved the Quran through the hands of wise people like Uthman ibn Affan.
