## 🌍 What Happened?

In 830 CE, Caliph al-Ma'mun led the greatest translation movement in history. Thousands of books from Greece, Persia, and India were translated into Arabic at the Bayt al-Hikmah in Baghdad.

Hunayn ibn Ishaq and his team worked day and night translating great works like Galen's medical books, Aristotle's philosophy, and Euclid's mathematics. They not only translated but also created new terms in Arabic.

The translation team used a three-stage system: from the original language to Syriac, then improved, then to Arabic. This system ensured accurate and easily understood translations.

## 📖 The Story

Al-Ma'mun greatly loved knowledge. He sent envoys to Constantinople to bring Greek manuscripts. The Byzantine Emperor gave hundreds of books as part of a peace agreement.

Bayt al-Hikmah became the world's center of learning. Translators of various religions worked together: Muslims, Christians, Jews, and Sabians. What mattered was their ability, not their religion.

Thabit ibn Qurrah created new words in Arabic for mathematical and philosophical concepts that didn't exist yet. These words were then used by all Muslim scientists until now.

## 🌟 The Lesson

The translation movement taught that knowledge knows no borders of country or religion. Muslims learned from Greeks, Persians, and Indians, then developed it to become better.

Arabic became an international scientific language like English today. Scientists from around the world wrote works in Arabic so everyone could read them.

The translated works were later sent to Europe and helped start the Renaissance. Universities in Europe used Latin translations of Arabic works.
