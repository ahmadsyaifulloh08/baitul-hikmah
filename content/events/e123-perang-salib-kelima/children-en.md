# The Fifth Crusade - Attack on Egypt

## 🌍 What happened?

In 1217 CE, the Crusader armies decided to attack Egypt first, rather than going directly to Jerusalem. They thought that by controlling Egypt, they could more easily capture the Holy Land.

Cardinal Pelagius from Rome led this expedition together with John of Brienne, King of Jerusalem. But these two leaders often disagreed because they had different plans.

## 📖 The Story

Their main target was the city of Damietta at the mouth of the Nile River. This city was very important because it controlled the entrance into Egypt. Sultan al-Kamil of the Ayyubid Dynasty defended this city tenaciously.

The siege lasted almost two years! Both sides suffered from hunger and disease. In 1219, al-Kamil made a surprising offer: he would surrender Jerusalem if the Crusader army returned home from Egypt.

But the arrogant Cardinal Pelagius rejected the offer. He wanted total victory and complete control over Egypt. This decision was very bad because al-Kamil's offer was actually better than anything previous crusades had ever achieved.

## 🌟 The Lessons

Finally Damietta fell in November 1219, and the Crusader army became very arrogant. They rejected all peace offers and decided to attack Cairo. This was a very foolish decision.

Al-Kamil cleverly opened the dams of the Nile River, causing a massive flood that surrounded the Crusader forces in the Mansura area. Trapped in the middle of floodwaters, Cardinal Pelagius was forced to surrender and return Damietta.

Most sadly, they failed completely despite having a golden opportunity to get Jerusalem without bloodshed. Arrogance and greed made them lose everything.

## 🤲 Prayer & Guidance

This war taught how dangerous arrogance and greed can be. When Allah provides an opportunity to gain something through good means, we should accept it with gratitude.

Cardinal Pelagius rejected al-Kamil's good offer because of arrogance. As a result, he lost everything. Islam teaches us not to walk arrogantly on the earth.

> وَلَا تَمْشِ فِي الْأَرْضِ مَرَحًا ۖ إِنَّكَ لَن تَخْرِقَ الْأَرْضَ وَلَن تَبْلُغَ الْجِبَالَ طُولًا ﴿٣٧﴾
>
> *"And do not walk upon the earth exultantly. Indeed, you will never tear the earth apart, and you will never reach the mountains in height."* ﴾37﴿

Subhanallah, al-Kamil showed noble character by offering peace, even though in the end he had to defend his country.
