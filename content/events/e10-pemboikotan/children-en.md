# Three Years in the Lonely Valley

## 🏔️ Driven into a Narrow Valley

Once upon a time in the city of Mecca, the Quraysh leaders were very angry. **Prophet Muhammad SAW** kept spreading goodness and inviting people to worship Allah alone. Day by day, more and more people followed him.

The Quraysh leaders gathered together and hatched an evil plan. They wrote an agreement on a sheet of leather and hung it inside the Ka'bah. It stated: no one was allowed to trade, marry, or even speak with the family of **Abu Talib** — the Prophet Muhammad's uncle who always protected him.

So the entire Banu Hashim family had to move to a small, narrow valley on the outskirts of Mecca. The valley was called *Shi'b Abi Talib*. That is where they lived — far from the marketplace, far from neighbors, far from normal life.

## 🍞 Extremely Difficult Days

Imagine, friends, that you couldn't go to a shop to buy bread. You couldn't buy rice, milk, or fruit. No one would even sell you drinking water. That is exactly what the Banu Hashim family endured for nearly three years!

Little children cried from hunger. Their cries could be heard all the way outside the valley. But the Quraysh leaders who passed by only smiled when they heard them. They felt no compassion at all.

**Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas**, one of the Prophet's companions, once told the story of how one night he found a piece of dried camel hide on the ground. Because he was so hungry, he washed it, burned it, ground it up, then mixed it with water and ate it. That piece of camel hide became his food for three days! That is how harsh their life was.

## 🌙 Secret Help

But do you know what? Not all the Quraysh people agreed with this cruelty. There were kind people who secretly helped!

**Hisham ibn Amr**, a brave man, loaded his camel with food every night. Then he released the camel toward the mouth of the valley so the Banu Hashim family could take the food. He did this again and again, even though it would be very dangerous for him if he were caught.

**Hakim ibn Hizam**, the nephew of **Khadijah** (the Prophet Muhammad's wife), also smuggled wheat to his aunt. He did not care about the threats from the Quraysh leaders. To him, letting his family starve was something he simply could not accept.

Allah saw all of this goodness. Every morsel of food sent in secret, every prayer whispered in the quiet of the night — none of it was ever wasted.

## 📜 Termites and a Miracle

After nearly three years, five kind-hearted Quraysh men gathered their courage to speak up. They stood before the Ka'bah and called out: "This is not fair! How can we eat well while the children of Banu Hashim are starving?"

Then a miracle happened! When the evil agreement was taken down from inside the Ka'bah, it turned out that tiny termites had eaten away all the writing! The only words remaining were: *"Bismika Allahumma"* — which means "In Your name, O Allah."

Prophet Muhammad SAW already knew this would happen before the sheet was opened, because Allah had told him through revelation. Subhanallah! Allah always protects those who are patient and faithful.

Finally, the boycott was cancelled. The Banu Hashim family could leave the narrow valley and return to normal life. Three years of patience had come to an end. But they emerged with faith stronger than ever before!
