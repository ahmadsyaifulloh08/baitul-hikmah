# Three Years in the Lonely Valley

## 🏔️ Driven into a Narrow Valley

The Quraysh leaders were very angry. More and more people in Mecca were following **Prophet Muhammad SAW**. So they hatched a cruel plan.

They wrote an agreement on a sheet of leather and hung it inside the Ka'bah. It said: no one may trade, marry, or even speak with the family of **Abu Talib**!

The entire Banu Hashim family had to move to a small, narrow valley called *Shi'b Abi Talib*. Far from the marketplace, far from neighbors, far from normal life.

## 🍞 Hunger and Suffering

Imagine you could not buy bread, rice, milk, or even drinking water. That is what the Banu Hashim family endured for nearly three years!

Little children cried from hunger. Their cries could be heard outside the valley. But the Quraysh leaders who passed by only smiled. They felt no compassion at all.

> **🎨 Illustration Brief:**
> *Inside the dark valley at night. A thin mother holds a crying child. Other families huddle together. A tiny fire flickers. Colors of deep blue, faint orange fire, earthy brown. Mood heartbreaking. Close-up composition with families around the small fire.*

---

## 🦴 Sa'd and the Dried Camel Hide

**Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas**, one of the Prophet's companions, once found a piece of dried camel hide on the ground. Because he was so hungry, he washed it, burned it, ground it up, mixed it with water, and ate it.

That piece of camel hide became his food for three whole days! That is how harsh their life in the valley was.

> **🎨 Illustration Brief:**
> *Nighttime. A young man (Sa'd) sits near a small fire, holding a piece of dried hide. Stars visible in the sky. Colors of deep night blue, small fire orange, brown earth. Mood touching and somber. Close-up with the fire as the center light source.*

## 🌙 Secret Help in the Night

Not all the Quraysh agreed with this cruelty. **Hisham ibn Amr** loaded his camel with food every night and released it toward the valley so the family could eat.

**Hakim ibn Hizam**, the nephew of Khadijah, also smuggled wheat to his aunt. He did not care about the threats. Letting family starve was something he could not accept.

> **🎨 Illustration Brief:**
> *A moonlit desert night near the valley mouth. A man (Hisham) leads a camel loaded with food sacks toward a gap in the rocks. Hands reach out from behind the rocks. Colors of silvery blue moonlight, white sand, brown camel. Mood hopeful and courageous.*

---

## 📜 The Miracle of the Termites

After nearly three years, five brave Quraysh men stood before the Ka'bah and spoke up: "This is not fair! How can we eat well while children are starving?"

Then a miracle happened! When the agreement was taken down, tiny termites had eaten all the writing. The only words left were: *"Bismika Allahumma"* — "In Your name, O Allah."

> **🎨 Illustration Brief:**
> *In front of the Ka'bah on a bright day. A man holds a sheet of leather full of tiny holes, stunned. Quraysh people stand around with amazed faces. Colors of golden sunlight, white Ka'bah, brown leather. Mood of triumph and wonder. Central composition — the hole-riddled sheet as focal point.*

---

## 🕊️ Freedom at Last

The boycott was finally cancelled! The Banu Hashim family could leave the narrow valley and return to normal life.

Three years of patience had come to an end. But they emerged with faith stronger than ever before. Allah always protects those who are patient and faithful. Subhanallah!

> **🎨 Illustration Brief:**
> *Sunrise over the valley mouth. Families walking out into the golden light, faces full of relief and joy. Children running ahead. Colors of golden sunrise, warm brown, bright sky. Mood of liberation and hope. Wide composition — people emerging from the dark valley into light.*
