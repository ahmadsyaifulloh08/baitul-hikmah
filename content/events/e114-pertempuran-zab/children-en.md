# The Battle of the Great Zab - End of Umayyad, Beginning of Abbasid (750 CE) - Children's Content

## 🌍 What Happened?

In 750 CE, a great battle occurred that changed Islamic history. The Umayyad dynasty that had ruled for almost 100 years (661-750 CE) faced a serious challenge from the Abbasid family. The Abbasid family were descendants of Abbas ibn Abd al-Muttalib, uncle of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.

Caliph Marwan II, the last Umayyad ruler, had to fight a revolutionary movement led by Abu al-Abbas as-Saffah and Abu Ja'far al-Mansur. This movement was supported by many people dissatisfied with Umayyad rule, especially non-Arabs who felt discriminated against.

## 📖 The Battle Story

Abu Muslim al-Khurasani, the charismatic Abbasid general, successfully organized forces consisting of Persians, Turkics, and Arabs. They used the slogan "*ar-rida min al Muhammad*" (the one approved from Muhammad's family) to attract support from various groups dissatisfied with the Umayyads.

The two armies met at the Great Zab River in January 750 CE. Marwan II led 120,000 Umayyad troops, while Abdullah ibn Ali led 200,000 Abbasid forces. This location was strategic because it controlled access to Baghdad and Damascus.

The battle began with Umayyad cavalry attacks, but Abbasid forces had strong defensive formations. The decisive factor was when some Umayyad troops from Iraq switched sides to the Abbasids. This weakened Umayyad ranks and gave the Abbasids the opportunity to counterattack.

## 🌟 Great Changes

Marwan II was wounded in battle and had to flee. He was pursued to Egypt and was eventually killed in August 750 CE. His death marked the end of the Umayyad dynasty in the East, though one branch of the family managed to survive and establish a kingdom in Cordoba, Spain.

Abu al-Abbas as-Saffah was proclaimed as the first Abbasid caliph in Kufa in February 750 CE. This began a new era in Islamic history. The center of the caliphate moved from Damascus to Baghdad, which was built by Caliph al-Mansur.

This change was not only political, but also cultural. While the Umayyads involved more Arabs and were oriented toward the Mediterranean-Byzantine world, the Abbasids were more inclusive by involving Persians, Turkics, and various other ethnicities, oriented toward Persia-Central Asia.

## 🤲 Prayer & Wisdom

The Battle of the Great Zab teaches that no power is eternal except Allah's power. In the Quran, Allah says:

> قُلِ اللَّهُمَّ مَالِكَ الْمُلْكِ تُؤْتِي الْمُلْكَ مَن تَشَاءُ وَتَنزِعُ الْمُلْكَ مِمَّن تَشَاءُ وَتُعِزُّ مَن تَشَاءُ وَتُذِلُّ مَن تَشَاءُ ۖ بِيَدِكَ الْخَيْرُ ۖ إِنَّكَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ ﴿٢٦﴾
>
> *"Say, 'O Allah, Owner of Sovereignty, You give sovereignty to whom You will and You take sovereignty away from whom You will. You honor whom You will and You humble whom You will. In Your hand is [all] good. Indeed, You are over all things competent.'"* ﴾26﴿

This dynastic change did not mean the decline of Islamic civilization. On the contrary, the Abbasid era became the golden age of science with Bayt al-Hikmah (House of Wisdom) in Baghdad. However, we must also appreciate Umayyad achievements in architecture (Umayyad Mosque, Dome of the Rock) and Islamic expansion. Each era has its own contributions.
