# Mustansiriya University, Baghdad (1227-1234 CE)

## 🏛 Fun Fact

In Baghdad during the 13th century, a caliph named **Al-Mustansir Billah** built a very special university. **Mustansiriya University** was the first university in the world to teach all four Islamic schools of law simultaneously on one campus!

This university had four different faculties: **Hanafi, Maliki, Shafi'i, and Hanbali**. Each faculty had its own professors and library, but all students could learn together and share knowledge.

This university didn't only teach religion. Students also learned **mathematics, astronomy, medicine, history, and philosophy**. There was also a **hospital** and **observatory** for hands-on practice!

Most interesting of all, students from throughout the Islamic world could study **for free**! They received living allowances, food, clothing, even books. There was no discrimination - what mattered was being smart and willing to study hard.

## 🔬 Explanation

**Mustansiriya** was built because Caliph Al-Mustansir wanted to unite Muslims through knowledge. At that time, the caliph's political power was already weak, but he chose to build a university rather than an army.

The educational system at Mustansiriya was very modern for its time. There were **entrance exams** that were strict but based on ability, not social status. Graduates received **ijazah** (diplomas) recognized throughout the Islamic world and could become judges, legal experts, or professors.

🎨 *Illustration: Examination room with prospective students from various regions taking written tests. Background: magnificent room with high dome and Arabic calligraphy. Dominant colors: white, light blue, brown, gold. Mood: concentration and fairness. Composition: rows of test takers, supervisor in center, magnificent architecture surrounding.*

**Famous professors** like **Ibn al-Salah** (hadith expert) and **Ibn al-Nafis** (doctor who discovered blood circulation) taught here. Their research produced important discoveries for scientific advancement.

The Mustansiriya library had **80,000 manuscripts** - the largest collection in the world at that time! There were specialized staff to copy and maintain these precious manuscripts so they wouldn't be damaged by time.

## 🌍 Modern Impact

The **Mustansiriya** university model inspired other great universities like **Al-Azhar** in Egypt. The concept of integrating various schools within one institution demonstrated tolerance and intellectual pluralism.

When Baghdad fell in 1258 CE, some professors and manuscripts were moved to **Damascus** and **Cairo**. Mustansiriya alumni spread throughout the Islamic world, becoming agents of knowledge dissemination.

🎨 *Illustration: Map of Islamic world with migration routes of Mustansiriya alumni after Baghdad's fall. Background: map with important cities like Cairo, Damascus, Cordoba. Characters: alumni with books traveling to various cities, local people welcoming them. Key objects: books, map, migration routes, important cities. Dominant colors: sea blue, land green, gold cities, brown routes. Mood: knowledge spread and hope. Composition: map as main background, alumni figures scattered along migration routes.*

Even today, the concept of **research university** with various integrated faculties pioneered by Mustansiriya is still applied in modern universities. The full scholarship system also became a model for democratizing higher education.

The most important **legacy** of Mustansiriya is proving that investment in education is more valuable than military power. The knowledge spread by their alumni lasted longer than any political power.

## 📖 Islam & Knowledge
