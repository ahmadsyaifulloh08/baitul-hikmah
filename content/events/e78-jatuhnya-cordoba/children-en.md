# The Fall of Cordoba to Reconquista

## 🌍 What happened?

In 1236 CE, the city of Cordoba, once the jewel of Islamic civilization in Europe, fell to Ferdinand III of the Kingdom of Castile. Cordoba was the former capital of the Umayyad Caliphate, very magnificent and a center of Islamic knowledge.

At that time al-Andalus (Muslim Spain) was already divided into small kingdoms called taifas. They were fighting each other and couldn't unite to resist Christian attacks from the north. Ferdinand III cleverly exploited this division.

## 📖 The Story

Ferdinand III began his attack by besieging small cities around Cordoba first, such as Ubeda, Baeza, and Martos. This made Cordoba isolated and unable to get help from outside.

Sadly, when Ibn Hud who ruled Cordoba asked for help from the rulers of Granada and Sevilla, they refused! Each kingdom only thought of itself, not wanting to help fellow Muslims. They were afraid that if they helped Cordoba, Ferdinand would attack them next.

The siege of Cordoba began in spring 1236. Ferdinand used advanced war machines like trebuchets to destroy the city walls. He also blocked the river so food couldn't enter the city.

## 🌟 The Lessons

After several months under siege, on June 29, 1236, Cordoba finally surrendered. Ibn Hud realized that continuing to resist would only cause civilians to be massacred. He decided to surrender to save the lives of civilians.

The beautiful Great Mosque of Cordoba was converted into a cathedral church. Many Muslim residents chose to migrate to Granada or North Africa. Scholars and scientists moved to Granada, bringing their knowledge there.

Most sadly, many libraries and scientific books were burned because they were considered "heretical" by the conquerors. This was a great loss to world knowledge.

## 🤲 Prayer & Guidance

The fall of Cordoba teaches how dangerous division among Muslims can be. If the taifa kingdoms had wanted to unite and help each other, perhaps they could have defended that beautiful city.

Each leader only thought of their own interests, not the interests of the ummah. As a result, they were defeated one by one by enemies who were more united. Allah SWT has warned us not to be divided:

> وَاعْتَصِمُوا بِحَبْلِ اللَّهِ جَمِيعًا وَلَا تَفَرَّقُوا ۚ وَاذْكُرُوا نِعْمَتَ اللَّهِ عَلَيْكُمْ إِذْ كُنتُمْ أَعْدَاءً فَأَلَّفَ بَيْنَ قُلُوبِكُمْ فَأَصْبَحْتُم بِنِعْمَتِهِ إِخْوَانًا ﴿١٠٣﴾
>
> *"And hold firmly to the rope of Allah all together and do not become divided. And remember the favor of Allah upon you - when you were enemies and He brought your hearts together and you became, by His favor, brothers."* ﴾103﴿

Subhanallah, unity is strength, division is weakness.
