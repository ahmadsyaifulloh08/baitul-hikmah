# The Battle of Khaybar ⚔️

---

## The Fortresses in the North

North of Madinah, there was a place called **Khaybar**. There stood **seven great fortresses** on top of rocky hills. The people there were very wealthy and had many weapons.

Unfortunately, the leaders of Khaybar kept planning attacks on Madinah. They invited other Arab tribes to fight the Muslims. They were the masterminds behind the very dangerous Battle of the Trench!

After the peace treaty with the Quraysh, **Prophet Muhammad ﷺ** decided it was time to stop the threat from Khaybar. He set out with about **1,600 companions**.

## Ali the Brave

The battle at Khaybar was very tough. The fortresses were incredibly strong — thick walls on top of hills. **Abu Bakr** tried to attack one fortress, but couldn't break through. **Umar** tried next, but couldn't either.

Then one night, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said: *"Tomorrow I will give this banner to a man who loves Allah and His Messenger, and whom Allah and His Messenger love. Allah will grant victory through his hands."*

Every companion hoped to be that person! The next morning, the Prophet asked: "Where is Ali?" It turned out **Ali ibn Abi Talib** was suffering from a painful eye illness. The Prophet called him and blew on his eyes — and miraculously, Ali's eyes were healed instantly!

Ali marched to **Fortress Qamus** — the strongest fort in Khaybar. The mightiest warrior of Khaybar, named **Marhab**, challenged him to single combat. Ali accepted the challenge and won!

## Victory and Fairness

One by one, the fortresses of Khaybar fell. In about **ten days**, all of Khaybar was conquered!

But here's the interesting part — Prophet Muhammad ﷺ did not drive the people of Khaybar away. He made a fair agreement: they could stay and keep farming, as long as they gave **half of the harvest** to the Muslims. The people of Khaybar agreed because they were expert farmers.

This shows that Islam does not teach us to destroy a defeated enemy. Skills and hard work are always valued!

## Lessons from Khaybar

⚔️ **The right person for the task** — The Prophet gave the banner to Ali, not based on seniority, but because Ali was the best person for the job. Everyone has their own special strengths!

💪 **Courage comes from faith** — Ali was ill, but with Allah's help through the Prophet's prayer, he was healed and became a hero. When we trust in Allah, we can face any challenge!

🤝 **Win with fairness** — After winning, the Prophet didn't destroy everything. He made a fair deal. Winning doesn't mean being cruel!

🛡️ **Stay alert** — Even after victory, we must remain careful. Goodness must be guarded at all times.
