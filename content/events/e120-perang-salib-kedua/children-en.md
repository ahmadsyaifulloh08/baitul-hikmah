# The Second Crusade — Failure at Damascus (1147-1149 CE)

## 🌍 What Happened?

In 1144 CE, something surprising happened in the Levant region. **Imad al-Din Zangi**, the Muslim ruler of Aleppo, successfully recaptured the city of Edessa from the Crusaders. This was the first time a Crusader-controlled city was successfully retaken by Muslims.

This news spread quickly throughout Europe and made Christian kings very worried. Pope Eugenius III then called for all of Europe to wage war again in the Levant. This time, the two strongest kings of Europe joined directly: Louis VII of France and Conrad III of Germany.

Both kings brought massive armies to the Levant via the overland route. However, their journey was not easy. Many of their soldiers died in attacks by Seljuk Muslim forces in the mountains before reaching the Levant.

## 📖 The Story

After arriving in the Levant with remaining forces that had been greatly reduced, the Crusader kings met with **Baldwin III**, king of Jerusalem. They held a great meeting in the city of Acre to decide which city to attack.

Their decision turned out to be unwise. They chose to attack Damascus, even though this city was not actually hostile to them. Damascus under **Mu'in al-Din Unur** even often cooperated with Jerusalem against their common enemy from Aleppo.

In July 1148 CE, the combined Crusader forces besieged Damascus. This city had high and strong walls. **Nur al-Din**, son of Imad al-Din Zangi, immediately sent reinforcements to defend Damascus. The Crusaders initially succeeded in entering the gardens outside the city, but then they made a mistake.

## 🌟 The Lesson

The siege of Damascus lasted only four days. The Crusaders decided to change their attack position from the west side of the city (which was easy to attack) to the east side (which had stronger defenses). This decision was a big mistake.

Muslim forces managed to cut off the Crusaders' food and supply lines. Meanwhile, reinforcements from Aleppo were getting closer. Facing an increasingly difficult situation, the Crusader leaders finally decided to retreat.

In the hasty withdrawal, they lost much military equipment. This failure at Damascus was a heavy blow to the Crusaders and simultaneously strengthened the Muslim position in the Levant region.

This failure taught the importance of making wise decisions in warfare. The Crusaders should not have attacked their allies, but instead cooperated against common enemies.

## 🤲 Prayer & Scripture

From this event, we learn that Allah SWT grants victory to His servants who unite and are patient. Muslim leaders like Nur al-Din succeeded because they united their forces against the enemy.

The failure of the Second Crusade became the first step toward Muslim unity in the Levant. This unity would later reach its peak when Saladin al-Ayyubi successfully recaptured Jerusalem in 1187 CE.

Allah SWT says about the importance of unity and patience:

>
> *"And indeed, We have written in the Zabur after the Reminder that the righteous among My servants shall inherit the earth."* ﴾105﴿

This verse reminds us that Allah will grant victory to those who believe and do good deeds. Subhanallah.
