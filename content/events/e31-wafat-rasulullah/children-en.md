# The Death of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ 🌙

---

## The Prophet Falls Ill

After completing the Farewell Hajj, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ began to feel unwell. He suffered from a **very high fever** — even worse than what ordinary people experience.

As the illness grew worse, the Prophet ﷺ wanted to stay in the room of his wife **Aisha**. There, Aisha took care of him with great love and tenderness. She would recite protective prayers while gently passing the Prophet's own hand over his body.

Even though he was very ill, the Prophet ﷺ still climbed up to the mosque pulpit to speak to his companions. He told them about "a servant whom Allah gave a choice between this world and meeting Allah — and he chose to meet Allah." **Abu Bakr** immediately began to cry because he understood that the Prophet ﷺ was talking about himself.

## The Last Dawn

On **Monday morning**, Abu Bakr was leading the Fajr (dawn) prayer in the mosque. Suddenly, the Prophet ﷺ drew aside the curtain of Aisha's room that faced the mosque.

The companions saw his face — **bright and radiant** like a page of the Quran. They were overjoyed! They thought the Prophet ﷺ had recovered.

The Prophet ﷺ smiled at the sight of the neat rows of worshippers, then gestured for them to continue their prayer. He lowered the curtain again.

That was the **very last time** the companions saw the face of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.

## The Final Moments

The Prophet ﷺ was resting against Aisha's chest. Aisha's brother entered carrying a **siwak** (a wooden toothbrush). The Prophet ﷺ looked at it, and Aisha knew he wanted it. She softened the siwak for him, then gave it to him. The Prophet ﷺ cleaned his teeth very thoroughly.

Then, the Prophet ﷺ raised his hand upward and said:

**"O Allah, forgive me, have mercy on me, and join me with al-Rafiq al-A'la (the Highest Companion)."**

He repeated those words three times. Then his hand fell, and he **returned to Allah**. Aisha said: "He passed away between my chin and my chest."

It was Monday, **12 Rabi al-Awwal, 11 AH** — or June 8, 632 CE. The Prophet ﷺ was 63 years old.

## Abu Bakr and the Words That Changed Everything

When the news of the Prophet's death spread, the companions were devastated. **Umar ibn al-Khattab** — a man known for his great strength — could not accept reality. He stood up and declared that the Prophet ﷺ could not possibly have died and would surely return.

Then **Abu Bakr** arrived. He entered Aisha's room, kissed the Prophet's forehead, and said: *"You are good in life and good in death."*

Abu Bakr then came out to the mosque and spoke calmly but firmly:

**"Whoever among you worshipped Muhammad — then Muhammad has died. And whoever worshipped Allah — then Allah is Ever-Living and shall never die."**

Then he recited a verse from the Quran: **"Muhammad is not but a messenger. Other messengers have passed before him."** (QS Ali Imran: 144)

Hearing this, everyone wept. Umar collapsed to the ground — his legs could no longer hold him. He said: "It was as though I heard this verse for the first time."

Abu Bakr's words taught everyone that **Islam is not about one person** — no matter how great — but about Allah and His eternal teachings.

## Lessons from the Saddest Day

The death of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ teaches us many important things:

💫 **All creatures return to Allah** — Even the Prophet ﷺ, the most beloved of Allah, also died. Nothing in this world is eternal except Allah.

📖 **The Quran and Sunnah are the most beautiful legacy** — The Prophet ﷺ left behind two things that will guide the community forever: the Quran and the example of his life.

💪 **Be strong even when sad** — Abu Bakr was deeply grieved, but he was still able to lead the community. He wept, then rose and took action.

❤️ **Love the Prophet ﷺ by following his teachings** — The best way to love the Prophet ﷺ is not just to mourn his departure, but to practice what he taught.
