# Construction of Al-Aqsa Mosque - Baitul Maqdis (~705 CE) - Children's Content

## 🏛 Fun Fact

Al-Aqsa Mosque was built in a very special place - Baitul Maqdis (Jerusalem). This is a holy city for three major religions: Islam, Christianity, and Judaism. Caliph al-Walid ibn Abd al-Malik ordered the construction of this magnificent mosque around 705 CE.

The name "al-Aqsa" means "the farthest" in Arabic. This mosque was called so because it was located farthest from Masjidilharam in Mecca at that time, yet remained spiritually connected through the Night Journey (*Isra'*) of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.

## 🔧 Construction Process

Caliph al-Walid gathered the best architects and builders from throughout the caliphate territories. There were experts from Syria (*Sham*), Egypt, and Persia working together to build this mosque. The finest materials were brought from various regions: marble from Anatolia, cedar wood from Lebanon, and gold ornaments.

Construction was carried out in three phases. First, strengthening the foundation and building the main structure. Second, installing tall marble columns and building the dome. Third, completing ornaments with beautiful Arabic calligraphy and geometric motifs typical of Islam.

The architects had to be clever in overcoming challenges because the location was above ancient foundations. They had to integrate the new building with the unique shape of the Noble Sanctuary, so the mosque could stand strong and beautiful.

## 🌍 Impact and Uniqueness

Al-Aqsa Mosque has a unique design, combining Byzantine, Persian architectural styles, and Islamic innovations. This mosque has seven *saff* (rows) for congregational prayer with a *mihrab* facing the Ka'bah. Its acoustic system was designed very carefully so the imam's voice could be heard clearly throughout the room.

The tall marble columns not only strongly support the building, but also create visual beauty. The wooden ceiling with intricate carvings shows the high level of Islamic art during the Umayyad period. This mosque became both a spiritual center and a learning center for Muslims.

The construction of Al-Aqsa Mosque also demonstrated religious tolerance. Caliph al-Walid built a magnificent mosque while still respecting the holy places of other religions in Jerusalem. This became an example of how Islam values diversity.

## 📖 Spiritual Meaning

Al-Aqsa Mosque is very special because it is mentioned in the Quran as the destination of Prophet Muhammad's ﷺ Night Journey. Allah SWT says:

> سُبْحَانَ الَّذِي أَسْرَىٰ بِعَبْدِهِ لَيْلًا مِّنَ الْمَسْجِدِ الْحَرَامِ إِلَى الْمَسْجِدِ الْأَقْصَى الَّذِي بَارَكْنَا حَوْلَهُ ﴿١﴾
>
> *"Exalted is He who took His Servant by night from al-Masjid al-Haram to al-Masjid al-Aqsa, whose surroundings We have blessed."* ﴾1﴿

The construction of this mosque teaches the importance of preserving the spiritual heritage of Muslims. Caliph al-Walid showed how visionary leaders can create monumental works that inspire future generations. Al-Aqsa Mosque became proof of Islam's commitment to glory, tolerance, and heritage continuity.
