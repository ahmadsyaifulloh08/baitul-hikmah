# Wa'ad al-Mau'udah — Islam Saved the Girls

## ⚔️ The Age of Jahiliyyah 🏜️

Before Islam came, the Arab people lived in a time called **Jahiliyyah** — the Age of Ignorance. During this period, many Arab tribes did not know the teachings of monotheism. They worshipped idols and had many harmful customs.

One of the worst customs was how they treated baby girls. Girls were considered weak and a source of shame for the family.

## ⚔️ The Fate of Baby Girls ☹️

In some Arab tribes, when a father heard the news of a baby girl being born, his face would turn dark with sadness. He felt ashamed in front of his people. Some fathers chose to get rid of their baby daughters — a terrible practice called **Wa'ad al-Mau'udah**.

Not all tribes did this, but the practice was widespread across the Arabian Peninsula. Girls were treated unjustly simply because of their gender.

## ⚔️ A Verse Describing That Time 📜

Allah described how the people of Jahiliyyah reacted when they received news of a baby girl:

> *"And when one of them is given the news of (the birth of) a female, his face becomes dark, and he is filled with grief. He hides himself from the people because of the bad news he has received. Should he keep her in humiliation or bury her in the ground?"*
> — **QS. An-Nahl (16): 58-59**

This was the terrible mindset of the Age of Ignorance towards girls.

## 📖 Islam Brought Change ☀️

Then Allah sent Prophet Muhammad ﷺ with the message of Islam. Islam firmly prohibited this cruel practice. Allah declared that on the Day of Judgement, the baby girls who were victims would be asked:

<ayat>وَإِذَا الْمَوْءُودَةُ سُئِلَتْ ۝ بِأَيِّ ذَنبٍ قُتِلَتْ</ayat>

> *"And when the girl who was buried alive is asked — for what sin was she killed?"*
> — **QS. At-Takwir (81): 8-9**

This verse shows that the baby girl was **completely innocent**. Allah will hold accountable those who wronged them.

## 📖 Prophet Muhammad ﷺ Honoured Daughters 💝

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ set a personal example. He deeply loved his daughters, especially **Fatimah Az-Zahra**. Whenever Fatimah came to visit, the Prophet ﷺ would stand up to greet her, kiss her forehead, and offer her his seat.

The Prophet ﷺ also said:

> *"Whoever raises two daughters until they reach maturity, he will come on the Day of Judgement with me like this."* — **HR. Muslim (Sahih Muslim)**

He said this while holding his two fingers together, showing how close that person would be to the Prophet ﷺ in Paradise.

## 📖 Honoured Women in Islam 🌹

Islam completely transformed the status of women. Here are some examples:

**Khadijah bint Khuwailid** — the Prophet's first wife — was a successful businesswoman and the first person to believe in the Prophet ﷺ.

**Aisha bint Abu Bakr** became one of the greatest scholars in Islamic history. The companions often consulted her on matters of religious law.

**Fatimah Az-Zahra** was given the title *Sayyidatu Nisa'il 'Alamin* — Leader of the Women of Paradise.

Islam gave women the right to inherit, the right to education, and full dignity.

## 📖 Women's Rights in Islam ⚖️

Islam established rights for women that had never existed before in the Arabian Peninsula:

- **Right to inheritance** — women were entitled to a share of family wealth
- **Right to choose a husband** — no one could force a woman to marry
- **Right to seek knowledge** — the Prophet ﷺ said: "Seeking knowledge is an obligation upon every Muslim" (men and women)
- **Right to own property** — a wife's wealth belongs to her alone

Islam granted all of these rights **1,400 years ago**, long before many other nations gave similar rights to women.

## 🌟 Lesson: Every Child is Precious 💎

The main lesson from this story: **every child is a gift from Allah**, whether boy or girl. No one should be looked down upon because of their gender.

Islam teaches that a person's value is determined by their **taqwa (piety)**, not by whether they are male or female. Allah says that the most honoured in His sight are those with the most taqwa.

Having daughters is a **great blessing** and a path to Paradise for parents who raise them well.

## 🌟 Lesson: The Courage to Stand Against Wrong 💪

Islam did not just change one custom — it transformed an entire civilisation. The courage to oppose wrong traditions is part of true faith.

When seeing something unjust, a Muslim must have the courage to speak up. The Prophet ﷺ said that changing wrongdoing can be done with the hand, the tongue, or the heart — and the last is the weakest level of faith.

Great change begins with the courage to say: **"This is wrong, and it must change."**
