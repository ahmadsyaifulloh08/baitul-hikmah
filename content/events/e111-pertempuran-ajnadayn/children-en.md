# The Battle of Ajnadayn 🗡️🌍

---

## A New Mission: Toward the Land of al-Sham

After the entire Arabian Peninsula was reunited under Islam, Caliph **Abu Bakr** made a momentous decision: sending Muslim armies to the land of **al-Sham** — the region that today includes Palestine, Jordan, Syria, and Lebanon.

Why al-Sham? Because the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ himself had already sent forces there. And al-Sham was the land that Allah called **blessed** — it was home to **al-Masjid al-Aqsa**, the first direction of prayer for Muslims!

Abu Bakr dispatched **four armies** in four different directions. Among them was **Amr ibn al-As**, heading toward Palestine. But the enemy they faced was enormous: the **Eastern Roman Empire**, one of the most powerful kingdoms in the world!

## Khalid Crosses the Impossible Desert

When the Muslim commanders in al-Sham reported that the Roman army was enormous, Abu Bakr summoned his greatest general: **Khalid ibn al-Walid**! But Khalid was far away in **Iraq**, fighting on another front.

Khalid had to make a journey considered **impossible** — crossing the **Samawah Desert**, a barren wasteland with almost no water, in just **18 days**!

How did they survive without water? Khalid had a clever idea: he ordered some camels to drink as much water as possible, then slaughtered them during the journey and took water from their stomachs! This is how his army survived the crossing through the deadly desert.

When Khalid arrived in al-Sham, he was immediately appointed **supreme commander** of all Muslim forces!

## The Great Battle at Ajnadayn

The Muslim and Roman armies finally met in the valley of **Ajnadayn**, in the land of Palestine. This was the first great battle between the Muslims and the Roman Empire!

The Roman soldiers wore heavy iron armor, carried large shields, and vastly outnumbered the Muslims. But the Muslim forces were lighter and faster!

**Khalid ibn al-Walid** led the Muslim cavalry in devastating charges into the heart of the Roman lines. **Amr ibn al-As**, who knew the Palestinian terrain well, fought with great valor.

The battle raged fiercely throughout the day. And in the end... **the Muslims won!** The Roman army was shattered, their commanders killed or fled!

## Abu Bakr's Testament: War with Ethics

Before sending his armies, Abu Bakr gave them a beautiful and important message:

*"Do not kill women, children, or the elderly. Do not cut down fruit-bearing trees. Do not destroy buildings. Do not slaughter animals except for food."*

This testament shows that Islam teaches **ethics of war** — even on the battlefield, there are rules that must be upheld. Women, children, and the elderly must be protected. Trees and buildings must not be destroyed.

These principles were incredibly advanced — even more advanced than modern laws of war that were only created thousands of years later!

## Gateway to a New World

The victory at Ajnadayn opened the **gateway** to great conquests that followed:

- **Damascus** was conquered shortly after
- The legendary **Battle of Yarmuk** ensured all of al-Sham came under Muslim rule
- **Jerusalem** — the city of al-Masjid al-Aqsa — surrendered to **Umar ibn al-Khattab** without bloodshed!

It all began with one battle in a small valley called Ajnadayn. Who would have thought that a group of soldiers from the Arabian desert could defeat one of the most powerful empires in the world?

## Lessons from Ajnadayn

The Battle of Ajnadayn teaches us:

- **Courage in the face of great challenges** — the Muslim forces were not intimidated even though the enemy far outnumbered them
- **Intelligence and strategy** — Khalid crossed an impossible desert with a brilliant idea
- **Ethics matter** — even in war, there are rules to protect the vulnerable
- **Small steps open great paths** — one victory at Ajnadayn opened the door to the conquest of all al-Sham

Allah says about the land of al-Sham: **"...al-Masjid al-Aqsa, whose surroundings We have blessed."** (QS. Al-Isra: 1)
