# Abu Bakr — The First Caliph 🌟

---

## Who Was Abu Bakr?

**Abu Bakr as-Siddiq** was the closest companion of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. He was the first adult man to accept Islam. He was the one who accompanied the Prophet ﷺ during the migration to Madinah and hid with him in the Cave of Thawr. His title means **"The Supremely Truthful"** — because he always believed whatever the Prophet ﷺ said without any doubt.

After Prophet Muhammad ﷺ passed away, the Muslim community needed a leader. Who would take the Prophet's place? The companions gathered and discussed. In the end, they all agreed: **Abu Bakr** was the most worthy to lead — the first caliph of Islam.

In his very first speech as caliph, Abu Bakr said: *"I have been appointed as your leader, yet I am not the best among you. If I do well, support me. If I err, correct me."* How humble he was!

## The Army of Usamah — Keeping the Prophet's Promise

One of the first things Abu Bakr did was send out the **army of Usamah ibn Zayd**. The Prophet ﷺ had ordered this army to march before he passed away, but their departure had been delayed.

Many companions said: "Don't send the army yet! We're in danger!" But Abu Bakr said firmly: **"I will not untie a knot that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ has tied!"**

This meant: if the Prophet ﷺ had given a command, that command must be carried out — no matter how difficult things were. And this decision turned out to be exactly right! Usamah's army succeeded, and the Arab tribes knew that Madinah was still strong.

## The Riddah Wars — Defending the Truth

After the Prophet ﷺ passed away, many Arab tribes **apostatized** — they left Islam. Some returned to worshipping idols, some followed false prophets like **Musaylimah the Liar**, and some refused to pay **zakat** (charitable tax).

Abu Bakr was very firm. He said: **"If they withhold even the rope used to tie a camel that they used to give to the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, I will fight them for it!"**

The biggest battle was the **Battle of Yamamah** against Musaylimah the false prophet. The fighting was very fierce. In the end, the Muslim forces won and Musaylimah was defeated. In less than a year, the entire Arabian Peninsula was reunited under Islam.

## Compiling the Quran — Preserving Allah's Word

During the Battle of Yamamah, many **memorizers of the Quran** were killed as martyrs. Umar ibn al-Khattab was very worried: what if more and more memorizers died and parts of the Quran were lost?

Umar proposed to Abu Bakr: **"Let us collect the entire Quran into one book!"**

Abu Bakr was hesitant at first — because the Prophet ﷺ had never done this. But after careful thought, he agreed. He assigned **Zayd ibn Thabit** — the Prophet's scribe — to collect every verse of the Quran from written records and the memorization of the companions.

This was an **incredibly important** decision! Thanks to this project, the Quran we read today is still exactly the same as the Quran revealed to Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. SubhanAllah!

## Abu Bakr's Death — A Simple Leader

Abu Bakr served as caliph for only **two years and three months**. Short, but filled with extraordinary achievements!

Before he died, he appointed **Umar ibn al-Khattab** as his successor. And he requested to be wrapped in his **old, worn-out clothes** — not new cloth. He said: *"The living need new cloth more than the dead."*

Abu Bakr passed away in 634 CE at the age of **63** — the same age as Prophet Muhammad ﷺ when he died. He was buried beside the Messenger of Allah ﷺ.

**Lessons from Abu Bakr:**

⭐ **Humility** — even though he was the highest leader, he never considered himself the best

⚖️ **Firm in truth** — he never compromised when it came to matters of faith

📖 **Preserving the Quran** — thanks to his decision, the Quran has been protected to this day

🤲 **Simplicity** — he lived simply and never pursued luxury

Abu Bakr was an example of the perfect leader — firm yet humble, strong yet compassionate, and always holding on to the truth!
