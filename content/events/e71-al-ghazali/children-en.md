# Al-Ghazali and Ihya Ulum al-Din

## 🧑 Who Was He?

Abu Hamid Muhammad al-Ghazali was born in 1058 CE in the city of Tus, Khurasan (now Iran). His father was a simple wool spinner who loved knowledge very much. When his father died, al-Ghazali and his brother were raised by a Sufi who taught them religious knowledge.

From childhood, al-Ghazali showed extraordinary intelligence. He could memorize and understand lessons very quickly. His mother always prayed that her son would become a great scholar who would benefit the Muslim community.

Al-Ghazali studied in various places, from his hometown to Nishapur, a famous educational center of that time. He studied jurisprudence, hadith, theology, logic, and even philosophy. His intelligence amazed his teachers and they predicted a bright future for him.

## ⭐ What Did He Do?

At age 33, al-Ghazali had become a professor at the Nizamiyyah Madrasah in Baghdad, the most famous university of its time. Thousands of students came to learn from him. He was famous for being very skilled in debate and explaining religious knowledge in easy-to-understand ways.

However, at the peak of his success, al-Ghazali began to feel empty in his heart. Even though he was very smart, he felt that his knowledge could not make his heart peaceful and close to Allah. This condition made him sick and unable to teach anymore.

Al-Ghazali then left his prestigious position and began a spiritual journey. He went to Damascus, Jerusalem, and performed hajj to Mecca. For ten years, he lived simply, worshipped much, and reflected on the true meaning of life.

After finding peace of heart, al-Ghazali wrote a great book titled "Ihya Ulum al-Din" (Revival of Religious Sciences). This book consists of 40 volumes that discuss all aspects of Muslim life very comprehensively.

## 💡 Why Was He Extraordinary?

Al-Ghazali successfully bridged two worlds often considered contradictory: Islamic law knowledge and Sufi knowledge. Previously, scholars usually focused on only one. Al-Ghazali showed that both must be studied together.

"Ihya Ulum al-Din" became the most influential book in Islamic history after the Qur'an and Hadith. This book teaches how to become a perfect Muslim, not only smart in religious knowledge but also pure of heart and close to Allah.

What was special about his book was its very practical explanation method. Al-Ghazali not only told what should be done but also explained why it should be done and what its impact would be on the heart and soul.

Al-Ghazali's influence is very great until now. His book has been translated into various languages and studied worldwide. In Indonesia, "Ihya Ulum al-Din" has become a required book in many pesantren.

## 📖 Scripture

The Qur'an reminds that the purpose of sending Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was as mercy for all worlds:

> وَمَا أَرۡسَلۡنَٰكَ إِلَّا رَحۡمَةٗ لِّلۡعَٰلَمِينَ ﴿١٠٧﴾
>
> *"And We have not sent you except as a mercy to all the worlds. ﴾107﴿"*
> — QS. Al-Anbiya' (21): 107

Al-Ghazali wrote "Ihya Ulum al-Din" with the purpose of reviving the spirit of being a true Muslim, as taught by Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. He wanted Muslims to be not only smart in religious knowledge but also possess noble character and pure hearts.

Al-Ghazali's story teaches that true happiness does not come from intelligence or high position alone, but from closeness to Allah. Knowledge must be accompanied by good deeds and purification of the heart.
