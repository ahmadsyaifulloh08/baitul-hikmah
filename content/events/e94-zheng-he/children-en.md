# Admiral Zheng He & the Spread of Islam in the Archipelago

## ⛵ Great Sailor from China

In 1405 CE, a fleet of giant ships sailed from China to the Archipelago. This armada was led by a great admiral named Zheng He. What was special was that Zheng He was a Muslim who worked for the Chinese Emperor.

Zheng He's original name was Ma He. He came from a Muslim family in Yunnan. His father had once performed the pilgrimage to Mecca. Although working in the Chinese palace, Zheng He never forgot his religion. He continued to perform prayers and fasting like other Muslims.

## 🚢 Large Treasure Ships

Zheng He's ships were called "treasure ships." His main ship was very large, 150 meters long - almost twice the length of a soccer field! The fleet had 300 ships with 30,000 crew members.

Interestingly, many crew members were Muslim. Zheng He deliberately chose Muslim sailors because they already knew the trade routes at sea. These Muslim sailors could also communicate with Islamic traders at Archipelago ports.

🎨 *Illustration: Zheng He's large fleet sailing at sea, main ship very large in center with yellow sails, other ships around, white clouds in blue sky, calm sea, magnificent and peaceful atmosphere*

---

## 🕌 Building Mosques at Ports

Every time Zheng He's fleet stopped at a port, he always built temporary mosques. In Palembang, Semarang, and other cities in the Archipelago, Zheng He met with Muslim traders who already lived there.

Zheng He and his Muslim crew performed congregational prayers together with local Islamic people. They exchanged stories about Islam and trade. Many Muslim crew members eventually settled and married local residents.

🎨 *Illustration: Zheng He and sailors performing congregational prayers in a simple wooden mosque by the port, sunset in background, ships visible in distance, sarongs and caps, solemn and peaceful atmosphere*

---

## 🌟 Helping Spread Islam

Zheng He's arrival strengthened Islam in the Archipelago. He did not come as a preacher, but his presence made Islamic people in the Archipelago feel proud. They saw that a Muslim could become a great admiral in China.

Zheng He also helped Islamic sultanates like Malacca become stronger. He provided protection from China so these sultanates could develop and spread Islam to other places.

## 📖 Lessons of Brotherhood

> إِنَّمَا الْمُؤْمِنُونَ إِخْوَةٌ ﴿١٠﴾
>
> *"The believers are but brothers."* ﴾10﴿

Zheng He's story teaches us that Islamic brotherhood knows no boundaries of country or ethnicity. Zheng He from China was brothers with Islamic people in the Archipelago. They helped each other and strengthened one another.

Zheng He also showed that a Muslim can work well anywhere while maintaining his religion. He proved that Islam teaches tolerance and can live harmoniously with different cultures.
