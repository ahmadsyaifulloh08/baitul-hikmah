# The Great Mosque of Samarra and Malwiya Tower: Marvel of Abbasid Architecture

## 🏛 Fun fact

The Malwiya Tower in Samarra was the world's first mosque tower with a spiral shape! It stands 52 meters tall with a winding path that allows people to climb to the top without using stairs. Even donkeys could climb to the top because of its gentle and wide slope.

The Great Mosque of Samarra was once the largest mosque in the world at its time, covering 38,000 square meters. That's about the size of 5 football fields! This mosque could accommodate tens of thousands of worshippers at once for congregational prayers.

Samarra city itself was built from scratch by Caliph Al-Mu'tasim in 836 CE as the new capital of the Abbasid Caliphate. The name "Samarra" comes from Arabic "Surra man ra'a" which means "happy is he who sees it."

## 🔬 Explanation

Construction of the Great Mosque of Samarra began in 848 CE and was completed in 852 CE under the command of Caliph Al-Mutawakkil. The architects used very advanced construction techniques, combining fired brick with high-quality lime mortar.

The mosque walls were built 10-11 meters high and reinforced with supporting towers around the perimeter. The inner courtyard of the mosque was very spacious to allow thousands of people to pray congregationally comfortably.

Most amazing was the Malwiya Tower with its unique spiral design. This tower functioned not only as a place for the call to prayer, but also as a watchtower for city security and a place to observe the stars.

Samarra artists developed a new decorative technique called "luster" for ceramics, which produced a shiny metallic effect on tiles. They also created a beveled carving style on plaster that gave beautiful shadow effects on walls.

## 🌍 Modern impact

The design of Malwiya Tower influenced mosque architecture throughout the Islamic world. Its unique spiral shape was later adopted by various buildings, including the Ibn Tulun Mosque in Cairo built in 879 CE.

Samarra's urban planning system became a model for the development of other Islamic cities. The concept of integrating palaces, commercial areas, and religious centers in one city complex was copied in various places.

Unfortunately, in 892 CE the capital was moved back to Baghdad and Samarra began to be abandoned. The Mongol attack in the 13th century CE caused severe damage, and in 2005 there was a terrorist attack that damaged part of the reconstructed dome.

Today, the Samarra Archaeological Site has been designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2007. International teams use 3D scanning and photogrammetry technology to document and preserve the remaining structures.

## 📖 Islam & Science

Islam teaches that Allah SWT is everywhere and we can face any direction to pray, although the qibla remains the special direction for prayer. The Quran states:

> وَلِلَّهِ الْمَشْرِقُ وَالْمَغْرِبُ ۚ فَأَيْنَمَا تُوَلُّوا فَثَمَّ وَجْهُ اللَّهِ ۚ إِنَّ اللَّهَ وَاسِعٌ عَلِيمٌ ﴿١١٥﴾
>
> *"And to Allah belongs the east and the west. So wherever you turn, there is the face of Allah. Indeed, Allah is all-Encompassing and Knowing." ﴾115﴿*
> — QS. Al-Baqarah (2): 115

The construction of Samarra Mosque shows how Muslims during the Abbasid period used cutting-edge knowledge and technology to build places of worship. They combined knowledge from various civilizations to create extraordinary architectural works.

The Samarra library stored manuscripts in various languages: Arabic, Persian, Greek, Syriac, and Sanskrit. This shows that Islam encouraged the pursuit of knowledge from any source, in accordance with the hadith of Prophet Muhammad SAW: "Seek knowledge even unto China."

The Malwiya Tower was also used for astronomical observation, which was an important part of Islamic science. Muslim scientists used star observations to determine prayer times, qibla direction, and the Islamic calendar.
