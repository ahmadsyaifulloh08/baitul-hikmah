## 🏛 Fun Fact

In the 10th century CE, Cordoba in Spain became the most advanced city in Europe! While other European cities were still dark at night, Cordoba's streets were brightly lit with lamps. The city had 700 mosques, 300 public baths, and 70 libraries.

Most amazing was Caliph **Al-Hakam II's** private library which contained 400,000 books! Imagine, at a time when books were very rare and expensive, he collected almost half a million books from various languages and fields of knowledge. He even sent special envoys to Baghdad and Cairo just to buy rare books.

## 🔬 Explanation

Cordoba became a meeting place for scholars from three religions: Islam, Christianity, and Judaism. They worked together translating ancient books from Greek, Latin, and Persian into Arabic, then into European languages. This was like Google Translate of ancient times, but done by experts!

**Abbas ibn Firnas**, a Cordoba scientist, conducted flying experiments with artificial wings in 875 CE—almost 600 years before Leonardo da Vinci! He also created the first crystal glass in Spain and designed advanced water clocks.

Cordoba scholars wrote about mathematics, astronomy, medicine, philosophy, and literature. Their works were later studied at European universities like Oxford and Paris, becoming the foundation for scientific advancement on the continent.

## 🌍 Modern Impact

Cordoba's tolerance model called "*convivencia*" (living together) became an inspiration for modern multicultural societies. Muslims, Christians, and Jews lived side by side, learning from each other, and contributing to mutual progress. This showed that religious differences are not obstacles to advancing knowledge.

The library system developed in Cordoba—with catalogs, classification, and borrowing rules—became a model for modern libraries. The tradition of scientific discussion and free research that began in Cordoba was later adopted by universities worldwide.

Works translated in Cordoba became an important bridge between ancient civilizations and the European Renaissance. Without Cordoba, the works of Aristotle, Plato, and other great scientists might have been lost from history.

## 📖 Islam & Knowledge

The Qur'an teaches the importance of dialogue and cooperation in goodness:

> يَٰٓأَهْلَ ٱلْكِتَٰبِ تَعَالَوْا۟ إِلَىٰ كَلِمَةٍ سَوَآءٍۢ بَيْنَنَا وَبَيْنَكُمْ ﴿٦٤﴾
>
> *"O People of the Book, come to a word that is equitable between us and you..." ﴾64﴿*
> — QS. Ali Imran (3): 64

The caliphs of Cordoba embodied this teaching by creating an environment where scholars from various religious backgrounds could work together. They showed that seeking knowledge is a universal task that transcends religious and ethnic boundaries.

The massive investment in libraries and education in Cordoba showed that in Islam, seeking knowledge is not only an individual obligation but also a state responsibility. Caliphs not only led political affairs, but also became the main patrons for the advancement of knowledge and civilization.
