## 🏛 Did you know?

In 634 CE, Caliph Umar ibn al-Khattab expanded the Prophet's Mosque to twice its original size! This mosque was not just for prayer, but also became the world's largest learning center.

In this expanded mosque, there were special zones for learning the Quran, hadith, fiqh, and Arabic language. Each zone was led by a companion of the Prophet who was expert in that field.

Thousands of people from various countries came to Madinah to study at the Prophet's Mosque. They learned directly from companions who had lived with Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.

## 🔬 What is it?

The expanded Prophet's Mosque became like a great university. There were four main zones: Quran zone led by Zaid ibn Thabit, hadith zone led by Abu Hurairah, fiqh zone led by Ibn Abbas, and Arabic language zone.

Each zone had a small library with Quran manuscripts and important records. Students sat in circles around their teachers in halaqah (study circles).

What was special was that this mosque also had a special room for storing the state treasury (baitul mal) and meeting rooms for discussing state affairs. All important state functions were centered in one place.

## 🌍 Its impact until now!

The Prophet's Mosque model was copied throughout the Islamic world. Great mosques in Baghdad, Cairo, and Cordoba were built with the same pattern: prayer areas and learning areas.

The learning zone system by subject started by Umar became the precursor to the faculty system in modern universities. Faculty of Sharia, Faculty of Language, and others follow this pattern.

Until now, large mosques in Indonesia also apply similar concepts. They are not just places of prayer, but also places for religious study, schools, and social activities.

## 📖 Islam & Knowledge

Allah says in the Quran: "And say: 'My Lord, increase me in knowledge.'" This verse was posted above the entrance of each learning zone in the Prophet's Mosque.

Caliph Umar said: "This mosque was not built for prayer only, but to be a beacon of knowledge for all worlds." This vision made Madinah the world's learning center.

Islam teaches that seeking knowledge is an obligation for every Muslim. Mosques are the right place because they combine spirituality and learning in one blessed place.
