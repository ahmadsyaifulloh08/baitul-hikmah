# Masjid Nabawi 🕌

---

## The Camel That Chose the Spot

When Prophet Muhammad ﷺ arrived in Madinah, many people wanted him to stay near their homes. Everyone was competing to invite him! But the Prophet said: "Let my camel walk freely. Wherever she stops, that is our place."

His camel, **al-Qaswa'**, walked past many houses, then stopped and knelt on **an empty piece of land** owned by two orphan boys. The Prophet wanted to buy the land — he refused to take it for free even though it was for building a mosque. In the end, **Abu Bakr** paid for it.

That turned out to be the spot where **Masjid Nabawi** was built — the Prophet's Mosque that still stands in Madinah to this day! 🕌

## Building the Mosque Together

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ didn't just give orders — he **joined in the work**! He carried clay bricks, hauled stones, and helped build the walls alongside his companions.

The first mosque was very simple: its walls were made of **clay bricks**, its pillars were **date palm trunks**, and its roof was **palm fronds**. The floor was just bare earth! But this mosque became the most important place in Madinah — not just for prayers, but also for **learning, discussions, and helping people in need**.

The companions sang joyfully as they worked: *"O Allah, there is no life but the life of the hereafter, so forgive the Ansar and the Muhajirin!"* And the Prophet sang right along with them! 🎶

## New Brothers!

The **muhajirin** — Muslims from Makkah — arrived in Madinah with nothing at all. They had left their homes and possessions behind for the sake of Islam. Prophet Muhammad ﷺ had a brilliant idea: he **paired** every Muhajir with an Ansari (a Muslim from Madinah) as brothers!

The most touching story is about **Abd al-Rahman ibn Awf** and **Sa'd ibn al-Rabi'**. Sa'd said: "My brother, take half of my wealth!" But Abd al-Rahman replied humbly: **"Thank you, just show me where your market is."**

Abd al-Rahman went to the market, started trading, and in no time he became a **successful merchant**! He didn't want to burden his brother — he wanted to be independent through his own hard work. 💪

## A Mosque for Everyone

At the back of the mosque was a covered veranda called the ***Suffah***. This was home to companions who had nowhere else to live. They studied the Quran directly from Prophet Muhammad ﷺ every single day! One of the most famous residents of the Suffah was **Abu Hurayrah**, who later memorised and narrated thousands of the Prophet's hadiths.

Masjid Nabawi was not just a place for prayers — it was a **school, a meeting hall, and a home for those in need**. That is why the Prophet built the mosque as his very first step in Madinah, before building anything else!

**An important lesson:** Prophet Muhammad ﷺ taught us that **a true leader works alongside** their people, not just giving orders. And true brotherhood means **helping each other** — but also **striving to be self-reliant**. Just like Abd al-Rahman, who didn't want to burden his brother but worked hard to build his own future! 🌟
