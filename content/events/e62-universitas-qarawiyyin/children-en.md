## 🏛 Fun Fact

In 859 CE, in the city of Fez, Morocco, a woman named **Fatimah Al-Fihri** built something extraordinary. She used her entire inheritance to build a mosque and learning center now known as Al-Qarawiyyin University. This university is the oldest continuously operating university in the world—older than Oxford and Cambridge!

Amazingly, Fatimah fasted every day during the mosque's construction. She only broke her fast after performing the first prayer in the newly completed mosque. What great sacrifice and faith she showed for the advancement of knowledge.

## 🔬 Explanation

Al-Qarawiyyin was not just an ordinary mosque. This place developed into a learning center that taught various sciences. Students studied the Qur'an, Hadith, and religious sciences, but also mathematics, astronomy, medicine, and philosophy.

The educational system at Al-Qarawiyyin became a model for European universities. They created degree systems, structured curricula, and teaching methods that were later adopted by Oxford, Cambridge, and the Sorbonne. Great scholars like Ibn Rushd and Al-Idrisi once studied there.

Al-Qarawiyyin's library houses more than 4,000 ancient manuscripts, including works by Ibn Sina, Al-Ghazali, and Ibn Khaldun. These books became priceless treasures of knowledge.

## 🌍 Modern Impact

Al-Qarawiyyin University is still operating today after more than 1,160 years! UNESCO and Guinness World Records recognize it as the world's oldest continuously functioning university. This proves how strong the foundation built by Fatimah Al-Fihri was.

Al-Qarawiyyin's holistic educational model—combining religious and secular sciences—became an inspiration for modern educational systems. The concept that knowledge is not divided, but rather a unity in seeking truth, is very relevant for today's education.

Fatimah's story also inspires many women to contribute to education and science. She proved that women have an important role in advancing civilization.

## 📖 Islam & Knowledge

The Qur'an teaches the importance of seeking knowledge. Allah says:

> وَقُل رَّبِّ زِدْنِي عِلْمًا ﴿١١٤﴾
>
> *"And say: 'My Lord, increase me in knowledge.'" ﴾114﴿*
> — QS. Thaha (20): 114

Fatimah Al-Fihri embodied this teaching by dedicating her entire life to building a place for seeking knowledge. She showed that waqf—giving wealth for the good of the ummah—is worship whose rewards continue to flow as long as it benefits others.

Fatimah's decision to build the university was not merely social charity, but a long-term investment for Islamic civilization. Thousands of scholars who studied at Al-Qarawiyyin later spread knowledge throughout the world, continuing the relay of goodness begun by a righteous woman.
