# The Founding of Al-Qarawiyyin University

## Historical Context

In the 9th century CE, the Islamic world experienced an extraordinary period of intellectual flourishing. Under the rule of the Abbasid Dynasty in Baghdad and the Umayyad Dynasty in Andalusia, centers of learning emerged throughout various Islamic territories. However, at the western edge of the African continent, in the city of Fez—now part of Morocco—an educational revolution was waiting to be born.^1

The Idrisid Dynasty, which had ruled Morocco since 788 CE, had transformed Fez into a rapidly developing capital.^2 The city became an important stopover for merchants, scholars, and pilgrims from across North Africa. In the midst of this urban prosperity, prominent families began to consider the importance of establishing permanent educational institutions.

## The Main Event: Birth of the World's Oldest University

In 859 CE, a righteous woman named **Fatimah bint Muhammad Al-Fihri** made a historic decision that would change the face of Islamic and world education.^3 Fatimah, who had inherited considerable wealth from her father Muhammad Al-Fihri—a wealthy merchant from Kairouan—decided to use her entire inheritance to build a mosque and educational institution.

Together with her twin sister Maryam, who also built the Al-Andalus Mosque on the other side of Fez, Fatimah began construction of what would become known as the Al-Qarawiyyin Mosque and University.^4 The name "Qarawiyyin" derives from "Qairouan," the Al-Fihri family's city of origin in Tunisia.

### A Construction Full of Blessings

The construction process of Al-Qarawiyyin became proof of Fatimah's extraordinary faith and dedication. According to classical historians' records, Fatimah fasted every day during the mosque's construction until its completion, breaking her fast only after performing the first prayer in the mosque.^5 The initial construction was completed in 860 CE, but the development of its educational institution continued for centuries.

### Transformation from Mosque to University

Initially, Al-Qarawiyyin functioned as a mosque for worship while serving as a place for informal religious instruction. However, over time, educational activities there became increasingly structured. Prominent scholars and religious teachers began teaching regularly, and a curriculum system began to take shape.^6 By the 10th century, Al-Qarawiyyin had developed into a university with various faculties and comprehensive study programs.

## A Holistic Islamic Educational Vision

Al-Qarawiyyin became a pioneer of Islamic educational models that combined religious sciences with secular knowledge. Students studied the Qur'an, Hadith, Islamic jurisprudence, and theology, while also learning mathematics, astronomy, medicine, and philosophy.^7 This holistic educational model later became an inspiration for other Islamic universities throughout the world.

## An Eternal Legacy^8

Al-Qarawiyyin not only became the oldest continuously operating university to this day, but also became a center for the dissemination of knowledge throughout the Islamic world. Many of the world's great scholars once studied there, including **Ibn Rushd (Averroes)**, **Al-Idrisi** the renowned geographer, and **Maimonides** the prominent Jewish philosopher.^9

Al-Qarawiyyin's library houses more than 4,000 ancient manuscripts, including rare works by Ibn Sina, Al-Ghazali, and Ibn Khaldun.^10 To this day, the university continues to operate and is recognized by UNESCO and Guinness World Records as the world's oldest continuously functioning university.

### Inspiration for World Universities

Al-Qarawiyyin's educational model became an inspiration for the establishment of universities in Europe. The degree system, structured curriculum, and teaching methods developed at Al-Qarawiyyin were later adopted by universities such as Oxford, Cambridge, and the Sorbonne.^11

## Wisdom and Lessons

The story of Al-Qarawiyyin's founding teaches us several valuable lessons:

**The Role of Women in Civilizational Progress**: Fatimah Al-Fihri proved that women play an important role in advancing Islamic civilization. Her dedication to building educational institutions demonstrated a far-sighted vision of the importance of knowledge.

**Waqf as an Economic Force in Islam**: Fatimah's decision to endow all her wealth for education demonstrated the power of the waqf system in building sustainable social infrastructure.

**Integration of Religious and Secular Sciences**: Al-Qarawiyyin became proof that Islam does not separate religious sciences from secular knowledge. Both are viewed as a unity in seeking Allah's pleasure.
