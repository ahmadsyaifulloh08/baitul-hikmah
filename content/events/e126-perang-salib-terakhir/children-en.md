# The Eighth & Ninth Crusades — The Final Expeditions

## 🌍 What Happened?

In 1270 CE, King Louis IX of France wanted to conduct a Crusade for the second time. He had failed 20 years earlier in Egypt, and now wanted to redeem his failure. However, this time he chose a strange strategy: attacking Tunis in North Africa first, not directly to the Holy Land.

Meanwhile, Sultan Baibars had already controlled almost all territories once held by Crusaders. Only a few coastal cities remained like Acre, Tyre, and Tripoli. Support from Europe for Crusades was also beginning to decline because kings were more focused on their domestic problems.

## 📖 The Story of the Eighth Crusade

Louis IX landed at Carthage near Tunis with 10,000 troops in July 1270 CE. He hoped to convert Emir al-Mustansir to Christianity and make Tunis a base for attacking the Holy Land. However, fate had other plans.

The Crusaders faced not armed resistance, but a far more dangerous enemy: plagues of dysentery and typhus. Hot African weather and poor sanitation made diseases spread rapidly. Within weeks, thousands of soldiers lay sick.

Most tragically, King Louis IX himself fell ill and died on August 25, 1270 CE, just one month after landing. The death of the king dubbed "Saint Louis" marked the end of the Eighth Crusade even before real fighting began.

## 🌟 The Ninth Crusade and Prince Edward

After Louis IX died, Prince Edward of England (future Edward I) decided to continue the mission by bringing 1,000 troops to Acre in May 1271 CE. Edward was only 32 years old and wanted to prove himself as a brave Christian leader.

Edward conducted several small attacks against Mamluk outposts around Nazareth and Qaqun. However, these attacks were more symbolic because he knew his forces were too small to seriously threaten Sultan Baibars.

Most dramatic was the assassination attempt against Edward by an assassin in June 1272 CE. Edward survived but was seriously wounded. Allegedly Edward's wife, Eleanor of Castile, sucked poison from her husband's wound to save him - this story later became a romantic legend in Europe.

## 🤲 Prayer and Lessons

Finally Edward realized his limitations and negotiated with Sultan Baibars. They reached a truce for 10 years, 10 months, 10 days, and 10 hours in September 1272 CE. This strange formula reflected medieval diplomatic traditions.

The Eighth and Ninth Crusades failed for several reasons: Europe no longer prioritized the Holy Land, Mamluk power was strengthening, and poor logistical preparation. Most importantly, religious spirit had been replaced by political and economic interests.
