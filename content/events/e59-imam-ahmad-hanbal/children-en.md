## 🧑 Who Was He?

Ahmad ibn Muhammad ibn Hanbal was born in 780 CE in Baghdad. His father died when he was still a baby, so his mother sold jewelry to finance his education.

From childhood, Ahmad loved the hadith of Prophet ﷺ very much. At age 16, he began serious studies and traveled to various cities to collect hadith from great teachers.

Ahmad met Imam al-Shafi'i in Baghdad and became his loyal student. Al-Shafi'i often praised Ahmad by saying: "Ahmad is more knowledgeable in hadith than us."

## ⭐ What Did He Do?

Ahmad collected more than 750,000 hadith from various teachers, then selected 28,000 of the best hadith to compile in "Musnad Ahmad" - one of the largest hadith collections in Islamic history.

He created the Hanbali school that strongly emphasized the Quran and hadith as primary legal sources. His principle: "No personal opinion when there is evidence."

Ahmad experienced severe torture for refusing to acknowledge that the Quran was a created thing. He was whipped for years but remained steadfast in his position.

## 💡 Why Was He Extraordinary?

Ahmad showed that commitment to truth must be maintained despite facing pressure and suffering. His steadfastness in defending the purity of creed became an example for subsequent generations.

Ahmad's methodology in hadith was very strict and careful. He not only collected but also conducted selection with very high criteria to ensure hadith authenticity.

The Hanbali school founded by Ahmad later inspired many Islamic purification movements in the future, including Muhammad ibn Abd al-Wahhab's movement in Saudi Arabia.

## 📖 Evidence

Allah SWT said about people who are steadfast in truth:

> مِنَ ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ رِجَالٌ صَدَقُوا۟ مَا عَٰهَدُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ عَلَيْهِ ۖ فَمِنْهُم مَّن قَضَىٰ نَحْبَهُۥ وَمِنْهُم مَّن يَنتَظِرُ ۖ وَمَا بَدَّلُوا۟ تَبْدِيلًا ﴿٢٣﴾

> *"Among the believers are men who have been true to their covenant with Allah. Among them are some who have fulfilled their vow, and some are still waiting, but they have never changed in the least."* ﴾23﴿
> — QS. Al-Ahzab (33): 23

Ahmad ibn Hanbal was an example of someone who kept his promise to Allah to defend the truth even though he had to suffer.

Let us pray: "O Allah, grant us steadfastness like Imam Ahmad to always hold fast to the Quran and Sunnah in facing trials."
