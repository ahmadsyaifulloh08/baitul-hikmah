# Tariq ibn Ziyad and the Conquest of Al-Andalus ⚔️

---

## A Berber Commander from Africa

**Tariq ibn Ziyad** was a Muslim commander from the **Berber** tribe in North Africa. He was not originally Arab — he was a Berber who embraced Islam and became one of the greatest commanders in history!

Tariq served as governor of the city of **Tangier** in Morocco, right across the sea from Spain. At that time, Spain was ruled by the **Visigothic** kingdom, which was in turmoil as its kings fought over power. Its people — especially the Jewish population and farmers — lived under oppression.

## Crossing the Strait of Gibraltar

In April 711 CE, Tariq led about **7,000 soldiers** across the narrow strait between Africa and Europe. They landed at the foot of a great rocky hill.

That rocky hill was later named **Jabal Tariq** — meaning "Mount Tariq." Do you know what? That name is still used today! In English, Jabal Tariq became **Gibraltar**. So every time you hear the word "Gibraltar," remember this Muslim commander from Africa! 🗻

Before the great battle, Tariq gave a famous speech to his troops: *"The sea is behind you and the enemy before you. You have nothing but honesty and patience."*

## The Great Battle of Guadalete

The Visigothic king named **Roderic** assembled a large army to face Tariq. A great battle took place near the **Guadalete** River in July 711 CE.

Although Roderic's forces were far larger, they were divided by internal disputes — some nobles actually sided with Tariq! Tariq's united and spirited forces won the battle. King Roderic was killed, and the Visigothic kingdom collapsed.

After that, the conquest moved incredibly fast. Tariq captured **Toledo** — the Visigothic capital — and other major cities. His superior, **Musa ibn Nusayr**, arrived the following year with reinforcements. Within three years, nearly all of Spain became part of the Muslim world!

## Al-Andalus — A Brilliant Civilization

This conquest was not the end of the story — it was actually the **beginning** of something extraordinary! For nearly **800 years**, this land — called **Al-Andalus** — became one of the most advanced centers of civilization in the world!

In the city of **Cordoba**, there was a library with hundreds of thousands of books — far more than all the other libraries in Europe combined! Muslims, Christians, and Jews lived side by side, learned together, and built civilization together.

Lessons from this story:
- **Courage changes history** — Tariq and his 7,000 soldiers dared to cross the sea to an unknown land
- **Unity is key** — Tariq's united army defeated a larger but divided force
- **An enduring name** — Gibraltar reminds the world of a Muslim commander from Africa
- **Civilization, not just conquest** — What matters more than winning wars is building a brilliant civilization
