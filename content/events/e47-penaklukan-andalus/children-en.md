# Conquest of Al-Andalus 🌉

---

## ⚔️ Why Did This Happen?

In the Iberian Peninsula (present-day Spain and Portugal) stood the **Visigoth Kingdom** — a Christian kingdom suffering a major crisis: kings fighting for the throne, nobles in conflict, and Jewish people and farmers being oppressed.

Across the Strait of Gibraltar, **Musa ibn Nusayr** had completed the conquest of North Africa. One of his best commanders was **Tariq ibn Ziyad** — a brilliant Berber who became governor of Tangier.

An opportunity opened when parties within the Visigoth Kingdom asked for help due to internal conflicts. **Julian** (Ilyan), governor of Ceuta who had disputes with King Roderic, even offered ship assistance for the crossing.

Muslims had missionary spirit and wanted to liberate oppressed people. The Quran says: *"If you support Allah's religion, Allah will support you."*

## 🛡 How Did It Happen?

Before the main crossing, **Tarif ibn Malik** was sent with 400 men for reconnaissance in 710 CE. They successfully landed at a point later named **Tarifa** — the first city in Europe with a Muslim name!

In April 711 CE, **Tariq ibn Ziyad** led 7,000 troops — mostly newly converted Berbers — across the strait using ships provided by Julian. They landed at the foot of a large rocky hill named **Jabal Tariq** (Mount Tariq) — which in European languages became **Gibraltar**.

King **Roderic** gathered a large army to face Tariq. A major battle occurred at **Guadalete River** in July 711 CE. Though Visigoth forces were more numerous, they were divided by internal conflicts. Even some Visigoth nobles defected to Tariq's side!

Roderic died in battle, and the Visigoth Kingdom collapsed spectacularly. After victory, Tariq moved quickly to conquer **Toledo** — the Visigoth capital — and other major cities.

## 🕊 What Happened Next...

In 712 CE, **Musa ibn Nusayr** followed with 18,000 additional troops, conquering **Seville**, **Merida**, and other cities not yet taken by Tariq.

Amazingly, within **three years** almost the entire Iberian Peninsula was under Islamic rule! Many cities surrendered without resistance because people saw Muslims bringing better justice.

Thus was born **Al-Andalus** — one of the most brilliant civilizations in human history! For nearly 8 centuries (711-1492 CE), Al-Andalus became a beacon of science, arts, and interfaith tolerance.

Cities like **Cordoba**, **Granada**, and **Toledo** became world centers of learning when much of Europe was still in darkness. Cordoba's library had hundreds of thousands of manuscripts — more than all Christian European libraries combined!

**Gibraltar** — the name from *"Jabal Tariq"* — survives today as an eternal reminder of that historic crossing.

## 💪 Values We Can Learn

**Thorough preparation before action** — Tariq didn't just cross randomly. Tarif's expedition proved good planning and careful reconnaissance.

**Courage to seize opportunity** — Seeing opportunity from Visigoth internal crisis, Tariq dared take big steps that changed history.

**Unity in diversity** — Tariq's army consisted of Arabs and Berbers working together. Al-Andalus later became an example of multicultural harmony.

**Fast but not hasty** — After winning at Guadalete, Tariq moved quickly but measuredly. Didn't give enemies time to reorganize.

**Bringing justice, not oppression** — Many cities surrendered because Muslims offered fairer alternatives. Oppressed Jewish people gained religious freedom.

**Leaving eternal legacy** — Al-Andalus lasted 800 years and left civilizational heritage whose influence is still felt today.
