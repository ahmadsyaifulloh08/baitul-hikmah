## 🌍 What Happened?

In 750 CE, a great revolution occurred that changed Islamic history. The Umayyad Dynasty, which had ruled for nearly 100 years, came to an end and was replaced by the Abbasid Dynasty.

This revolution began in the region of Khurasan (eastern Iran) by a leader named Abu Muslim. He successfully gathered many people who were dissatisfied with Umayyad rule, especially because they were treated unfairly.

The final battle took place at the Zab River in Iraq, where Abbasid forces defeated the last Umayyad khalifah, Marwan II. This victory marked the beginning of a new era in Islamic history.

## 📖 The Story

After the victory, the first Abbasid khalifah named As-Saffah came to power. However, it was the second khalifah, Al-Mansur, who had grand plans to build an amazing new capital.

Al-Mansur chose a location on the banks of the Tigris River, near the ruins of the ancient city of Ctesiphon. This place was strategic because it was at the crossroads of trade routes between Asia and Europe.

In 762 CE, the construction of Baghdad began. The city was designed as a perfect circle with the khalifah's palace and grand mosque at its center. The city wall was 30 meters high with four large gates facing the four cardinal directions.

## 🌟 The Lessons

The Abbasid Revolution teaches the importance of justice in government. The Umayyad Dynasty collapsed because it did not treat all Muslims fairly, especially those who were not of Arab descent.

The Abbasids succeeded because they promised equality for all Muslims, regardless of their origins. They chose officials based on ability, not just on lineage.

Baghdad became proof that with good planning and long-term vision, a city could become the center of world civilization. The city later became a gathering place for the finest scholars, scientists, and artists.
