# Muhammad the Problem Solver

## 🏛️ The Ka'bah Is Damaged and a Roman Ship Is Wrecked

In the heart of the city of Mecca stood a very special building — called the Ka'bah, which was first built by Prophet Ibrahim and his son Prophet Ismail thousands of years ago as a house for worshipping Allah. The Ka'bah at that time was still simple — its walls were made of mountain stones without mortar, it had no roof, and was only four meters high. But one day a massive flood struck the Ka'bah walls until they cracked and nearly collapsed! At the same time, a Roman merchant ship was wrecked at Jeddah port carrying high-quality wood that was very rare in the desert, and a Roman carpenter named Baqum came to help repair the Ka'bah.

## 🔨 Al-Walid Dares to Demolish and Everyone Works Together

No one dared to start dismantling the Ka'bah because they were afraid of calamity, until finally a brave leader named al-Walid took an axe and began demolishing the damaged wall while praying: "O Allah, we only want good!" All the people of Mecca watched from a distance with worry, but the next day al-Walid was healthy and fresh. So all the clans worked together to build the Ka'bah higher and stronger than before, using wood from the Roman ship for the new roof, with Baqum leading the installation of beams and the entrance door that was raised high above the ground.

🎨 *Illustration: Al-Walid holding an axe, about to strike the Ka'bah wall. Quraysh people watching from distance with worried faces. Baqum the Roman carpenter directing workers carrying wooden beams. Dominant colors brown stone, golden sand, white and brown robes. Mood tense but determined. Composition al-Walid in foreground, crowd in background.*

## ⚔️ The Black Stone Dispute and Abu Umayyah's Proposal

The building work went smoothly until it was time to put back the Black Stone — the special black stone in the corner of the Ka'bah that was considered the highest honor to install. Arguments between clans grew heated and some had even dipped their hands in bowls of blood as a sign they were ready for war! The dispute lasted four days, until finally a wise elder named Abu Umayyah stood up raising his hand and proposed: "The first person to enter through the gate of the Sacred Mosque tomorrow morning will be the judge!" All clans agreed and waited tensely.

## 💡 Muhammad al-Amin and the Brilliant Cloth Solution

The next morning the first person to step in was Muhammad — a thirty-five-year-old man known as al-Amin, the most trusted person in all of Mecca! Everyone cheered joyfully and accepted him as judge. Muhammad asked for a large cloth, placed the Black Stone in the middle of it, and told each clan to send one representative to hold an edge of the cloth and lift it together. When the cloth was at the height of the Ka'bah wall, Muhammad took the Black Stone with his own hands and placed it in the southeast corner.

## 🕋 The Ka'bah Stands Majestically and Unity Is Preserved

Everyone clapped and cheered takbir with joy — the great dispute that almost became civil war was resolved without a single drop of blood spilled! The Ka'bah now stood more magnificent than before — taller, stronger, with a new wooden roof and the Black Stone installed back in its corner. All clans felt satisfied because everyone participated. This event showed that Muhammad had extraordinary wisdom long before becoming a prophet — he did not take sides, respected everyone, and found solutions that made all parties feel honored.
