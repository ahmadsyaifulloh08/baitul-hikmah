# Muhammad the Problem Solver

## 🏛️ The Damaged Ka'bah

Friends, in the heart of the city of Mecca stood a very special building — called the **Ka'bah**. This cube-shaped structure was first built by **Prophet Ibrahim (Abraham)** and his son **Prophet Ismail (Ishmael)** thousands of years ago as a house for worshipping Allah.

But one day, an incredibly heavy rain fell upon Mecca. Floodwaters rushed down from the surrounding hills and poured into the city. The massive flood struck the aging walls of the Ka'bah until they cracked and nearly collapsed!

The Quraysh — the people of Mecca — were very worried. "Our Ka'bah must be repaired!" they said. But the problem was, nobody dared to start tearing it down! They were afraid something terrible might happen if they touched the sacred building.

Finally, a leader named al-Walid took an axe and began dismantling the damaged wall while praying: "O Allah, we only intend to repair it!" Everyone waited through the night — would al-Walid be alright? He was! So they all joined together to rebuild the Ka'bah.

## ⚔️ The Big Argument

The building work went smoothly until it was time to put back the **Black Stone** — a special stone sitting in the corner of the Ka'bah. And here is where the big trouble began!

Every large family (clan) among the Quraysh wanted to be the one to place the Black Stone. They felt it was the greatest honor possible. "We have the right!" said one clan. "No, we have more right!" said another.

The argument grew hotter and hotter. Some clans were even ready to go to war! They dipped their hands in bowls of blood as a sign they were willing to fight. Imagine, friends — they nearly battled each other just over who would place a stone!

The quarrel went on for four days with no solution. Everyone was confused. Finally, an elder named Abu Umayyah had an idea: "Let's do this — the first person to walk through the gate of the Sacred Mosque tomorrow morning will be the judge for all of us!"

Everyone agreed. They waited nervously...

## 💡 A Brilliant Idea

The next morning, everyone gathered around the Sacred Mosque. Who would walk in first? They were dying to know.

And guess who was the first to step through the gate? **Muhammad**! A thirty-five-year-old man whom all of Mecca knew as *al-Amin* — the most trustworthy person.

"It's *al-Amin*! It's *al-Amin*!" the people cheered happily. "We are all willing to accept him as our judge!"

Muhammad listened to their problem calmly. Then he had a very clever idea! He asked for a large cloth and placed the Black Stone in the middle of it. Then he said: "Each clan, send one person to hold an edge of this cloth. Then lift it together!"

The leaders of each clan held an edge of the cloth and lifted the Black Stone together. When it had been raised to the height of the Ka'bah wall, Muhammad took the stone with his own hands and placed it in its spot.

Everyone cheered! No one felt cheated. Everyone had shared in lifting the stone together. A huge problem was solved without anyone getting hurt!

## 🌟 Important Lessons

Friends, from this story we can learn so many things!

**First**, complicated problems can be solved with a clear mind. Muhammad didn't panic, didn't get angry, and didn't take sides. He thought calmly and found a solution that made everyone happy.

**Second**, being honest and trustworthy is incredibly important. Muhammad was trusted by all of Mecca not because he was rich or powerful, but because he had always been honest throughout his life. The title *al-Amin* was earned through everyday actions, not given by anyone special.

**Third**, teamwork is better than fighting. When all the clans held the cloth together, the problem was solved. But when they were competing against each other, the problem only grew bigger.

Later, Muhammad would become the Prophet and Messenger of Allah who taught justice and peace to all of humanity. And the story of the Black Stone shows that these noble qualities were already within him long before the first revelation came down!
