# Sultanate of Demak - First Islamic Kingdom in Java

## 🏛️ First Islamic Kingdom

In 1475 CE, the first Islamic kingdom in Java called the Sultanate of Demak was formed. This kingdom was founded by Raden Fatah, a smart and wise prince. Demak was located on the northern coast of Java, a very strategic place for sea trade.

Before Demak was established, Java was ruled by the Majapahit Kingdom which was Hindu-Buddhist. When Majapahit began to weaken, Raden Fatah saw an opportunity to establish an Islamic kingdom. He did not attack Majapahit with violence, but built power gradually through trade and preaching.

## ⭐ Wise Leader

Raden Fatah was a very special figure. His father was the King of Majapahit, but his mother was a Chinese princess who was Muslim. Because of this background, he understood both Javanese culture and Islam well.

Raden Fatah learned religious knowledge from Wali Songo, especially Sunan Ampel. He also studied how to lead and wage war. The combination of religious knowledge, politics, and culture made him the perfect king to lead the transition from Hindu-Buddhist to Islamic kingdom.

🎨 *Illustration: Raden Fatah studying the Quran with Sunan Ampel in a pendopo, books open before them, solemn and blessed atmosphere, soft light from oil lamp, dominant brown wood colors*

## 💡 Combining Islam and Javanese Culture

What was great about the Sultanate of Demak was how they combined Islam with Javanese culture. The Grand Mosque of Demak was built in distinctive Javanese architectural style. Its roof was multi-tiered like traditional Javanese buildings, but the qibla direction faced Mecca.

Demak's government system was also unique. The Sultan was not only a worldly king, but also a religious leader. Islamic law was combined with good Javanese customs. Royal ceremonies still used Javanese traditions, but with Islamic prayers.

🎨 *Illustration: Grand Mosque of Demak with distinctive Javanese tumpang roof, spacious courtyard with people performing Friday prayers, traditional wooden architecture with Islamic ornaments, solemn and peaceful atmosphere*

## 🌟 Spreading Islam Throughout Java

Demak became the center for spreading Islam in Java. Wali Songo received full support from the sultan to preach throughout Java. Demak also established relationships with other Islamic kingdoms like Malacca and Aceh.

Sultan Trenggana, the second king of Demak, successfully defeated the remaining forces of Majapahit. With this victory, Java officially became a territory ruled by Islamic kingdom. Many Javanese people happily embraced Islam.

> *"And among His signs is that He created for you mates from among yourselves, that you may find tranquility in them, and He placed between you affection and mercy."* ﴾21﴿

The Sultanate of Demak teaches us that Islam and local culture can coexist harmoniously. Like the verse above that speaks of peace and compassion, Demak created peace between Islamic values and Javanese traditions.

Demak's legacy can still be felt today. The architecture of Javanese mosques, the Islamic boarding school system, and the gentle way of practicing Islam are the results of the hard work of Demak's founders. They proved that Islam could become a truly Indonesian religion.
