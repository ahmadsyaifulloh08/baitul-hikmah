# The First Crusade and Fall of Jerusalem

## 🌍 What Happened?

In 1095 CE, Pope Urban II from Europe gave a speech that changed history. He called upon all Christians in Europe to wage war against Muslims and recapture the city of Jerusalem which was considered holy land.

Thousands of people from various European countries joined this expedition. They were called "Crusaders" because they wore cross symbols on their clothes. Their motivations were different: some wanted adventure, to seek new lands, or escape from poverty in Europe.

What made this situation dangerous for Muslims was the divided condition of the Islamic world. Various Muslim rulers were fighting against each other, so there was no unity to face threats from outside.

## 📖 The Story

The Crusaders began their long journey from Europe to the Middle East in 1096 CE. They were led by nobles like Godfrey of Bouillon and Raymond of Toulouse. This journey was very difficult because they had to cross thousands of kilometers of dangerous terrain.

The first city to fall to the Crusaders was Antioch after a siege lasting almost one year. During the siege, both besiegers and besieged experienced starvation and disease. Finally the city fell due to betrayal by a guard who opened the gate for the enemy.

In June 1099 CE, the Crusaders finally arrived before Jerusalem's walls. This city was defended by Fatimid dynasty troops who were not many in number. The siege lasted for five weeks under very difficult conditions for both sides.

On July 15, 1099 CE, the Crusaders successfully breached the defenses and captured Jerusalem. Unfortunately, the city's fall was followed by massive massacres of Muslim and Jewish inhabitants who were not involved in the war.

## 🌟 The Lesson

The tragedy of the First Crusade teaches how dangerous division among Muslims can be. When Muslim rulers were busy fighting each other, they became weak facing enemies from outside. Unity is the key to strength.

This event also shows the importance of maintaining peace and protecting civilian populations in any conflict. The Qur'an teaches that war must have limits and should not exceed humanitarian boundaries.

The fall of Jerusalem became a valuable lesson for the Muslim generations that followed. They learned that to defend their homeland, Muslims must unite, be strong, and help each other.

Although this was a tragedy, in the long term this event also awakened the spirit of Muslim resistance. Great figures like Saladin al-Ayyubi would emerge later to recapture Jerusalem.

## 🤲 Prayer & Scripture

The Qur'an teaches limits in warfare:

> وَقَٰتِلُوا۟ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ ٱلَّذِينَ يُقَٰتِلُونَكُمۡ وَلَا تَعۡتَدُوٓا۟ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يُحِبُّ ٱلۡمُعۡتَدِينَ ﴿١٩٠﴾
>
> *"And fight in the way of Allah those who fight you but do not transgress. Indeed, Allah does not like transgressors. ﴾190﴿"*
> — QS. Al-Baqarah (2): 190

This verse reminds that even in war, there are rules that must be respected. It is forbidden to harm civilian populations not involved in war, including women, children, and elderly people.

The Crusade tragedy teaches us to always maintain unity of the community, respect humanity, and strive to resolve conflicts in peaceful ways. Allah loves people who are just and do not exceed limits.
