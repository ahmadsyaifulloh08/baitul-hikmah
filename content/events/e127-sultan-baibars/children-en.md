# Sultan Baibars — Expulsion of the Crusaders

## 🧑 Who Was He?

Baibars al-Bunduqdari was the Mamluk Sultan who became leader after Sultan Qutuz died in 1260 CE. He was called the "Lion of Egypt" because of his bravery in battle. Baibars was not only a great warrior, but also a skilled strategist and clever diplomat.

He had sharp blue eyes and always studied his enemies carefully before attacking. Baibars had a spy network spread even into enemy fortresses. He never attacked without thorough preparation.

## ⭐ What Did He Do?

Baibars led many battles to expel the Crusaders from Muslim lands. The biggest battle was when he captured the city of Antioch in 1268 CE. Before attacking, he sent a letter to the Crusader prince: "You will see your soldiers torn by swords and your city destroyed!"

Most amazing was when Baibars captured Krak des Chevaliers, the strongest Crusader fortress that could never be conquered for 160 years. He used clever tactics: cutting food supply lines to the fortress and creating fake letters ordering the fortress's surrender. After 36 days, the fortress surrendered!

Baibars also fought the Mongols and won at the Battle of Elbistan. He proved that the victory at Ain Jalut was not luck, but because of superior Muslim strategic strength.

## 💡 Why Was He Extraordinary?

Baibars was extraordinary because he successfully expelled the Crusaders from almost the entire Levant (Syria-Palestine) region which they had controlled for nearly 200 years. He relied not only on military strength, but also clever diplomacy by building friendships with Byzantine emperors and European kings.

What made him special was the "lightning war" system he created: sudden attack, destroy target, then quickly retreat before the enemy could ask for help. He also built an express postal system that could send messages from Cairo to Damascus in just 4 days - very fast for that time!

Baibars proved that with thorough preparation, clever strategy, and strong leadership, Muslims could defeat enemies that appeared stronger. He saved the Holy Land from occupation and opened the way for the total liberation of Jerusalem.

## 📖 Verses

Allah SWT says:

> إِنَّ مَعَ الْعُسْرِ يُسْرًا ﴿٦﴾
>
> *"Indeed, with hardship comes ease."* ﴾6﴿
> — QS. Al-Insyirah (94): 6

Baibars faced many difficulties that seemed impossible: strong fortresses that had never fallen, enemies with advanced technology, and complicated political situations. But he never despaired and always looked for wise solutions.

The story of Baibars teaches us that Allah will provide ease for those who struggle earnestly in His path. We must always be prepared, learn from enemies, and never give up despite facing great challenges. With faith, strategy, and hard work, nothing is impossible.
