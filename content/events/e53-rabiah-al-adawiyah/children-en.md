## 🧑 Who Was She?

Rabi'ah al-Adawiyah was born around 717 CE in Basrah, Iraq. Her father, Isma'il, was a pious but poor man from the Adiyy tribe. When her father died, her family experienced severe poverty and young Rabi'ah was even sold into slavery.

Her childhood was full of suffering, but Rabi'ah never gave up hope. Her nights were spent in prayer and supplication to Allah. One night, her master saw a strange light surrounding Rabi'ah while she was praying, and amazed by this, he freed her.

After gaining freedom, Rabi'ah chose to live alone in the desert near Basrah. She built a simple hut and spent all her time in worship, dhikr, and contemplating Allah's greatness.

## ⭐ What Did She Do?

Rabi'ah developed the revolutionary concept of mahabbah ilahiyyah (divine love) in Sufism. Unlike people who worship out of fear of hell or desire for paradise, Rabi'ah taught pure love for Allah.

Her famous prayer: "O Allah, if I worship You from fear of Hell, burn me in it. If from hope of Paradise, exclude me from it. But if I worship You for Your own sake, do not withhold from me Your eternal beauty."

Various important figures, even the governor of Basrah, had proposed to her. However, Rabi'ah rejected all proposals. She said: "Marriage is for those who still have existence. I have been annihilated in Allah and live only through Him."

## 💡 Why Was She Extraordinary?

Rabi'ah opened a new dimension in Islamic spirituality with the concept of pure love for Allah. Previously, Sufism was dominated by fear and hope. Rabi'ah showed that the highest motivation in worship is sincere love.

She taught fana (annihilation in Allah) and baqa (subsistence in Allah). This concept does not mean physical union with Allah, but eliminating bad qualities and surrendering completely to Allah's will.

Despite being a woman in difficult times, Rabi'ah was respected by great scholars like Hasan al-Bashri. She even dared to correct Hasan's views that focused too much on law without considering deeper spiritual dimensions.

## 📖 Verse

Allah SWT says about love and closeness to Him:

> وَٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوٓا۟ أَشَدُّ حُبًّا لِّلَّهِ ﴿١٦٥﴾

> *"And those who believe are stronger in love for Allah."* ﴾165﴿
> — QS. Al-Baqarah (2): 165

Rabi'ah showed that love for Allah is the highest motivation in worship. Pure love produces sincere worship without ulterior motives, only because of Allah and for Allah.

Let us pray: "O Allah, grant us pure love like Rabi'ah, so that we worship You not from fear or hope, but solely because of You."
