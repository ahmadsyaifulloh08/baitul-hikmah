# The Treaty of Hudaybiyyah 🕊️

---

## Missing Makkah

For six years, the Muslims had been living in Madinah, far from Makkah — the city where they were born and where the Ka'bah stood. They missed it so much.

One night, **Prophet Muhammad ﷺ** had a dream of performing umrah at the Ka'bah. A prophet's dream is always true! So he invited about **1,400 companions** to travel to Makkah. They wore white *ihram* clothes and brought sacrificial animals — a sign that they came to worship, not to fight.

But the Quraysh were not happy. They sent horsemen to block the Muslims' path!

## Stopping at Hudaybiyyah

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ chose a winding path through rocky cliffs to avoid the Quraysh soldiers. At a place called **Hudaybiyyah**, the Prophet's camel named **al-Qaswa'** suddenly knelt down and refused to move!

Some companions said: "The camel is being stubborn!" But the Prophet smiled and said: *"Al-Qaswa' is not being stubborn. She has been stopped by the One who stopped the elephant army."*

It turned out that Allah wanted them to stop there! At Hudaybiyyah, the well was almost dry. The Prophet placed an arrow in the well — and amazingly, water gushed out! Everyone could drink to their fill.

Nearby, a small well with water beginning to flow. Dominant colors: rocky brown, white garments. Mood: wondrous. Composition: focus on the camel and Prophet at center, valley stretching in the background.

## The Pledge Under the Tree

The Prophet sent **Uthman ibn Affan** to Makkah to talk with the Quraysh. But Uthman didn't come back for a long time, and a rumor spread that he had been killed!

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ sat under a big tree and asked all his companions to make a pledge — a promise that they would not retreat no matter what happened. One by one, **1,400 companions** extended their hands and pledged. This was called the **Pledge of Ridwan**.

Allah was very pleased with their bravery. In the Quran, Allah says: **"Certainly was Allah pleased with the believers when they pledged allegiance to you under the tree."** (QS Al-Fath: 18)

Thankfully, the rumor was not true — Uthman was safe!

Dominant colors: green foliage, white garments, brown earth. Mood: solemn and brave. Composition: the large tree as the focal point, companions gathered around it.

## A Difficult Agreement

Finally, the Quraysh sent **Suhayl ibn Amr** to negotiate. The result was an agreement that made many companions sad:

- The Muslims had to **go home without performing umrah** that year
- They could come back **next year** and stay for three days
- A **ceasefire** for ten years

**Umar ibn al-Khattab** was very upset. He asked: "Why should we accept this?" The Prophet calmly answered: *"I am the Messenger of Allah, and He will surely help me."*

The saddest moment was when **Abu Jandal** — a Muslim being tortured in Makkah — came begging for help, but had to be returned according to the agreement. Many companions wept.

But it turned out... this treaty was a **great victory**! Allah declared: **"Indeed, We have granted you a clear victory."** (QS Al-Fath: 1)

## Lessons from Hudaybiyyah

Why did this seemingly unfair treaty turn out to be a victory?

🕊️ **Peace opened the door to dawah** — Without war, many Arabs could learn about Islam. In just two years, the number of Muslims multiplied!

🤝 **The Quraysh recognized the Muslims** — By sitting down to negotiate, the Quraysh acknowledged that the Muslims were a strong and legitimate group.

💪 **Patience is strength** — Prophet Muhammad ﷺ taught that patience and wisdom are stronger than anger. Sometimes stepping back one pace can take you forward ten!

📨 **Dawah to the whole world** — With peace secured, the Prophet could send letters to the great kings: Rome, Persia, Egypt, and Abyssinia!
