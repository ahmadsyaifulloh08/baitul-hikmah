# The Seventh Crusade - Louis IX Captured in Egypt

## 🌍 What happened?

King Louis IX of France, who was very pious and generous, decided to lead the 7th crusade after Jerusalem fell back to Muslim hands in 1244. He even sold crown jewels to finance this expedition!

Louis IX learned from previous crusade mistakes. He didn't want to share command with church cardinals, so he became the sole leader himself. His strategy was the same as the Fifth Crusade: attack Egypt first to cut Muslim power at its roots.

## 📖 The Story

In June 1249, Louis IX's fleet with 15,000 troops landed at Damietta. Strangely, the city fell easily because Muslim soldiers panicked and fled. Louis IX thought this was a good sign, but it was actually a trap!

Turan Shah, the new sultan, deliberately let Louis control Damietta so Crusader forces would enter deeper into Egypt and could be besieged. When Louis advanced to the city of Mansura, he faced very tough Mamluk soldiers.

The battle at Mansura became a disaster for Crusader forces. Robert, Louis IX's brother, led a reckless attack and got trapped in the city's narrow streets. Many elite Templar and Hospitaler knights died in this battle.

## 🌟 The Lessons

Even worse, Louis IX's forces were hit by disease outbreaks and famine because supply lines were cut. Many soldiers died not from war, but from sickness and hunger. This condition forced Louis IX to retreat to Damietta.

But the troop withdrawal became a total disaster. Mamluk forces attacked from all directions, and finally Louis IX was captured along with other French nobles in April 1250! A very pious king became a prisoner in a Muslim land.

From prison, Louis IX showed extraordinary dignity. He didn't beg, but negotiated as an honorable king. The Mamluk sultan respected him and treated him well. Finally Louis IX was freed after paying a ransom of 1 million gold coins plus returning Damietta.

## 🤲 Prayer & Guidance

Despite military failure, Louis IX actually gained extraordinary spiritual honor. The way he faced defeat and suffering in prison showed the noble character of a true king. The church even made him a saint (Saint Louis).

This story teaches that sometimes defeat can be a hidden blessing. Allah SWT says:

> وَعَسَىٰ أَن تَكْرَهُوا شَيْئًا وَهُوَ خَيْرٌ لَّكُمْ ۖ وَعَسَىٰ أَن تُحِبُّوا شَيْئًا وَهُوَ شَرٌّ لَّكُمْ ۗ وَاللَّهُ يَعْلَمُ وَأَنتُمْ لَا تَعْلَمُونَ ﴿٢١٦﴾
>
> *"But perhaps you hate a thing and it is good for you; and perhaps you love a thing and it is bad for you. And Allah knows, while you know not."* ﴾216﴿

Subhanallah, how someone faces trials actually shows their true character.
