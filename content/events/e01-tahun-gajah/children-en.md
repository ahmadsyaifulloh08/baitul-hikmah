# The Year of the Elephant 🐘

---

## Abraha and the Grand Cathedral

In Yemen, a king named **Abraha** built a huge and beautiful cathedral called **al-Qullays** in the city of Sana'a. He wanted the Arabs to stop visiting the **Ka'bah** in Makkah and come to his cathedral instead.

But his plan failed. The Arabs remained loyal to the Ka'bah — the sacred house built by Prophet Ibrahim and Prophet Isma'il. Abraha became furious and decided to destroy the Ka'bah.

## The Elephant Army Marches to Makkah

Abraha gathered a massive army with **war elephants**. The biggest elephant was named **Mahmud** — it was enormous, unlike anything the Arabs had seen before.

The army marched from Yemen to Makkah. Anyone who tried to stop them was defeated. Near Makkah, they seized 200 camels belonging to **Abd al-Muttalib**, the chief of the Quraysh tribe.

Abd al-Muttalib went to meet Abraha and only asked for his camels back. Abraha was surprised: "I've come to destroy the Ka'bah, and all you care about is camels?"

Abd al-Muttalib replied calmly: **"I am the owner of those camels. The Ka'bah has a Lord who will protect it."**

## The Elephant Refuses and the Birds Arrive

When the army was ordered to advance, the elephant Mahmud **refused to walk toward the Ka'bah**. Every time it was pointed toward Makkah, it knelt down. But it would walk in any other direction.

Then flocks of **ababil birds** came from the sky. Each bird carried three small stones — one in its beak and one in each claw. These stones destroyed Abraha's entire army.

Allah recorded this event in **Surah Al-Fil (105:1-5)** — about how the elephant army was destroyed by small birds carrying stones.

## The Birth of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

In the same year the elephant army was destroyed — the year **570 CE**, known as the **Year of the Elephant** — **Muhammad ibn Abdullah** was born in Makkah.

He was born on a **Monday**, on the **12th of Rabi' al-Awwal**. His father, Abdullah, had already passed away before his birth. Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was born as an **orphan**.

After birth, he was nursed by **Halimah al-Sa'diyyah** from the Banu Sa'd tribe in the desert. From the moment baby Muhammad came into her care, Halimah's life was filled with blessings.

## Why Is the Year of the Elephant Important?

The Year of the Elephant carries great significance:

- **The Ka'bah was protected by Allah** — not by human power, but directly by Allah
- **The birth of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ** — the final prophet who would change the world
- **It became a time marker** — all Arabs used "the Year of the Elephant" as their reference point
- **An important lesson** — even the mightiest army is powerless against the will of Allah
