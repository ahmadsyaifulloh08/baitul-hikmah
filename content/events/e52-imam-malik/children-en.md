## 🧑 Who Was He?

Malik ibn Anas was born in 711 CE in Madinah, a very special city because it was where Prophet Muhammad ﷺ lived. His family was from the respected Ashja' tribe. His grandfather, Malik al-Akbar, had met several companions of the Prophet.

Madinah at that time still preserved traces of Rasulullah's ﷺ life. The Prophet's Mosque became the center of learning, where great scholars gathered such as Sa'id ibn al-Musayyab and Nafi', the former slave of Abdullah ibn Umar.

His mother, Al-Aliyah bint Shurayk, encouraged Malik to learn adab (ethics) before delving into knowledge. She advised that adab is the foundation for a good scholar.

## ⭐ What Did He Do?

Malik became a great scholar who taught hadith and Islamic law at the Prophet's Mosque. He learned from Nafi', the former slave of Abdullah ibn Umar, who memorized many hadith. Malik also learned from al-Zuhri about how to collect and verify hadith.

His greatest achievement was compiling the book Al-Muwatta, the first book to systematically collect hadith and Islamic law. He spent 40 years compiling and revising it. From 10,000 hadith he collected, only 1,700 were included in Al-Muwatta.

Malik developed the concept of 'Amal Ahl al-Madinah, which are traditions practiced by Madinah society since the Prophet's time. According to him, practices that persisted in Madinah better reflected true sunnah.

## 💡 Why Was He Extraordinary?

Malik successfully created a learning system that honored hadith with the highest respect. Every time he taught hadith, he always performed ablution, wore his best clothes, and used perfume. When asked his reason, he said: "I honor the hadith of Rasulullah ﷺ."

When Khalifah al-Ma'mun forced scholars to acknowledge that the Quran was a creature, Malik firmly refused. He was beaten until his shoulder was dislocated and never healed. Despite the torture, he remained steadfast in his position.

The Maliki school he founded spread widely to North Africa and Islamic Spain. The concept of 'urf (local tradition) he developed allowed Islamic law to adapt to local cultures without losing its substance.

## 📖 Verse

Allah SWT says about the importance of preserving knowledge:

> إِنَّا نَحْنُ نَزَّلْنَا ٱلذِّكْرَ وَإِنَّا لَهُۥ لَحَٰفِظُونَ ﴿٩﴾

> *"Indeed, it is We who sent down the Reminder, and indeed, We will be its guardian."* ﴾9﴿
> — QS. Al-Hijr (15): 9

Malik showed that preserving the hadith and sunnah of the Prophet is as important as preserving the Quran. Through Al-Muwatta, he preserved the legacy of Rasulullah ﷺ very carefully and with full responsibility.

Let us pray: "O Allah, make us trustworthy guardians of Your Messenger's teachings, as Imam Malik did in compiling Al-Muwatta."
