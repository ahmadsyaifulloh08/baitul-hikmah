
# The Fall of Granada 🏰😢

## ⚔️ Why Did This Happen?

Did you know that **Spain** was once a Muslim land? For almost **800 years**, Muslims lived there and built an amazing civilization! They called it **Al-Andalus**.

In Al-Andalus, Muslims, Christians, and Jews **lived together** peacefully. The city of Cordoba was bigger and more advanced than London and Paris! It had libraries, hospitals, and even **street lights** — which no other European city had! 🌟

But over time, Christian kingdoms from the north started taking back Islamic cities one by one. Until finally, only **one Islamic kingdom** remained — **Granada**, with its beautiful palace called the **Alhambra**. 🏰

## 🛡️ How Did It Happen?

Sadly, the Muslims in Granada were **not united**. The king and his son were fighting each other! Their enemies took advantage of this division.

King Ferdinand and Queen Isabella attacked Granada for **10 years**! Finally, on **2 January 1492**, Granada's last king, **Boabdil**, handed over the keys to the city.

There's a famous story: when Boabdil looked back at Granada one last time, he cried. And his mother said: *"You weep for what you did not defend."* 😢

## 🕊️ What Happened After...

After Granada fell, sad things happened:

- 📚 Thousands of Arabic books were **burned** in the city square — books about medicine, astronomy, and literature!
- 🕌 Mosques were turned into churches
- Muslim people were **forced to change their religion** or leave Spain

But the legacy of Al-Andalus **cannot be erased**!
- The **Alhambra** palace still stands today — and millions of people come to admire it! 🏰
- Thousands of Spanish words come from Arabic
- The famous **flamenco** music has roots in Arab music!

## 💪 Values We Can Learn

1. **Division destroys** — Al-Andalus fell because Muslims weren't united. Never fight with your brothers and sisters!
2. **Knowledge lasts longer than kingdoms** — The kingdom of Granada is gone, but the knowledge they taught the world lives on
3. **Living together is beautiful** — Al-Andalus proved that people of different religions can live peacefully together

Allah says:

> *"O mankind! We have created you from a male and a female, and made you into nations and tribes, that you may know one another."*
> — Qur'an, Al-Hujurat: 13

Diversity is beautiful — and that's what Al-Andalus proved! 🌈
