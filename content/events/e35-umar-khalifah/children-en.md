# Umar al-Faruq — The Just Leader ⚖️

---

## From Opponent to Champion of Islam

Long ago, **Umar ibn al-Khattab** was one of the most feared men in Makkah. He was tall and strong, with a booming voice and heavy footsteps that made people move out of his way. He was fiercely opposed to Islam — he even once set out to harm the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.

But Allah had a different plan. One day, Umar heard verses of the Quran being recited by his own sister, Fatimah. His heart was moved, and he decided to embrace Islam. From that moment on, Umar became one of Islam's most loyal defenders.

Years later, after the Prophet ﷺ passed away and Abu Bakr led the Muslims, it was Umar's turn to lead. Abu Bakr chose Umar as the second caliph because he knew — Umar was the most just and the most resolute.

## The Leader Who Could Not Sleep

Umar had a special habit — he would often **walk through the streets of Madinah at night**, disguised as an ordinary person. He wanted to see for himself how his people were living. Was anyone going hungry? Was anyone sick without help?

One night, Umar found a mother boiling stones in a pot. Her children were crying from hunger, thinking she was cooking food. Umar's heart broke. He ran straight to the *bayt al-mal* (state treasury), lifted a sack of grain onto his own back, and carried it to the family's home.

His servant said: "O Commander of the Faithful, let me carry that!" Umar replied: **"Will you bear my sins on the Day of Judgment?"**

He once said: "If a mule were to stumble on the banks of the Euphrates River, I would fear that Allah might ask me — why did you not smooth the road for it?"

## A Vast Empire

Under Umar's leadership, the Muslim lands grew enormously! In just **ten years**, the Muslim armies conquered:

- **The Levant** (modern-day Syria, Jordan, Palestine, and Lebanon)
- **Iraq and Persia** (modern-day Iran)
- **Jerusalem** — the holy city that Umar personally traveled to receive
- **Egypt** — including the great city of Alexandria

Umar also created many things that had never existed before:
- **The Diwan** — a register of all Muslim citizens and their rights from the treasury
- **Bayt al-Mal** — a state treasury separate from the caliph's personal money
- **The Hijri Calendar** — the Islamic calendar we still use today!
- **New cities** — Kufa, Basra, and Fustat

Amazingly, despite ruling such a vast empire, Umar lived **very simply**. His robe was full of patches, he ate bread and olive oil, and he slept under a tree with no guards!

## The Death of the Caliph

One morning in the month of Dhul Hijjah, 23 AH (644 CE), while Umar was leading the Fajr prayer at the Prophet's Mosque, a man named **Abu Lu'lu'ah** — a Persian slave nursing a grudge — stabbed him.

Even though he was gravely wounded, Umar did not think about himself. What he thought about was: **who would lead the Muslims after him?** He appointed six of the best companions to consult and choose the next caliph.

Three days later, Umar ibn al-Khattab passed away. He was buried beside the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and Abu Bakr — the two people he loved most.

The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ once said about Umar: **"Were there to be a prophet after me, it would be Umar ibn al-Khattab."** (Tirmidhi)
