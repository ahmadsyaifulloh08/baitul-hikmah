# Bilal — The Brave One 💪

## 💔 A Slave Who Dared to Believe

Friends, this is the story of an incredibly brave man. His name was **Bilal ibn Rabbah**. Bilal came from Ethiopia, had dark skin, and in Mecca he lived as a slave. His master was **Umayyah ibn Khalaf** — a very cruel and wicked man.

In those times, slaves were not considered fully human. They could be sold, beaten, and had no rights at all. So sad, isn't it?

But when Bilal heard about Islam from **Abu Bakr**, his heart was moved. Islam taught that all human beings are equal — there is no difference between rich and poor, white and dark, master and slave. All are equal before Allah!

Bilal embraced Islam. He knew the risk was enormous — his master would be furious. But Bilal chose the truth.

## 🔥 The Hardest Test

When Umayyah found out that Bilal had become Muslim, he was furious! Every day, at noon when the desert sand was scorching hot, Umayyah ordered his men to lay Bilal on his back on the burning sand. Then they placed a **huge boulder** on his chest!

Imagine the pain! His back burning on hot sand, his chest crushed by a heavy rock, the sun beating down relentlessly.

Umayyah screamed: "Leave Islam! Worship our idols! Then I'll let you go!"

But do you know what Bilal said?

**"Ahad... Ahad..."**

It means: **"The One... The One..."**

Just that one word! In the midst of terrible pain, Bilal didn't cry for mercy, didn't give up, didn't deny Allah. He kept saying: "Ahad... Ahad..." — Allah is One, and no one can force me to worship anyone else!

Subhanallah, how strong was Bilal's faith!

## 🤝 Abu Bakr the Rescuer

News of Bilal's torture reached **Abu Bakr al-Siddiq** — the best friend of Prophet Muhammad SAW. Abu Bakr was a wealthy man with a generous heart. It pained him deeply to hear what Bilal was going through.

Abu Bakr went to Umayyah and said: "Do you not fear Allah? Release this man!"

Umayyah replied arrogantly: "You're the one who ruined him. If you want, buy him yourself!"

So Abu Bakr bought Bilal for a very high price — and then **set him free**! Bilal was no longer a slave. Bilal was a free man!

Abu Bakr didn't just free Bilal. He freed seven other slaves who were being tortured for accepting Islam. All with his own money. What a noble man Abu Bakr was!

## 🕌 The Most Beautiful Voice in Medina

After the Muslims migrated to Medina, Prophet Muhammad SAW built a mosque. But who would call the people to prayer?

The Prophet chose... **Bilal!**

Imagine! The man who was once a slave, tortured and humiliated, now standing at the highest point in the city of Medina, calling out the adhan heard by every person in town!

**"Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar..."**

Bilal's melodious voice echoed across all of Medina. Every morning, every noon, every evening — it was Bilal's voice that called Muslims to worship.

Prophet Muhammad SAW even told Bilal: "I heard the sound of your sandals walking ahead of me in Paradise!" This means Bilal held an incredibly high position in the sight of Allah.

## 🌟 Lessons for Us

**All people are equal.** Bilal was dark-skinned and a former slave — but in Allah's sight, he was nobler than many Quraysh nobles. What determines greatness is not skin color or wealth, but faith and a good heart.

**True faith cannot be broken.** Even under the cruelest torture, Bilal kept saying "Ahad." When we believe something is right, we should hold on to it even when it's hard.

**Use what you have for good.** Abu Bakr could have just prayed for Bilal. But he used his money to actually free him. If we have something that can help others, let's use it!

**Never look down on anyone.** The most despised person in Mecca became the most honored voice in Medina. Never judge people by their appearance!
