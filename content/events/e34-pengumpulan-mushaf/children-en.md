# The Compilation of the Quran 📜✨

---

## The Quran in the Prophet's Time

When the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was still alive, the Quran was kept safe in two ways. First, the companions **memorized it** in their hearts. Second, the scribes **wrote it down** on palm stalks, flat stones, camel bones, and pieces of leather!

— the Quran was not yet in the form of one neat book like the one we know today. Its verses were scattered in many places and in the memories of many people.

While the Prophet was alive, this was not a problem. If there was any question about a verse, people could simply ask the Prophet. But after the Prophet passed away, who would make sure everything was correct?

## The Battle of Yamamah and Umar's Concern

After the Battle of Yamamah, hundreds of **Quran memorizers** fell as martyrs. This deeply worried **Umar ibn al-Khattab**.

Umar thought: *"What if more and more Quran memorizers fall in future battles? What if parts of the Quran are lost?"*

Umar went to Caliph **Abu Bakr** and said: *"We must compile the Quran in one place!"*

Abu Bakr hesitated at first: *"How can we do something that the Messenger of Allah never did?"*

But Umar kept persuading him: *"By Allah, this is a good thing!"* Finally, Allah opened Abu Bakr's heart to accept this idea.

## Zayd ibn Thabit Is Chosen

Abu Bakr chose **Zayd ibn Thabit** for this enormous task. Zayd was still young — only about **22 years old** — but he was the most qualified person! Why?

- Zayd was the **chief scribe of revelation** — he most often wrote down Quran verses as the Prophet dictated them
- Zayd **was present when the Prophet recited the entire Quran** in his final Ramadan
- Zayd had **memorized the entire Quran** with great precision

When he received this assignment, Zayd said: *"By Allah, had they asked me to move a mountain, it would not have been heavier than compiling the Quran!"*

Zayd knew this was the **greatest trust** — to guard the Word of Allah so that not a single letter would be wrong!

## How Zayd Compiled the Quran

Zayd worked with incredible care. He did not rely on his own memory alone — even though he had memorized the entire Quran! Here is how he did it:

1. **Gathering the writings** — Zayd collected all Quran writings from the companions: those written on palm stalks, stones, bones, and leather
2. **Requiring two witnesses** — for every verse, two people had to confirm that the verse was genuinely written in front of the Prophet
3. **Cross-checking with memorization** — Zayd compared the writings with the memory of living memorizers

This way, the Quran was protected from errors. Every verse was verified multiple times!

There was one touching moment: Zayd found the last two verses of Surah al-Tawbah **only in written form** from one companion named **Abu Khuzaymah**. Although many had memorized them, only Abu Khuzaymah had the written record!

## The Quran Preserved Forever

Once complete, the compiled Quran sheets were kept by **Abu Bakr**. After Abu Bakr passed away, they were kept by **Umar**. And after Umar passed away, they were kept by **Hafsah bint Umar** — Umar's daughter and a wife of the Prophet ﷺ.

Allah had promised in the Quran: **"Indeed, it is We who sent down the Reminder, and indeed, We will be its guardian."** (QS. Al-Hijr: 9)

And Allah kept His promise! Through the hard work of Abu Bakr, Umar, and Zayd, the Quran has been preserved to this day — **more than 1,400 years** — without a single letter changed!

## Lessons from the Quran's Compilation

The story of the Quran's compilation teaches us:

- **The Quran is incredibly precious** — the companions worked extraordinarily hard to preserve it
- **Precision matters** — Zayd did not just collect randomly, but verified every verse multiple times
- **Youth is no barrier** — Zayd was only 22 but was trusted with the greatest task in history
- **Allah always keeps His promises** — He promised to guard the Quran, and He did so through His sincere servants
