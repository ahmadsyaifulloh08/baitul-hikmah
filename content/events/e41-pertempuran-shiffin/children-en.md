# Battle of Siffin ⚖️

---

## ⚔️ Why Did This Happen?

After Ali became caliph, **Muawiya ibn Abu Sufyan** — the powerful governor of Syria — refused to obey him. Muawiya had two demands: Ali must first punish Uthman's killers, and the caliphate election should go through broader consultation.

Ali had a different view: as the legitimate caliph, he must establish stability first before he could prosecute the killers. But without Muawiya's recognition, how could there be stability?

These two companions of Prophet ﷺ both had valid arguments. Ali was the caliph who received bay'ah in Medina, while Muawiya felt he had the right to demand justice for Uthman, who was his relative.

Months of negotiations failed. Finally, two great armies moved toward the plains of **Siffin** by the Euphrates River.

## 🛡 How Did It Happen?

The first thing that happened was the **water dispute**. Muawiya's army arrived first and prevented Ali's army from taking water from the Euphrates River. Ali sent an envoy saying: *"We didn't come to fight over water. Let us take water, then we can talk properly."*

When refused, Ali's army attacked and successfully seized access to the river. But Ali didn't seek revenge — he actually opened water access for **both sides**. This showed the nobility of his character.

They negotiated for months during the sacred month of **Muharram**. Many letters were exchanged between Ali and Muawiya. But still no agreement was reached.

Finally, fierce fighting occurred, most intense during ***Laylat al-Harir*** (Night of Clamor). Shouts and clashing swords were heard throughout the night.

When Ali's forces began to gain the upper hand, **Amr ibn al-Ash** (Muawiya's advisor) suggested a brilliant idea: Syrian soldiers raised **Quran manuscripts** on the tips of their spears while shouting: *"Allah's Book between us and you!"*

## 🕊 What Happened Next...

Seeing the Quran raised high, many of Ali's troops refused to continue fighting. They said: *"How can we fight against the Quran?"* Ali knew this was just a tactic, but pressure from his own forces was very strong.

Finally Ali agreed to **tahkim** (arbitration) — solving problems through judges, not swords. Both sides chose arbiters: **Abu Musa al-Ash'ari** for Ali and **Amr ibn al-Ash** for Muawiya.

But this arbitration failed. The results were not accepted by both sides, and the problem continued.

Worse yet, some of Ali's troops were angry that Ali accepted arbitration. They said: *"La hukma illa lillah!"* (No rule except Allah's). They left Ali's army and formed the **Khawarij** group.

So from one conflict, three groups emerged: Ali supporters, Muawiya supporters, and Khawarij. The ummah's division became worse.

## 💪 Values We Can Learn

**Noble character in conflict** — Ali didn't seek revenge over water. Though he could have retaliated for bad treatment, he instead shared fairly.

**Respecting the Quran** — When manuscripts were raised, Ali's forces were reluctant to fight. This showed how much they loved their holy book.

**Prioritizing peaceful ways** — Despite having military advantage, Ali was willing to try arbitration to avoid bloodshed among fellow Muslims.

**Life's complexity** — Not all problems have easy solutions. Sometimes good people can disagree because they see from different perspectives.

**Danger of division** — From one conflict, many new divisions can emerge. Fitnah is like fire — easy to spread and hard to extinguish.

**Respecting all companions** — Ali and Muawiya were both companions of Prophet ﷺ. They disagreed due to ijtihad (different opinions), not because of evil.
