# When Muslims Disagreed 🕊️

---

## Two Leaders with Different Views

After Caliph Uthman passed away, **Ali ibn Abi Talib** became the fourth caliph. Most Muslims supported him. But **Mu'awiyah ibn Abi Sufyan**, the governor of Syria, disagreed.

Mu'awiyah demanded that Caliph Uthman's killers be punished first. Ali believed that stability needed to come first before the killers could be brought to justice. Both had their own reasons, and both were companions of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.

They tried to negotiate many times, but could not find a solution. Eventually, the two armies met at a place called **Siffin**, on the banks of the Euphrates River.

## Raising the Quran

The fighting went on for several days. Both sides didn't really want to fight — they were all Muslims. Many soldiers prayed for the battle to end soon.

Then something happened that changed everything: Mu'awiyah's soldiers **raised copies of the Quran** on the tips of their spears and called out: **"The Book of God between us and you!"**

This meant: *"Let's stop fighting and solve this problem based on the Quran!"*

Most of Ali's soldiers agreed to stop fighting. Ali eventually accepted the call, and both sides agreed to each choose a representative to settle the matter peacefully.

## An Important Lesson

The peace talks, called *tahkim*, didn't go perfectly. The result didn't satisfy either side, and the disagreement continued.

Some of Ali's supporters who were upset even broke away and formed their own group called the **Khawarij**. From one disagreement, three separate groups emerged.

This was a very sad time for Muslims. But there are important lessons we can learn:

- 🤝 **Disagreements are human** — even the Prophet's companions could have different opinions
- 🕊️ **Peace is better than war** — both sides preferred talking over continued fighting
- 🤲 **Respect everyone** — scholars teach us to pray for the good of all the Prophet's companions

## Keeping Unity

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ once warned his followers about the dangers of division. He strongly emphasized the importance of unity among Muslims.

The story of the Battle of Siffin teaches us that **preventing disagreements is much easier than stopping them** once they start. Like a fire — it's easier to prevent it from starting than to put it out once it grows.

We also learn that when there are differences of opinion, the best way is to **sit together, speak kindly, and find solutions together** — not by arguing or blaming each other.

May we always be able to maintain unity and brotherhood among Muslims! 💚
