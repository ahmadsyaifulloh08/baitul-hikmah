# Ibn al-Haytham: The Father of Modern Optics

## 🧑 Who was he?

**Ibn al-Haytham** was born around 965 CE in the city of Basra, Iraq. His full name was Abu Ali al-Hassan ibn al-Haytham. Western people called him Alhazen. He was a very smart Muslim scientist who loved conducting experiments to prove his theories.

Ibn al-Haytham lived during the Abbasid Dynasty, famous for its advancement in science and knowledge. Since childhood, he showed great interest in mathematics, astronomy, and natural philosophy. He studied the works of Greek scholars like Euclid and Ptolemy that had been translated into Arabic.

## ⭐ What did he do?

Ibn al-Haytham wrote a very important book called "*Kitab al-Manazir*" (Book of Optics). In this book, he explained how our eyes see. Scientists before him believed that eyes emit light to see objects. But Ibn al-Haytham proved the opposite - it's the light from objects around us that enters our eyes.

He conducted many experiments using "camera obscura" - a dark room with a small hole in its wall. When sunlight entered through that hole, he could see an inverted shadow on the wall. This experiment proved that light travels in straight lines.

Ibn al-Haytham also studied eye anatomy by dissecting human and animal eyes. He described parts of the eye like cornea, iris, pupil, lens, and retina in great detail.

## 💡 Why was he extraordinary?

Ibn al-Haytham is considered the father of the modern scientific method. He was the first person to emphasize the importance of making observations, creating hypotheses, then testing them with experiments. This way of working was different from Greek scholars who relied more on philosophical thinking without experiments.

His work "*Kitab al-Manazir*" was translated into Latin and became the main reference for European scientists for centuries. Roger Bacon, famous as the "father of experimental method" in Europe, actually learned a lot from Ibn al-Haytham's work.

Besides optics, Ibn al-Haytham also contributed to astronomy and mathematics. He criticized Ptolemy's theory about the solar system and provided improvements based on his observations. He also solved very complex mathematical problems about light reflection on curved mirrors.

## 📖 Evidence

Ibn al-Haytham always emphasized that seeking knowledge is an obligation and truth must be sought honestly and objectively. The Quran teaches the importance of knowledge:

> وَيَرَى الَّذِينَ أُوتُوا الْعِلْمَ الَّذِي أُنزِلَ إِلَيْكَ مِن رَّبِّكَ هُوَ الْحَقَّ وَيَهْدِي إِلَىٰ صِرَاطِ الْعَزِيزِ الْحَمِيدِ ﴿٦﴾
>
> *"And those who have been given knowledge see that what is revealed to you from your Lord is the truth, and it guides to the path of the Exalted in Might, the Praiseworthy." ﴾6﴿*
> — QS. Saba (34): 6

Ibn al-Haytham once said: "Truth is sought for its own sake. Finding truth is a hard task, and the path to truth is rough." This statement shows that he was very committed to seeking scientific truth impartially.
