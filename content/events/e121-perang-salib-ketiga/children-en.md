# The Third Crusade - Richard vs Saladin

## 🌍 What happened?

In 1189 CE, three great kings from Europe decided to fight against Saladin al-Ayyubi. They were angry because Saladin had successfully recaptured Jerusalem from the Crusader forces two years earlier.

King Frederick from Germany, King Philip from France, and King Richard from England gathered with their huge armies. They wanted to retake Jerusalem from Saladin who had united the entire Islamic world.

## 📖 The story

Frederick chose the land route through Turkey, but drowned in a river before reaching the Holy Land. The German army became sad and many went home. Philip and Richard came by ship, but they didn't get along with each other.

They besieged the city of Acre for almost three years. After Acre fell, Philip became angry and returned to France. Richard was left alone to face Saladin. These two leaders respected each other despite fighting.

Richard succeeded in capturing several coastal cities, but Saladin used clever guerrilla tactics. Every time Richard advanced, Saladin attacked from the sides and rear.

## 🌟 The lesson

What's most interesting about this war is that Richard and Saladin respected each other. They even negotiated to find peaceful solutions. Richard once proposed a marriage between his family and Saladin's family.

Although Richard was very close to Jerusalem, he decided not to attack because he knew it would be difficult to defend. This shows that sometimes retreating is a wise decision.

Finally they made a treaty: Saladin continued to control Jerusalem, but Christians were allowed to make pilgrimages there. Richard controlled some coastal cities. Both sides got something they needed.

## 🤲 Prayer & Verses

This war taught that peace is better than endless fighting. Allah SWT loves those who choose peaceful paths when there's opportunity.

Islam teaches to always seek peaceful solutions if enemies also desire it. Saladin demonstrated this attitude by accepting negotiations even when he was winning.

> وَإِن جَنَحُوا لِلسَّلْمِ فَاجْنَحْ لَهَا وَتَوَكَّلْ عَلَى اللَّهِ ﴿٦١﴾
>
> *"And if they incline to peace, then incline to it and trust in Allah."* ﴾61﴿

Subhanallah, how noble were the character of leaders who prioritized the good of people over personal victory.
