## 🧑 Who Was He?

Abu Yusuf Ya'qub ibn Ishaq al-Kindi was born around 801 CE in Kufa, Iraq. His father was the governor of Kufa appointed by Harun al-Rashid. His family came from the respected Kindah tribe.

Al-Kindi moved to Baghdad as a teenager and joined the intellectual circle at Bayt al-Hikmah. He studied under great translators like Hunayn ibn Ishaq who introduced him to Greek philosophy.

Caliphs al-Ma'mun and al-Mu'tasim gave full support to al-Kindi. They provided him access to rare manuscripts and opportunities to collaborate with the best scholars of his time.

## ⭐ What Did He Do?

Al-Kindi became the first Islamic philosopher to systematically prove that Greek philosophy did not contradict Islamic teachings. He wrote "Fi al-Falsafah al-Ula" which showed that reason and revelation complement each other.

He developed logical arguments to prove the existence of Allah and His unity. Al-Kindi's cosmological argument became the foundation for the development of Islamic rational theology.

Al-Kindi was also expert in mathematics, medicine, music, and astronomy. He created mathematical systems to calculate drug doses and developed music theory based on mathematical principles.

## 💡 Why Was He Extraordinary?

Al-Kindi proved that Islam was not anti-science or philosophy. He showed that Muslims could use reason to understand the universe without diminishing their faith.

The methodology developed by al-Kindi combined empirical observation and rational thinking. He emphasized the importance of experiment and observation in acquiring knowledge about nature.

Al-Kindi's works were translated into Latin and became important references in European universities. He influenced the development of science and philosophy in Europe during the Middle Ages.

## 📖 Evidence

Allah SWT said about the importance of using reason to understand His creation:

> إِنَّ فِى خَلْقِ ٱلسَّمَٰوَٰتِ وَٱلْأَرْضِ وَٱخْتِلَٰفِ ٱلَّيْلِ وَٱلنَّهَارِ لَءَايَٰتٍ لِّأُو۟لِى ٱلْأَلْبَٰبِ ﴿١٩٠﴾

> *"Indeed, in the creation of the heavens and earth, and the alternation of night and day, there are signs for people of understanding."* ﴾190﴿
> — QS. Ali Imran (3): 190

Al-Kindi showed that studying philosophy and science is a way to understand the signs of Allah's greatness in the universe.

Let us pray: "O Allah, grant us clear minds like al-Kindi to understand Your signs in the universe and in the Quran."
