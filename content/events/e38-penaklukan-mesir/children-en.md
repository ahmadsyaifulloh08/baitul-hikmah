# The Conquest of Egypt 🏛️

---

## The Land of the Nile

**Egypt** was a legendary land — the land of pyramids, of the mighty Nile River, and of grand cities. One of its cities, **Alexandria**, was the biggest and most learned city in the world at that time!

But Egypt's native people — called the **Copts** — were suffering terribly. They were oppressed by the Byzantine (Eastern Roman) rulers who forced them to follow a particular version of Christianity. Their church leader, **Patriarch Benjamin**, even had to go into hiding for years to avoid being captured!

In Arabia, a companion of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ named **Amr ibn al-As** had once traveled to Egypt as a merchant. He knew that the people of Egypt were suffering and needed liberation.

## A Small but Brave Army

Amr asked Caliph **Umar ibn al-Khattab** for permission to conquer Egypt. Umar was hesitant at first — Egypt was enormous and guarded by a powerful Byzantine army. But eventually Umar agreed.

Here's the surprising part: Amr set out with only **4,000 soldiers**! A tiny number to conquer a country as big as Egypt. But Amr was a brilliant leader — he knew that the Egyptian people would welcome him as a liberator, not fear him as a conqueror.

The first battle took place at **Pelusium**, a border fortress. After that, Amr pushed on to **Heliopolis** and won a great victory. Caliph Umar then sent 4,000 more soldiers under **al-Zubayr ibn al-Awwam** — an incredibly brave warrior.

## The Fortress of Babylon and the Fall of Alexandria

The hardest battle was the **siege of the Fortress of Babylon** — a massive, powerful Roman fort. The siege lasted **seven months**!

Al-Zubayr ibn al-Awwam showed incredible bravery — according to the accounts, he **climbed the fortress walls with a ladder** and opened the gates from inside! With the Fortress of Babylon taken, the road to Alexandria was open.

**Alexandria** — Egypt's largest city and one of the greatest in the world — finally surrendered through a peace treaty. There was no massacre, no destruction. The people were given protection and freedom of worship.

And the happiest moment: **Patriarch Benjamin** could finally come out of hiding! After years of fleeing from Byzantine rulers, he could now lead his people safely. Amr personally guaranteed his protection.

## Fustat — The Tent City

After conquering Egypt, Amr had to choose: where would the new capital be? Many suggested Alexandria, since it was the biggest city. But Amr chose a different spot — near the Fortress of Babylon.

There's a lovely story about why he chose that place: when he was about to march to Alexandria, Amr noticed a **pair of doves nesting on top of his tent**. He didn't want to disturb their nest, so he left the tent standing. That tent became the starting point of a new city called **Fustat** — which means "tent"!

Fustat kept growing and growing — and eventually became the seed of **Cairo**, the capital of Egypt that we know today!

From Egypt also came **Al-Azhar University** — the oldest university in the world that is still running today! Subhanallah!
