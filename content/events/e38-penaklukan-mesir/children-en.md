# Conquest of Egypt 🇪🇬

---

## ⚔️ Why Did This Happen?

Egypt was a fertile land along the Nile River controlled by the Byzantine Empire. The native Egyptians were Coptic Christians who were persecuted by Byzantine rulers because their beliefs were different.

**Amr ibn al-Ash**, a clever companion of the Prophet who had once traded in Egypt, saw that the Egyptian people needed liberation. He proposed a plan to Caliph **Umar ibn al-Khattab** to free Egypt.

At first, Umar was hesitant because Muslim forces were already fighting in many places. But Amr was convinced this was an important mission — not to conquer, but to liberate oppressed people.

## 🛡 How Did It Happen?

Amr set out with a small army — only **4,000 men**. This number was very small compared to the large Byzantine forces. But Amr had a strategy: move quickly and gain local people's support.

The first battle was at **Pelusium**, then at **Heliopolis**. After seeing the seriousness of this war, Umar sent 4,000 additional troops led by **al-Zubayr ibn al-Awwam** — a brave companion famous for his courage.

The biggest battle occurred at **Fortress Babylon** — a large Roman fortress near present-day Cairo. The siege lasted seven months. Al-Zubayr climbed the fortress wall with a ladder and opened the gate from inside. The fortress fell!

The last city to be conquered was **Alexandria** — a great coastal city founded by Alexander the Great. After the Byzantine emperor died, this city surrendered through peaceful treaty.

## 🕊 What Happened Next...

After conquering Egypt, Amr did not make Alexandria the capital. He founded a new city called **Fustat** near Fortress Babylon. Legend says it was named after "tent" because Amr didn't want to disturb a pair of doves nesting on top of his tent.

Fustat became the foundation of **Cairo** — the great city we know today. From this city came Al-Azhar, the world's oldest university still in existence.

Most importantly, **Coptic people were free to worship**. Patriarch Benjamin I, who had been hiding from persecution for years, could finally lead his congregation safely. Taxes were also lighter than during Byzantine times.

Egypt transformed into one of the most important centers of Islamic civilization. Many great scholars and scientists emerged from Egyptian soil.

## 💪 Values We Can Learn

**Courage to take initiative** — Amr saw an opportunity to liberate oppressed people and dared to take action, even with a small army.

**Strategic intelligence** — With 4,000 troops, Amr managed to defeat much larger forces through proper strategy and people's support.

**Justice for all** — After victory, Muslims didn't seek revenge. Coptic people actually had more religious freedom and lived better lives.

**Wise leadership** — The relationship between Amr and Umar showed how field and central leaders can respect each other and work together.

**Liberation, not domination** — This conquest wasn't about looting or ruling, but about freeing people from oppression.
