# Ayyubid Dynasty — Saladin's Legacy (~1171-1260 CE)

## 🌍 What Happened?

In 1171 CE, a Kurdish commander named **Salahuddin Yusuf ibn Ayyub** established a new dynasty in Egypt called the **Ayyubid Dynasty**. The dynasty's name was taken from Salahuddin's father's name, Ayyub.

Previously, Salahuddin worked for **Nur al-Din**, the ruler of Damascus. When Nur al-Din sent him to Egypt to help fight against the Crusaders, Salahuddin successfully overthrew the Fatimid Dynasty that had ruled there for centuries.

What was extraordinary about Salahuddin was his ability to unite two major regions: Egypt and the Levant (including Damascus and Aleppo). This unity was very important because previously the Crusaders could divide and conquer Muslim forces by playing them against each other.

## 📖 The Story

After successfully uniting Egypt and the Levant, Salahuddin prepared great forces to face the Crusaders. In 1187 CE, he won a great battle at **Hattin** which enabled him to recapture the holy city of Jerusalem after 88 years under Crusader control.

This victory shook all of Europe. The three strongest kings of Europe finally came directly to wage war against Salahuddin: **Richard Lionheart** of England, **Philip Augustus** of France, and **Frederick Barbarossa** of Germany. This was called the **Third Crusade**.

Salahuddin did not face them with open warfare alone. He used clever tactics like guerrilla warfare, taking advantage of desert weather, and cutting enemy supply lines. Finally, the war ended with a treaty in Ramla in 1192 CE.

## 🌟 The Lesson

Although Salahuddin died in 1193 CE, the dynasty he founded continued for almost a hundred years. His sons **al-Afdal**, **al-Aziz Uthman**, and **al-Zahir** ruled various regions. Salahuddin's grandson, **al-Kamil**, even succeeded in defeating French king **Louis IX** in Egypt.

The Ayyubid Dynasty was not only strong in war, but also built many schools (madrasas) and hospitals. They supported scholars and scientists from various regions. **Madrasah Salahiyyah** in Jerusalem became a famous center of learning.

Most importantly, this dynasty showed that unity is stronger than division. When Muslims united, they could face great challenges like the Crusades. Salahuddin succeeded because he united various tribes (Arab, Kurdish, Turkic) in one shared vision.

## 🤲 Prayer & Scripture

The Ayyubid Dynasty ended in 1260 CE when power transferred to the **Mamluks**, but their legacy lived on. They proved that with unity and strong faith, Muslims could face any challenge.

Allah SWT teaches the importance of unity in the Quran:

> وَاعْتَصِمُوا بِحَبْلِ اللَّهِ جَمِيعًا وَلَا تَفَرَّقُوا ﴿١٠٣﴾
>
> *"And hold fast, all of you together, to the rope of Allah, and do not be divided."* ﴾103﴿

This verse reminds us that true strength comes from unity in truth. Salahuddin and the Ayyubid Dynasty showed how this principle is applied in real life.

Their greatest legacy was returning Jerusalem as an Islamic holy city and proving that Islam could unite various nations in one great family. Subhanallah.
