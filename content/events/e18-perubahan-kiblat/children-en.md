# The Change of Qiblah 🕌

---

## 🤔 Which Way Do You Face When You Pray?

Did you know that Muslims didn't always face the Ka'bah when praying? For more than a year in Madinah, **Prophet Muhammad ﷺ** and his Companions prayed facing **Bayt al-Maqdis** — a holy mosque in the city of Jerusalem, far to the north.

Bayt al-Maqdis was a sacred place for many prophets before Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. So facing it showed that Islam was connected to the teachings of earlier prophets.

But deep in his heart, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ longed to face the **Ka'bah** — the first house of worship ever built, raised by **Prophet Ibrahim** and his son **Ismail** in Makkah. He would often turn his face toward the sky, hoping for a new command from Allah.

## 🌟 A Command from the Heavens!

One day, while Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was leading the Zuhr prayer, a revelation suddenly came down from Allah! Allah said:

**"We have certainly seen the turning of your face toward the heaven. So turn your face toward al-Masjid al-Haram (the Ka'bah)!"** (QS Al-Baqarah: 144)

And the most amazing part? This command came **right in the middle of prayer!** Prophet Muhammad ﷺ immediately turned around — from facing north to facing south. Everyone praying behind him turned too!

The mosque where this happened was named **Masjid al-Qiblatayn** — meaning the Mosque of Two Qiblahs. It still stands in Madinah to this day!

The news spread quickly across Madinah. At another mosque, a group of Companions were praying Fajr. Someone arrived and called out: *"The qiblah has been changed!"* They immediately turned toward the Ka'bah — even while still bowing in *ruku'*!

## 💬 Some Protested, Some Asked

Not everyone was happy about this change. The hypocrites and some Jewish leaders in Madinah mocked: *"Why do they keep changing direction?"*

Allah answered them: **"To Allah belongs the east and the west. He guides whom He wills to a straight path."** (QS Al-Baqarah: 142)

Some Companions also asked a sincere question: *"What about all the prayers we already performed facing Bayt al-Maqdis? Were they not accepted?"*

Allah answered very gently: **"And never would Allah have caused you to lose your faith. Indeed Allah is, to the people, Kind and Merciful."** (QS Al-Baqarah: 143)

So all the prayers they had performed were still valid and accepted by Allah. Nothing was wasted!

## 💡 Lessons We Can Learn

The change of qiblah teaches us so many things:

🕌 **The Ka'bah unites us all** — No matter where we come from, when we pray we all face the same direction. The Ka'bah is a symbol of Muslim unity around the world!

🙏 **Obeying Allah comes first** — Even though the Companions were used to facing one direction, they turned immediately when the new command came. No hesitation, no complaints — just obedience.

❤️ **Allah never wastes our good deeds** — Every act of worship we do sincerely, Allah accepts it. Nothing goes to waste.

🌍 **Islam is for everyone** — The Ka'bah was built by Prophet Ibrahim, who was neither Arab nor Jewish. Islam doesn't belong to one nation — it belongs to all of humanity.
