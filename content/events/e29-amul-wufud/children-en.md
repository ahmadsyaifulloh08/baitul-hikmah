# The Year of Delegations 🕌

---

## Makkah Now Belongs to Islam

After Prophet Muhammad ﷺ entered the city of Makkah peacefully, the entire Arabian Peninsula was amazed. Makkah was the most important city for the Arabs — and now its people had embraced Islam!

Tribes that had been watching from afar began to think: *"If the Quraysh — the most powerful tribe — have accepted Islam, then this religion must be true."*

And so began a very special year called **'Am al-Wufud** — the Year of Delegations. Tribes from every corner of Arabia sent their representatives to Madinah to meet Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.

## Guests from All Over Arabia

Delegations came one after another. Some traveled from the sandy deserts of Najd, others from the mountains of Yemen, and still others from the coast of Oman. They all wanted to meet the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in person.

One of the delegations that arrived was the **Banu Tamim** — a very large tribe. They came very confidently and called out to the Prophet from outside his room in loud voices. Allah reminded them in the Quran that this was not polite behavior.

There was also a delegation from **Ta'if** — the city whose people had once thrown stones at the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ until he bled! But now, they came in peace to declare their Islam. How great was the Prophet's heart — he welcomed them with a smile, without any grudge at all.

The most unique was the delegation from **Najran** — they were Christians. They discussed religion with the Prophet ﷺ. Even though they didn't agree, the Prophet ﷺ still respected them and made a peace treaty.

## Letters to the Kings

Did you know? Before the delegations came, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ had already sent **letters** to kings and rulers in various lands — the Roman Emperor, the Persian King, the Ruler of Egypt, and many more!

These letters contained an invitation to learn about Islam. The Prophet ﷺ did not force anyone — he invited them politely and wisely. This is what attracted many people to eventually send delegations to Madinah.

Allah says that there is no compulsion in religion: **"There shall be no compulsion in religion. The right course has become clear from the wrong."** (QS Al-Baqarah: 256)

## Lessons from the Year of Delegations

*'Am al-Wufud* teaches us many important things:

🤝 **Respect everyone** — The Prophet ﷺ welcomed every delegation with honor, even those who came with arrogance.

🕊️ **Never force anyone** — Islam was spread through kind invitation and beautiful example, not compulsion.

🌍 **Unity in diversity** — Tribes that were once enemies could unite because of one shared belief.

💪 **Forgive and be open-hearted** — The Prophet ﷺ welcomed the delegation from Ta'if, who had once hurt him. What a great heart he had!

— in just one year, almost the entire Arabian Peninsula united under the banner of Islam. Not because of war, but because of the kindness and the truth of Prophet Muhammad's message!
