# The Delhi Sultanate Founded

## 🌍 What happened?

In 1206 CE, a former slave named Qutb al-Din Aibak founded the first Islamic kingdom in India called the Delhi Sultanate. Aibak was once a slave who was very smart and worked hard until he became a trusted general.

After his master died in war, Aibak didn't return to Afghanistan. He decided to build his own kingdom in Delhi, India. This was a very brave decision because he had to face many enemies.

## 📖 The story

Aibak faced many great challenges. Hindu kings around Delhi didn't want to submit to the new kingdom. Other Muslim generals also wanted to seize power from Aibak. But Aibak was very clever in politics.

He didn't oppress Hindu, Buddhist, or Jain people. He even allowed them to continue practicing their religion and rebuild damaged temples. He also reduced taxes for poor farmers.

Aibak began construction of the very tall Qutb Minar tower and the beautiful Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque. These buildings combined Indian architectural style with Islamic style. The results were amazing.

## 🌟 The lesson

The most amazing thing about Aibak's story is how a former slave could become king. In Islam, a person's status is not determined by birth, but by ability and character. Aibak proved this.

Aibak also showed how to lead a diverse nation. He didn't force his religion on others, but still upheld justice. He respected local culture while introducing Islamic values.

Although Aibak's government lasted only four years (he died in a horse accident in 1210), the foundation he built made the Delhi Sultanate last for 300 years. This proved how solid the system he created was.

## 🤲 Prayer & Verses

Aibak's story teaches that Islam is a religion that values all humans without discriminating based on their origins. What matters most is a person's piety and ability to do good.

Allah SWT created humans differently so they could know each other and work together. Aibak applied this principle by building a peaceful kingdom despite its diverse population.

>
> *"O mankind, indeed We have created you from male and female and made you peoples and tribes that you may know one another. Indeed, the most noble of you in the sight of Allah is the most righteous of you."* ﴾13﴿

Subhanallah, how beautiful the Islamic teaching that values diversity and elevates human dignity based on good deeds.
