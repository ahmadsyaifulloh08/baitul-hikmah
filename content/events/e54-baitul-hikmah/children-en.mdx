# Bayt al-Hikmah — The House of Wisdom 🏛️📚

## 🏛 Did You Know?

In the city of **Baghdad** (now the capital of Iraq), there was once the **biggest and coolest library in the world**! It was called **Bayt al-Hikmah** — which means **"The House of Wisdom."** 📚

Imagine a place filled with books from all over the world — from Greece, India, Persia, and many other lands. And it wasn't just for reading books — it also had **science labs**, **telescopes to watch the stars**, and discussion rooms where scientists debated! 🔬🌟

## 🔬 What Was Bayt al-Hikmah?

Caliph **Harun al-Rashid** built Bayt al-Hikmah around 786 CE. Then his son, **Al-Ma'mun**, made it **way bigger and more awesome**!

Al-Ma'mun loved knowledge so much that he paid translators with **gold equal to the weight of the books they translated**! 🤩 Imagine — if a book weighed 5 kg, you'd get paid 5 kg of gold! 💰

At Bayt al-Hikmah, smart people from different religions worked together: Muslims, Christians, and Jews. What mattered wasn't their religion, but their **brains**! They translated books from Greek into Arabic, and in the process, they **discovered new things** that nobody had ever found before!

## 🌍 The Impact Reaches Us Today!

Thanks to Bayt al-Hikmah:
- **Al-Khwarizmi** invented **algebra** — the math you study in school! ➕✖️
- Ancient Greek books that were lost were **saved** in Arabic
- Europeans later **learned from these Arabic books** and started the Renaissance

So when you study math at school — you're actually learning something that **started at Bayt al-Hikmah**! 📐

Sadly, in 1258, the Mongol army destroyed Baghdad and threw the books into the river. The Tigris River turned **black from all the ink**. Very sad 😢. But the knowledge that had already spread around the world **could not be destroyed**!

## 📖 Islam & Knowledge

Allah says:

> *"My Lord, increase me in knowledge."*
> — Qur'an, Taha: 114

And Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said:

> *"Seeking knowledge is an obligation upon every Muslim."*
> — Narrated by Ibn Majah (Hasan)

Bayt al-Hikmah is proof that **Islam truly loves knowledge**. So if you love reading books and learning new things — you're continuing the spirit of Bayt al-Hikmah! 📚✨
