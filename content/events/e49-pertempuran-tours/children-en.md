# The Battle of Tours/Poitiers (732 CE) - Children's Content

## 🌍 What Happened?

In the year 732 CE, a great battle took place on the plains between the cities of Tours and Poitiers in France. This battle involved Muslim forces from Andalusia (present-day Spain) led by Abdul Rahman al-Ghafiqi against the Frankish forces led by Charles Martel.

The Muslim army came from the south after successfully conquering almost the entire Iberian Peninsula. They wanted to expand Islamic territory northward, entering mainland Europe. Meanwhile, Charles Martel, the Frankish leader, wanted to protect his territory from this expansion.

## 📖 The Story

Abdul Rahman al-Ghafiqi gathered a large army of approximately 60,000-80,000 soldiers consisting of Arab, Berber, and European converts to Islam. This was one of the largest military expeditions in the history of Islamic conquests in Europe.

Charles Martel, nicknamed "The Hammer," successfully united various Frankish tribes to face this threat. Although his forces were smaller (around 30,000 soldiers), they had advantages because they were more united and knew the battlefield terrain.

The two armies met in October 732 CE. For seven days, they faced each other without fighting, each waiting for the right moment. On the eighth day, the great battle began. The Muslim cavalry attacked fiercely, but found it difficult to penetrate the strong and tight Frankish infantry defense.

## 🌟 The Lessons

The most important aspect of this battle was leadership. When Abdul Rahman al-Ghafiqi fell on the battlefield, the Muslim forces lost their spirit and eventually retreated. This shows how important strong leadership is.

The Battle of Tours did not stop the development of Islamic civilization. In Andalusia, Muslims continued to build an extraordinary civilization. The city of Cordoba became the most important center of learning in Europe during the Middle Ages.

Modern historians see that Islamic expansion into Europe was part of the natural political dynamics of that time, not an "invasion" to destroy other civilizations. Muslim forces wanted to expand territory just as other political powers did.
