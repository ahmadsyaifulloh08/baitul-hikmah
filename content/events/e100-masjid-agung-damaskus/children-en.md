# The Great Mosque of Damascus / Umayyad Mosque (706-715 CE) - Children's Content

## 🏛 Fun Fact

The Umayyad Mosque in Damascus is one of the most beautiful mosques in the world! Caliph al-Walid ibn Abd al-Malik built it for almost 10 years (706-715 CE). What's interesting is that this mosque was built on the site of what used to be Saint John Church belonging to Christians.

Caliph al-Walid did not take the church by force, but conducted good negotiations. He offered to build several new churches elsewhere and provide substantial compensation. Finally, Christians agreed and surrendered their church. This shows high tolerance in Islam!

## 🔧 Construction Process

The construction of the Umayyad Mosque required the best architects and artists from various countries. There were experts from Byzantium (present-day Turkey), Persia (present-day Iran), and Arabs working together to create a unique design. They combined different architectural styles into one beautiful unity.

The finest materials were brought from throughout the Mediterranean region. Marble and granite columns were selected one by one to ensure strength and beauty. The most special feature was the mosaics - millions of pieces of colored glass and gold arranged to form pictures of heavenly gardens with beautiful rivers.

The architectural team also created important innovations such as a ventilation system that made cool air circulate well, and an acoustic system that made the imam's voice clearly heard throughout the mosque. The *mihrab* design not only showed the *qiblah* direction, but also amplified sound.

## 🌍 Beauty and Impact

The mosaics of the Umayyad Mosque are considered one of the finest works of Islamic art throughout history. Using millions of pieces of colored glass and gold, these mosaics depict heavenly scenes with gardens, rivers, and beautiful palaces. There are no human or animal images, in accordance with Islamic art principles.

Arabic calligraphy adorning the mosque walls displays Quranic verses about the beauty of Allah's creation. This mosque became not only a place of worship, but also a center for education, social activities, and politics. Scholars taught here, merchants traded around the mosque, and the community gathered for various events.

The Umayyad Mosque became a model for building grand mosques throughout the Islamic world. The architectural style developed here influenced mosque designs in Andalusia, North Africa, and Central Asia. The concept of combining beauty with practical function became a standard in mosque architecture.

## 📖 Meaning and Lessons

The Umayyad Mosque teaches many valuable lessons. First, the importance of dialogue and tolerance between religions. Caliph al-Walid showed that religious differences do not have to result in conflict, but can be resolved through fair and respectful negotiation.

Second, Islam values beauty and art. The beautiful mosaics and amazing calligraphy show that Islam encourages its followers to create noble works of art. Allah SWT says:

> وَلِلَّهِ الْأَسْمَاءُ الْحُسْنَىٰ فَادْعُوهُ بِهَا ﴿١٨٠﴾
>
> *"And to Allah belong the best names, so invoke Him by them."* ﴾180﴿

Third, the importance of international cooperation. Architects from various nations worked together to create an extraordinarily beautiful mosque. This shows that cultural diversity can enrich the treasury of Islamic arts and architecture.
