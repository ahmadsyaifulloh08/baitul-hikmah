## 🧑 Who Was He?

Abu al-Mughith al-Husayn ibn Mansur al-Hallaj was born around 858 CE in Persia. His father was a cotton carder, so he became known as al-Hallaj (the cotton carder).

Al-Hallaj learned Sufism from Sahl al-Tustari and al-Junayd al-Baghdadi. However, he had a different approach - more open in expressing his spiritual experiences compared to his teachers.

He traveled to various places including India, where he interacted with Hindu and Buddhist mystical traditions. This experience broadened his spiritual perspective.

## ⭐ What Did He Do?

Al-Hallaj developed a radical concept of divine love. According to him, love is a cosmic force connecting the world to God, like fire that burns all human attributes.

He preached not only to the spiritual elite but also to common people using poetry and sayings that were easy to understand. This made him popular among ordinary people.

Al-Hallaj wrote "Kitab al-Tawasin" and famous poetry collections. His works used deep symbolism to describe the spiritual journey toward Allah.

## 💡 Why Was He Extraordinary?

Al-Hallaj dared to express the highest spiritual experience with the statement "Ana al-Haqq" (I am the Truth). This wasn't a claim of divinity, but an expression that his ego had vanished and only Allah remained.

He showed extraordinary courage in seeking spiritual truth, even though he had to face harsh criticism from orthodox scholars and was finally executed for being considered heretical.

His death became a symbol of sacrifice in divine love. Later Sufis considered him a "martyr of love" who was willing to sacrifice for spiritual truth.

## 📖 Evidence

Allah SWT said about those who love Him:

> يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ مَن يَرْتَدَّ مِنكُمْ عَن دِينِهِۦ فَسَوْفَ يَأْتِى ٱللَّهُ بِقَوْمٍ يُحِبُّهُمْ وَيُحِبُّونَهُۥٓ أَذِلَّةٍ عَلَى ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ أَعِزَّةٍ عَلَى ٱلْكَٰفِرِينَ يُجَٰهِدُونَ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ وَلَا يَخَافُونَ لَوْمَةَ لَآئِمٍ ﴿٥٤﴾

> *"O you who believe! Whoever among you turns back from his religion, Allah will bring forth a people whom He loves and who love Him, humble toward the believers, stern toward the disbelievers, striving in the way of Allah and not fearing the blame of any blamer."* ﴾54﴿
> — QS. Al-Maidah (5): 54

Al-Hallaj was an example of someone who loved Allah very deeply and was not afraid of others' blame in conveying spiritual truth.

Let us pray: "O Allah, grant us sincere love for You like al-Hallaj, and courage to hold fast to truth despite facing challenges."
