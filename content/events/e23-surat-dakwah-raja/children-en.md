# Letters to the Kings 📜

---

## The Prophet Writes Letters

After the peace treaty at Hudaybiyyah, **Prophet Muhammad ﷺ** had an amazing idea. He wanted to share Islam not just with the Arabs, but with the entire world!

So he made a **silver signet ring** engraved with "Muhammad Rasul Allah" and began writing letters to the most powerful kings on earth. — a leader from a small desert city writing to the mightiest emperors!

The Prophet chose his best companions to carry these letters to distant lands.

## Letters to Four Great Kings

🏛️ **Heraclius — Emperor of Rome**
The letter was carried by **Dihyah al-Kalbi**. Heraclius was very interested! He even questioned Arab traders about Muhammad. He said: *"If what I hear is true, he will rule the ground beneath my feet."* But he was afraid of his court officials and did not accept Islam. Still, he **respected** the Prophet's letter and did not tear it.

👑 **Chosroes — King of Persia**
The letter was carried by **Abdullah ibn Hudhayfah**. Chosroes was very **angry** because Muhammad's name was written before his own. He tore the letter to pieces! When the Prophet heard this, he said: *"May Allah tear apart his kingdom."* And indeed — a few years later, the Persian Empire collapsed!

🏺 **Muqawqis — Ruler of Egypt**
The letter was carried by **Hatib ibn Abi Balta'ah**. Muqawqis was very **polite**. He read the letter carefully, stored it in an ivory casket, and sent beautiful gifts to the Prophet — including a white mule named **Duldul**!

⚜️ **The Negus — King of Abyssinia (Ethiopia)**
The letter was carried by **Amr ibn Umayyah**. The Negus already knew the Muslims because he had protected them before. He **accepted Islam** joyfully! He placed the Prophet's letter upon his eyes as a mark of the highest respect. When he passed away, Prophet ﷺ prayed for him from far away in Madinah.

## Different Responses

Four kings, four very different reactions! One was curious, one was furious, one was polite, and one joyfully accepted Islam.

But Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was not disappointed by those who refused. He knew his job was to **deliver the message**, not to force anyone. As Allah says: *"There is no compulsion in religion."* (QS Al-Baqarah: 256)

What mattered was that the message of Islam had reached every corner of the world — from Rome in the west to Persia in the east!

## Lessons from the Prophet's Letters

📝 **Be brave in sharing the truth** — The Prophet was not afraid to write to the most powerful kings in the world. If we believe something is true, we should be brave enough to share it!

🌍 **Islam is for everyone** — Letters were sent to different nations and faiths. Islam is not just for Arabs, but for all of humankind.

🤲 **Invite, don't force** — The Prophet invited politely. Whoever accepted, alhamdulillah. Whoever refused, that is between them and Allah.

✉️ **Good communication** — The Prophet used polite and respectful language, even toward those who might reject him. We should also speak politely to everyone!
