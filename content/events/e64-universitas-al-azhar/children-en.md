## 🏛 Fun Fact

Al-Azhar in Cairo, Egypt, is one of the oldest universities in the world still operating today! Founded in 970 CE, this university is more than 1,000 years old. Imagine, when Al-Azhar was built, many cities in Europe didn't even have libraries!

Interestingly, the name "Al-Azhar" comes from "Az-Zahra" meaning "The Radiant One," referring to Fatimah az-Zahra, the daughter of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. Originally this mosque was built by the Fatimid Dynasty which followed Shia teachings, but later became a center of Sunni Islamic learning famous throughout the world.

## 🔬 Explanation

Al-Azhar developed a unique learning system called "halaqah" - study circles where a shaykh sits leaning against a mosque pillar, surrounded by students. This system made learning more interactive and personal. Students had to master "instrumental sciences" like Arabic grammar and logic before studying higher sciences like Quranic interpretation and hadith.

The "ijazah" (certificate) system from Al-Azhar became a model for other universities. A student had to truly master all material and receive direct approval from their teacher to be able to teach or issue fatwas (Islamic legal opinions).

Students came from throughout the Islamic world - from Spain, West Africa, Central Asia, to Indonesia - to study at Al-Azhar. After graduating, they returned to their respective countries and spread the knowledge they gained, creating a global Islamic scholarly network.

## 🌍 Modern Impact

Al-Azhar still operates as a modern university that maintains its classical traditions. Its library houses thousands of ancient manuscripts that are treasures of Islamic knowledge. Many classical works are preserved only at Al-Azhar, making it an invaluable repository of knowledge.

Fatwas (Islamic legal decisions) issued by Al-Azhar remain references for Muslims worldwide. This university also played a role in modern Islamic reform movements through figures like Muhammad Abduh who advocated for reform in teaching methods to remain relevant to the times.

Al-Azhar's ijazah system model inspired modern academic degree systems. The concept that a teacher must truly master material and receive recognition from a competent mentor is still applied in higher education systems today.

## 📖 Islam & Knowledge

The Qur'an reminds us of human knowledge limitations and the importance of continuous learning:

> وَمَا أُوتِيتُم مِّنَ الْعِلْمِ إِلَّا قَلِيلًا ﴿٨٥﴾
>
> *"And you have not been given of knowledge except a little." ﴾85﴿*
> — QS. Al-Isra (17): 85

Al-Azhar embodies the spirit of this verse by being a place of learning that never stops. For more than 1,000 years, this university has continued to develop and adapt to the times, while maintaining the basic principle of seeking knowledge to attain Allah's pleasure.

The halaqah tradition at Al-Azhar shows that in Islam, knowledge is not only learned from books, but also through blessed teacher-student relationships. Shaykhs not only teach theory, but also become role models in practicing that knowledge.
