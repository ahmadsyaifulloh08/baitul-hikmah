# The Generous Caliph Uthman 🌟

---

## Who Was Uthman ibn Affan?

**Uthman ibn Affan** was a very special companion of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. He was called *Dhunnurayn* — meaning "the one with two lights" — because he married two daughters of the Prophet ﷺ: **Ruqayyah** and **Umm Kulthum**.

Uthman was known for being very shy and gentle. Prophet Muhammad ﷺ even said that the angels themselves were shy before Uthman! He was also one of the very first people to accept Islam.

Uthman was a wealthy merchant, but his wealth never made him proud — instead, he used it to help others.

## A Well for Everyone

One day, the people of Madinah had trouble getting clean water. The only good well was owned by a merchant who sold water at high prices.

Uthman heard about people's struggles. Without hesitation, he **bought the well** and gave it free to all the people of Madinah! From then on, anyone could take as much water as they needed.

When Prophet Muhammad ﷺ asked for help preparing an army, Uthman donated **300 fully equipped camels**. The Prophet ﷺ was so pleased that he said nothing Uthman did after that day could ever harm him.

## Becoming the Third Caliph

After Caliph **Umar ibn al-Khattab** passed away, the senior companions held a meeting to choose the next caliph. They chose Uthman because of his wisdom, gentleness, and dedication to Islam.

As caliph, Uthman accomplished many great things:

- 📖 **Unified the Quran's text** — he ordered copies of the Quran to be made so all Muslims would read the same text
- 🕌 **Expanded the Prophet's Mosque** — so more people could pray together
- 🌍 **Expanded Muslim lands** — reaching far distant places

## A Great Trial and Patience

Near the end of his time as caliph, a group of troublemakers grew unhappy and caused unrest. They surrounded Uthman's house for many days.

Even though many companions offered to defend him with swords, Uthman **refused**. He did not want any blood shed among Muslims. He said: *"I forbid anyone to fight on my behalf."*

Uthman passed away as a martyr while he was reading the Quran. Prophet Muhammad ﷺ had once foretold that Uthman would enter Paradise, but that he would face a great trial.

Uthman's story teaches us about **generosity**, **patience**, and **love of peace**. He chose to sacrifice himself rather than cause conflict among Muslims.
