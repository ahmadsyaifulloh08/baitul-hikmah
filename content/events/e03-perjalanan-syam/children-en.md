# The Journey to Syria

## 🐪 Young Muhammad Wants to Come Along

A twelve-year-old boy who is very close to his uncle. Wherever his uncle goes, he wants to follow. That boy's name was **Muhammad**, and his uncle's name was **Abu Talib**.

One day, Abu Talib was getting ready for a very long trading journey — all the way to the land of Syria! The trip would take weeks, crossing hot deserts, riding camels from morning until evening. Abu Talib thought the journey was too hard for a little boy.

But young Muhammad did not want to be left behind. He held tightly onto his uncle's camel rope and pleaded: "Uncle, please don't leave me!" Abu Talib looked at his nephew's hopeful face, and his heart melted. "Alright," said Abu Talib, "I will take you with me. We shall not be parted."

Young Muhammad joined his very first trading journey to a faraway land!

## 🏕️ Meeting a Mysterious Monk

After weeks of traveling, the caravan finally arrived at a town called Busra. On the edge of the road in that town, there was a small monastery where an old monk named **Bahira** lived.

Bahira was a Christian monk who spent his days reading ancient scriptures. For years, he had watched caravans from Mecca pass by his monastery. But he never cared — he simply stayed inside his monastery in silence.

But this time was different! Bahira suddenly came outside and invited everyone to share a meal at his monastery. All the merchants were surprised — "How strange that this monk is inviting us!"

When everyone was seated and eating, Bahira studied each person's face carefully. He seemed to be searching for someone. Then he asked: "Is everyone here?" They answered: "Oh, there's a young boy we left behind to watch our goods." Bahira said immediately: "Call him! He must come and eat with us!"

## ⭐ A Special Sign

When young Muhammad arrived, Bahira watched him closely and asked him many questions. Every answer made Bahira more certain, and then he found a special mark on Muhammad's back — a sign of a coming prophet, written in ancient scriptures.

Bahira rushed to Abu Talib: "Take good care of your nephew, he has a very great future — take him back to Mecca and protect him, especially from the Jews who may try to harm this child if they recognize the same sign." Abu Talib was alarmed and quickly finished his trading business, then brought Muhammad back to Mecca as fast as he could.

## 🌟 Lessons from the Journey

From this story we learn many beautiful things!

**First**, Allah always protects those He loves. Young Muhammad was an orphan — his father had passed away before he was born, and his mother died when he was still small. But Allah sent Abu Talib, who loved him like his own son.

**Second**, Abu Talib's love for Muhammad was tremendous. He brought Muhammad on a long, difficult journey simply because he couldn't bear to be parted from him. That's how family should be — loving and protecting each other.

**Third**, even though Muhammad was still young, he already showed extraordinary qualities. He was honest, polite, and calm — so much so that even a monk from a faraway land could recognize how special he was.

Later, Muhammad would grow up to be known by everyone in Mecca as *al-Amin* — which means "the most trustworthy person." Subhanallah!
