# The Translation Movement — Translating the World's Knowledge 📚

---

## The Prince Who Loved Learning

**Khalid ibn Yazid** was the grandson of Muawiyah, the founder of the Umayyad Dynasty. He could have become caliph — but the position was taken by someone else. Instead of being angry or sad, Khalid chose a different path: he decided to **devote his life to knowledge**!

Khalid was very curious about the ancient sciences written in Greek and Coptic (ancient Egyptian language). But the problem was, most Arabs could not read those books because of the language barrier.

## A Brilliant Idea: Translate!

Khalid had a very clever idea — why not **translate** those books into Arabic? That way, everyone could read them!

He brought brilliant scholars from Egypt, including a monk named **Marianos**, to translate books about:

- ⚗️ **Alchemy** — the study of the properties of substances (the ancestor of modern chemistry!)
- ⭐ **Astronomy** — the study of stars and planets
- 💊 **Medicine** — the science of healing the sick

This was the **very first time** scientific books were translated into Arabic on a large scale!

Serious yet friendly mood. Composition of two people facing each other with manuscripts between them.*

## A Seed That Grew into a Mighty Tree

What Khalid started turned out to be the beginning of something truly enormous! The translation movement continued to grow after Khalid, reaching its peak during the Abbasid era at a place called the **Bayt al-Hikmah** (House of Wisdom) in Baghdad.

There, hundreds of books from Greek, Persian, Indian, and other languages were translated into Arabic. From these translations were born brilliant Muslim scientists whose discoveries we still use today!

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ once said: **"Wisdom is the lost property of the believer; wherever he finds it, he has the greatest right to it."** (Narrated by al-Tirmidhi)

## Lessons from the Translation Movement

The story of Khalid and the translation movement teaches us:

- **Knowledge is more valuable than a throne** — Khalid chose learning over ruling, and his contribution was far greater than that of most kings
- **Learn from everyone** — Khalid was not ashamed to learn from non-Muslim scholars, because knowledge belongs to all people
- **Small steps, big impact** — What Khalid started eventually grew into the greatest scientific movement of its time in the world
- **Reading changes the world** — The very first command in Islam is "Read!" and the translation movement was the living proof of that command

📚 So never stop learning and reading — who knows, you might change the world just like Khalid!
