# Spanish Inquisition - Book Burning and Destruction of Islamic Heritage

## 🌍 End of Islam in Spain

In 1492 CE, Granada, the last Islamic kingdom in Spain, fell to the forces of King Ferdinand and Queen Isabella. After nearly 800 years of Islam flourishing in the Iberian Peninsula (Spain and Portugal), it was now the Christians' turn to rule.

Initially, the Spanish king and queen promised to protect the Muslims who still lived there. But that promise was not kept. They established the Spanish Inquisition, an institution tasked with punishing people considered disloyal to the Christian religion.

## 📖 Burning Millions of Books

The saddest part of this event was the mass burning of books. Cardinal Cisneros, a Christian religious leader, ordered the burning of more than one million Islamic books and manuscripts in Granada's town square in 1499 CE.

The books burned were not just religious books, but also books on science, medicine, mathematics, astronomy, history, and literature. Many works by famous Muslim scientists like Ibn Rushd and Ibn Sina were destroyed in the flames. This was one of the greatest losses in the history of human knowledge.

## 🕌 Muslims in Hiding

After Granada fell, Muslims were given choices: convert to Christianity, leave Spain, or be killed. Many were forced to pretend to become Christians to survive. They were called Moriscos (little Muslims).

These Moriscos secretly continued practicing Islam. They hid Qurans in false walls or buried Islamic books in the ground. Prayers were performed secretly at night. They even created a special way of writing Spanish using Arabic letters so they could continue studying Islam without being detected.

🎨 *Illustration: Muslim family secretly praying in an underground room, candlelight illuminating the dark space, Quran hidden behind a wall, expressions solemn yet anxious, mysterious and spiritual atmosphere*

## ⚔️ Final Resistance

The Moriscos did not give up easily. In 1568-1571 CE, they rebelled in what was called the War of Alpujarras. Led by Aben Humeya, they fought to defend their rights to practice Islamic culture and religion.

Unfortunately, this rebellion failed. Spanish forces successfully and cruelly suppressed the resistance. Finally, in 1609-1614 CE, all Moriscos were expelled from Spain. About 300,000-400,000 people were forced to leave their homeland and seek new homes in North Africa and the Ottoman Empire.

## 📖 Historical Lessons

> وَلَا تَقْتُلُوا النَّفْسَ الَّتِي حَرَّمَ اللَّهُ إِلَّا بِالْحَقِّ ﴿١٥١﴾
>
> *"And do not kill the soul which Allah has forbidden, except by right."* ﴾151﴿

The tragedy of the Spanish Inquisition reminds us that every human soul is precious and should not be oppressed. The burning of books and mass expulsion of Muslims from Spain is an example of how dangerous religious fanaticism and intolerance can be.

This event also teaches us to value knowledge. Many valuable scientific works were lost forever due to hatred and ignorance. We must protect books and cultural heritage so future generations can learn from the past.
