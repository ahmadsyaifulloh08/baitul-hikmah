# Umrah al-Qada 🕋

---

## Finally Going Home!

Remember the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah? A year ago, the Muslims had to go home without performing umrah. But the treaty said: they could come back **next year**!

And now the time had come! **Prophet Muhammad ﷺ** invited about **2,000 companions** to travel to Makkah. They wore white *ihram* garments and brought sacrificial animals. Many of them hadn't seen Makkah in seven years — the city where they were born!

This time, the Quraysh kept their promise. They emptied the city and moved to the surrounding hills, letting the Muslims enter.

## A Strong Tawaf

When they reached the Ka'bah, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and the companions performed **tawaf** — circling the Ka'bah seven times. But they didn't just walk normally — they walked **with quick, strong strides**!

Why? Because the Quraysh had spread rumors that the Muslims were weak and sickly. The Prophet wanted to show that it wasn't true! The companions also bared their right shoulders to show their strong muscles.

The Quraysh people peeking from the hills were surprised to see how strong the Muslims were. Their rumors were proven wrong!

## Bilal on Top of the Ka'bah

The most moving moment was when the Prophet ordered **Bilal ibn Rabah** to climb on top of the Ka'bah and call the **adhan**!

Bilal used to be a slave from Abyssinia (Ethiopia). He was once cruelly tortured right here in Makkah for embracing Islam. His master, Umayyah ibn Khalaf, placed a heavy boulder on his chest under the blazing sun. But Bilal kept saying: *"Ahad! Ahad!"* (Allah is One!)

And now... that same Bilal stood on top of the Ka'bah — the most honored place in Makkah — calling the adhan with his beautiful voice! His voice echoed across the entire city.

This is proof that in Islam, a person's honor comes not from wealth or family name, but from faith and piety!

## Lessons from Umrah al-Qada

🕋 **Patience is beautiful** — A year ago, they had to go home without umrah. But they were patient, and now they could worship at the Ka'bah with joy! Patience isn't easy, but its fruit is sweet.

💪 **Show your best** — The Prophet and companions walked strongly during tawaf to show their strength. We should always show the best version of ourselves!

🤝 **Keep your promises** — Both the Muslims and the Quraysh honored the agreement. The Prophet stayed exactly three days and left when asked. Keeping promises is very important!

⭐ **Everyone is equal** — Bilal, a former slave, stood at the most honored place in Makkah. In Islam, the most honored person is the most pious — not the richest or most famous!
