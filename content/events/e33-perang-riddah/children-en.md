# The Riddah Wars ⚔️

---

## Sad News from Madinah

The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ had passed away. All of Madinah was grief-stricken. The companions wept, and even the mighty **Umar ibn al-Khattab** was so overcome with sorrow that he could not accept the reality.

But one man remained calm and strong: **Abu Bakr al-Siddiq**, the Prophet's closest companion. Abu Bakr stood before the people and said: *"Whoever worshipped Muhammad, know that Muhammad has died. Whoever worships Allah, know that Allah is alive and will never die."*

Abu Bakr was then chosen as the **caliph** — the first leader of the Muslim community after the Prophet.

## The Tribes That Rebelled

After the Prophet's death, many tribes across the Arabian Peninsula refused to obey the new leader. Some stopped paying **zakat** — the obligatory charity to help the poor. Others followed people who falsely claimed to be prophets!

There was **Musaylimah al-Kadhdhab** (the liar) who claimed prophethood in Yamamah. There was **Tulayha** who claimed prophethood among the Banu Asad. And there were others in Yemen and other distant lands.

Nearly the entire Arabian Peninsula was in rebellion! Only Makkah, Madinah, and Taif remained loyal.

## Abu Bakr Would Not Compromise

The companions suggested Abu Bakr compromise — let the tribes skip *zakat*, as long as they still prayed. But Abu Bakr firmly refused!

Abu Bakr declared: *"By Allah, I will fight anyone who separates prayer from zakat! If they were to refuse me even a hobbling cord that they used to pay to the Messenger of Allah, I would fight them for it!"*

Abu Bakr knew that Islam is complete — you cannot pick and choose which parts to follow and which to ignore.

## Eleven Armies Sent Out

Abu Bakr then prepared **eleven armies**, each led by the best companions! Each army was sent in a different direction across the Arabian Peninsula.

The most famous commander was **Khalid ibn al-Walid** — the warrior known as the *Sword of Allah*. Khalid moved with incredible speed, defeating one rebel force after another. From one battlefield to the next, he brought victory after victory.

Abu Bakr instructed every commander: *"Offer Islam first. If they accept, welcome them back. If they refuse, only then fight them."*

## Victory and Reunification

One by one, the rebellious tribes were defeated or returned to Islam. The false prophets were stopped. **Tulayha** even repented and became a good Muslim again!

In less than a year, the entire Arabian Peninsula was reunited under the banner of Islam. Abu Bakr had saved the Muslim community from falling apart. Without his firmness, Islam might have remained a small religion in just Makkah and Madinah.

The Riddah Wars teach us that **having the courage to stand for what is right** matters most, especially when many people tell you to give up.

## Lessons from the Riddah Wars

The Riddah Wars teach us many things:

- **Steadfastness** — Abu Bakr did not waver even when almost everyone advised compromise
- **Islam is complete** — prayer, *zakat*, and other acts of worship cannot be separated
- **Good leadership** — Abu Bakr was always fair, even to enemies who wished to repent
- **The door of repentance is always open** — even those who rebelled could return to the truth

Allah says: **"Whoever among you turns back from his religion, Allah will bring forth a people whom He loves and who love Him."** (QS. Al-Ma'idah: 54)
