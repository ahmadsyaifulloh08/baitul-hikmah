# Imad ad-Din Zengi Captures Edessa

## 🌍 What Happened?

After the Crusaders captured many Muslim cities, they established four states in the Middle East. One of them was the County of Edessa located in the north. This city became the most distant and isolated from other Crusader states.

In 1127 CE, a Muslim leader named Imad ad-Din Zengi emerged. He was appointed as ruler of Mosul and began uniting scattered Muslim rulers. Zengi had a great dream: to drive the Crusaders from Muslim lands.

Zengi chose Edessa as his first target because this city was weakest and difficult to receive help. For years, he prepared mature strategies and built strong armies.

## 📖 The Story

In December 1144 CE, Zengi led massive attacks against Edessa. His army consisted of various tribes: Turkish, Arab, and Kurdish united for jihad. They brought sophisticated siege equipment and high spirits.

The siege lasted several weeks. Zengi's troops dug tunnels under city walls and used wall-breaking machines. They also chanted takbir and Qur'anic verses to raise spirits.

Count Joscelin II, ruler of Edessa, was outside the city when attacks began. He could not return to help defend his city. On December 24, 1144 CE, Zengi's troops successfully breached walls and captured Edessa.

Unlike brutal Crusader methods in Jerusalem, Zengi treated inhabitants well. He did not massacre civilians and respected those not involved in war.

## 🌟 The Lesson

Zengi's victory at Edessa taught that unity is key to strength. Previously, Muslim rulers fought each other and were weak. When they united under Zengi's leadership, they could defeat the Crusaders.

Zengi also showed the importance of mature preparation and proper strategy. He did not attack impulsively, but planned everything carefully for years.

Most importantly, Zengi proved that good treatment of civilian populations is part of Islamic teachings. Even in war situations, he maintained character and did not commit cruelties.

This victory became inspiration for other Muslim leaders that Crusaders could be defeated. This awakened resistance spirit that would eventually liberate entire Holy Land.

## 🤲 Prayer & Scripture

> وَلَيَنصُرَنَّ ٱللَّهُ مَن يَنصُرُهُۥٓۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَقَوِيٌّ عَزِيزٞ ﴿٤٠﴾
>
> *"And Allah will surely support those who support Him. Indeed, Allah is Powerful and Exalted in Might. ﴾40﴿"*
> — QS. Al-Hajj (22): 40

Zengi and his troops fought not for personal power, but to liberate holy lands from occupation. They united in jihad spirit and received Allah's help.

Zengi's story teaches that when Muslims unite, prepare well, and struggle in the right path, Allah will give victory. We must always remember that true strength comes from Allah.
