# Shaykh Abdul Qadir al-Jilani

## 🧑 Who Was He?

Abdul Qadir al-Jilani was born in 1077 CE in Gilan, near the Caspian Sea. His father was Abi Salih Musa and his mother was Fatimah. His family had noble lineage, descendants of Imam Hasan bin Ali.

From childhood, Abdul Qadir showed great love for religious knowledge. He was diligent in studying the Qur'an and often spent time in worship. His parents were very proud of their son's character and dedication to seeking knowledge.

## ⭐ What Did He Do?

At age 18, Abdul Qadir left his hometown to study in Baghdad. This journey required great courage because Baghdad was very far away and he had to live independently.

In Baghdad, he studied under many famous teachers. He learned jurisprudence, hadith, Qur'anic interpretation, and Sufism. His life was very simple - he lived in a madrasah, often fasted, and woke up at night for tahajud prayers.

After completing his education, Abdul Qadir began teaching. In 1127 CE, he was appointed head of the madrasah. Thousands of students came from various countries to learn from him. He didn't only teach Islamic law, but also how to purify the heart and develop noble character.

## 💡 Why Was He Extraordinary?

Abdul Qadir al-Jilani founded the Qadiriyyah Order, a way of learning religion that combined Islamic law with purification of the heart. This order taught people how to draw closer to Allah in the correct way.

His teachings spread throughout the Islamic world. From India to Africa, from Turkey to Indonesia, many people followed his order. He successfully showed that one could be both learned and pure of heart.

Abdul Qadir also wrote many important books, including "Al-Ghunya li Thalib Tariq al-Haqq" which became a complete guide for people wanting to learn Sufism. His books are still studied today.

He passed away in 1166 CE in Baghdad, but his teachings continue to live. His tomb in Baghdad is still visited by many people from around the world.

## 📖 Scripture

The Qur'an teaches the importance of seeking knowledge with humility:

> وَمَا أُوتِيتُم مِّنَ الۡعِلۡمِ إِلَّا قَلِيلًا ﴿٨٥﴾
>
> *"And you have not been given of knowledge except a little. ﴾85﴿"*
> — QS. Al-Isra' (17): 85

This verse was always remembered by Abdul Qadir al-Jilani. Even though he had become a great scholar, he still felt his knowledge was little compared to Allah's unlimited knowledge.

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever takes a path seeking knowledge, Allah will make easy for him a path to Paradise." This is what Abdul Qadir did - he left his hometown to seek knowledge in Baghdad.
