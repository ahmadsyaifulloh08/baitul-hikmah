# Madrasah Nizamiyya: The Revolution of Islamic Education

## 🌍 What happened?

In 1065 CE, a very intelligent minister named **Nizam al-Mulk** established the world's first Islamic higher education school. This school was named Madrasah Nizamiyya and was built in Baghdad, Iraq. Before this, people learned religion in mosques or in small circles with teachers.

Nizam al-Mulk saw that the Islamic world needed a more organized and structured education system. He wanted to create schools that could produce high-quality scholars with standardized knowledge.

Madrasah Nizamiyya was built magnificently on the banks of the Tigris River. The building had a large library containing thousands of rare books and classrooms that could accommodate hundreds of students.

## 📖 The story

The first rector of Nizamiyya Baghdad was **Abu Ishaq al-Shirazi**, a very famous Shafi'i scholar. He wrote a jurisprudence book that became a standard reference for centuries. Students came from all corners of the Islamic world, from Spain to Central Asia.

After Nizamiyya Baghdad's success, Nizam al-Mulk established branches in other important cities like Nishapur, Isfahan, Balkh, Herat, and Basra. Each madrasah had its own local characteristics, but still followed the same curriculum and standards.

The most historic moment was when **Imam al-Ghazali** was appointed as professor at Nizamiyya Baghdad in 1091 CE. Al-Ghazali was then 33 years old and already famous as a brilliant intellectual. It was at Nizamiyya that he wrote his great works such as "Ihya Ulum al-Din".

The madrasah funding system was very unique. Nizam al-Mulk used the waqf system, donating land and buildings whose proceeds were used to run the school. Students received scholarships, accommodation, and free books.

## 🌟 The lessons

Madrasah Nizamiyya taught important lessons about the importance of structured education systems. Previously, education quality depended heavily on individual teacher abilities. Nizamiyya created uniform curriculum standards, teaching methods, and examination systems.

The ijazah (certificate) system developed by Nizamiyya became the forerunner of modern diploma systems. Graduates who received ijazah had qualifications recognized throughout the Islamic world, so they could teach or work anywhere.

Nizamiyya also introduced the concept of holistic education that not only taught religious knowledge, but also shaped students' character and morals. Students were not only intellectually smart, but also had good morals.

Unfortunately, over time, the Nizamiyya system became too rigid and less open to renewal. When Mongol attacks occurred in the 13th century CE, many madrasas were destroyed, including the magnificent Nizamiyya Baghdad.

## 🤲 Prayer & Evidence

Islam strongly emphasizes the importance of seeking knowledge. The Quran teaches that we should always ask Allah for additional knowledge:

> وَقُل رَّبِّ زِدْنِي عِلْمًا ﴿١١٤﴾
>
> *"And say: 'My Lord, increase me in knowledge.'" ﴾114﴿*
> — QS. Taha (20): 114

Nizam al-Mulk and the founders of Nizamiyya believed that education was the best investment for the future of the Islamic community. They willingly donated their wealth through waqf so that future generations could receive quality education.

Nizamiyya scholars also taught that knowledge must be practiced and spread to others. It's not enough to be smart alone, but one must be beneficial to the wider community.

A good Islamic education system must integrate knowledge, noble character, and spirituality. This was the characteristic of Nizamiyya and what made it successful in educating thousands of great scholars.
