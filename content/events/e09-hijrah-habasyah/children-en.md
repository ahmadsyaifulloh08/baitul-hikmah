# A Journey Across the Sea! ⛵

## ⚔️ Why Did They Have to Leave?

Friends, imagine living in a place where bad people keep hurting you just because you believe in Allah. They hit you, mock you, and forbid you from praying.

That's what happened to Muslims in Mecca. After Prophet Muhammad SAW began preaching openly, the Quraysh became even angrier and crueler. They tortured Muslims — especially those who were poor and had no protection.

Prophet Muhammad SAW was deeply worried about his companions' safety. He thought: "Where is a safe place for them?"

Then he remembered a faraway land across the Red Sea — called **Abyssinia** (now known as Ethiopia). There lived a very just king named **the Negus** (Najashi). Even though the Negus was Christian, he was famous for never treating anyone unfairly.

Prophet Muhammad SAW told his companions: "Go to Abyssinia. There is a king who never wrongs anyone."

## 🛡️ The Story

The first group to leave numbered about sixteen people, including **Uthman ibn Affan** and his wife **Ruqayyah** — the Prophet's own daughter! They left secretly at night, slipped to the coast, and boarded a boat to cross the Red Sea.

Imagine how sad it must have been to leave home, family, and the land where they grew up. But they knew it was for their safety.

Soon after, a much larger group followed — about a hundred people! They were led by **Ja'far ibn Abi Talib**, the Prophet's cousin who was very clever and a wonderful speaker.

When they arrived in Abyssinia, King Negus welcomed them kindly and gave them a safe place to live. At last, after months of living in fear, they could worship in peace!

But the story wasn't over yet...

### The Quraysh Wouldn't Give Up!

The Quraysh were furious when they learned the Muslims had escaped to Abyssinia. They sent two envoys carrying expensive gifts for the Negus. The envoys said: "O King, these people are rebels. Send them back to us!"

The Negus was a just king. He wouldn't make a decision without hearing both sides. He called the Muslims to his palace and asked: "What do you have to say for yourselves?"

Then **Ja'far** stood up and delivered an amazing speech. He described how their lives were before Islam — full of wickedness and ignorance — and how Islam transformed them into honest, kind, and just people.

Then the Negus asked: "Do you have anything from Allah's revelation?"

Ja'far recited verses from **Surah Maryam** — the chapter about Mary and Jesus. When the Negus heard those verses, tears streamed down his face! He declared:

**"Indeed, this comes from the same source of light as what Jesus brought!"**

Then he turned to the Quraysh envoys and said firmly: **"Go! By Allah, I will never hand them over to you!"**

## 🕊️ What Happened After

The Muslims lived safely in Abyssinia for many years. They could worship peacefully, study their religion, and build their community. King Negus protected them from all threats.

What's amazing is that even though the Negus was Christian, he deeply respected the Muslims. And do you know what? Years later, when the Negus passed away, Prophet Muhammad SAW performed a special funeral prayer for him from far away (called salat al-gha'ib)! This shows how much the Prophet valued the Negus's kindness and justice.

## 💪 Lessons for Us

**If you're being treated unfairly, seek help.** Prophet Muhammad SAW taught that when we cannot survive in a dangerous place, it's okay to go somewhere safe. What matters is that we hold on to our faith.

**Justice knows no religion.** The Negus was a Christian king who protected Muslims. Goodness and justice can come from anyone. We should also be fair to everyone, no matter their religion or background.

**Honesty is stronger than gifts.** The Quraysh brought expensive presents, but Ja'far brought only the truth. And truth won! This teaches us that honesty is always worth more than riches.

**Never be ashamed to tell the truth about yourself.** Ja'far wasn't ashamed to say that his people were once ignorant and wicked. It was precisely his honesty that made the Negus trust him.
