## 🧑 Who Was He?

Muhammad ibn Idris al-Shafi'i was born in 767 CE in Gaza, Palestine. His family had noble lineage tracing back to Abd al-Manaf, the grandfather of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. His father died when he was still young, so his mother took him to Mecca.

In Mecca, al-Shafi'i grew up among the Hudhayl tribe, famous for their pure Arabic language. By age 7, he had memorized the entire Quran, and by age 15, he had memorized the Al-Muwatta book by Imam Malik.

When he was 20 years old, he traveled to Medina to study under Imam Malik. Later he moved to Baghdad and learned from the students of Abu Hanifah. This experience of learning from two major schools opened his mind to Islamic legal methodology.

## ⭐ What Did He Do?

Al-Shafi'i created a systematic and clear methodology for Islamic law. He established a hierarchy of legal sources: the Quran, authentic Hadith, Ijma (scholars' consensus), and Qiyas (analogy). No other sources were allowed to be used.

Al-Shafi'i's greatest work was the book "Al-Risala," the first book to discuss usul al-fiqh (Islamic legal methodology) comprehensively. This book became the foundation for all schools in developing Islamic law.

Al-Shafi'i also developed strict criteria for hadith that could be used as legal basis. He rejected hadith mursal (with incomplete chain to the Prophet) and only accepted hadith with perfectly connected chains.

## 💡 Why Was He Extraordinary?

Al-Shafi'i successfully united various conflicting legal approaches into one coherent system. Previously, the Maliki and Hanafi schools often disagreed due to unclear methodology.

The concepts of "old school" and "new school" that he developed showed intellectual flexibility. When he moved to Egypt, he changed some of his opinions based on different local conditions, yet remained consistent with his methodology.

Al-Shafi'i's influence was very extensive. All schools of fiqh adopted the methodological framework he established. Even schools that disagreed with his legal opinions still used his systematization of usul al-fiqh.

## 📖 Evidence

Allah SWT said about the importance of following clear guidance:

> وَأَنَّ هَٰذَا صِرَٰطِى مُسْتَقِيمًا فَٱتَّبِعُوهُ ۖ وَلَا تَتَّبِعُوا۟ ٱلسُّبُلَ فَتَفَرَّقَ بِكُمْ عَن سَبِيلِهِۦ ﴿١٥٣﴾

> *"And indeed, this is My straight path, so follow it! Do not follow other paths, lest they lead you astray from His path."* ﴾153﴿
> — QS. Al-An'am (6): 153

Al-Shafi'i showed that clear and systematic methodology is the straight path in understanding Islamic law. Without proper usul al-fiqh, we could go astray among various conflicting opinions.

Let us pray: "O Allah, grant us correct understanding of Your religion as taught by Imam al-Shafi'i, so that we do not go astray in understanding Your law."
