## 🏛 Did you know?

In 622 CE, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ built the world's first mosque in a place called Quba. This mosque was built in just four days!

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ didn't just stand around. He helped carry stones and wood to build the mosque. His companions also helped with great enthusiasm.

Quba Mosque was very simple. Its walls were made of clay bricks, the roof from palm leaves, and the floor from compacted earth.

## 🔬 What is it?

A mosque is a place for congregational prayer. The word "mosque" means "place of prostration to Allah." At Quba Mosque, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ taught how to pray in congregation correctly.

Quba Mosque also became a place of learning. The companions sat on the mosque porch to listen to teachings from Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.

What was special was that this mosque had its own ablution area. This was a new idea at that time. Prophet Muhammad ﷺ taught the importance of cleanliness before prayer.

## 🌍 Its impact until now!

Quba Mosque still stands today in Saudi Arabia. Millions of people from around the world come to visit every year.

The design of Quba Mosque became an example for other mosques in the world. Mosques are made simple but functional, with prayer areas, learning areas, and ablution areas.

In Indonesia, there are also many mosques that follow the Quba Mosque pattern. Every mosque has ablution areas, prayer rooms, and places to learn the Quran.

## 📖 Islam & Knowledge

Allah says in the Quran about Quba Mosque: "A mosque whose foundation was laid from the first day on piety is more worthy of your standing for prayer therein."

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ also said: "Whoever prays at Quba Mosque will have the reward equivalent to umrah." This shows how special this first mosque is.

Building a mosque is not just about the physical building. What matters most is sincere intention and a heart full of piety to Allah.
