# The Conquest of Jerusalem 🕌

---

## A Very Special Holy City

**Jerusalem** — called **Al-Quds** in Arabic — is a very special city. It is holy to three religions: Islam, Christianity, and Judaism. In this city stands **Al-Aqsa Mosque**, from where Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was taken up to the heavens during the miraculous Night Journey.

In 637 CE, Jerusalem was ruled by the Byzantine Empire (Eastern Rome). After the Muslim armies won great battles across Syria, the time came to liberate Jerusalem.

The Muslim commander, **Abu Ubaidah ibn al-Jarrah**, surrounded the city. But the Muslim army did not attack — they waited patiently. The Christian leader of Jerusalem, **Patriarch Sophronius**, finally agreed to surrender the city — with one condition: **"I will only surrender to the caliph himself!"**

## The Caliph Who Walked on Foot

When he heard Sophronius's condition, Caliph **Umar ibn al-Khattab** decided to travel from Madinah to Jerusalem himself. But the way he traveled surprised everyone!

Umar brought only **one camel and one servant**. And they **took turns** riding the camel — sometimes Umar rode, sometimes his servant did! When they arrived near Jerusalem, it happened to be the servant's turn on the camel, and Umar was walking through the mud on foot.

General Abu Ubaidah was embarrassed to see the caliph arrive like that. He said: "O Commander of the Faithful, the people of the city will see you this way..."

Umar answered firmly: **"We are a people whom Allah honored through Islam. If we seek honor from anything else, Allah will humiliate us."**

The people of Jerusalem, who had been afraid, were now amazed — the ruler of half the world turned out to be a truly humble man!

## A Promise of Protection for Everyone

After arriving, Umar gave the people of Jerusalem a very important **written promise**. It was called the **Covenant of Aelia**, and it said:

- Churches **must not** be destroyed or taken
- Christian residents are **safe** — their lives and property are protected
- **No one** will be forced to change their religion
- Everyone will be treated **fairly**

Then a truly historic moment happened. When prayer time came, Umar and Sophronius were inside the **Church of the Holy Sepulchre** — the holiest church for Christians. Sophronius invited Umar to pray inside.

But Umar **refused**! He said: "If I pray here, Muslims after me will take this church and turn it into a mosque." So Umar prayed **outside the church** — to protect the rights of Christians!

## Cleaning Al-Aqsa Mosque

Umar then searched for the site of **Al-Aqsa Mosque** — the holy place mentioned in the Quran. But he found a sad sight: the area had been turned into a **rubbish dump** by the Roman rulers!

Without hesitation, Umar immediately got down on his hands and knees and **started cleaning it himself**. He picked up rubbish and dirt with his own hands. Seeing the caliph at work, the companions rushed to help. Together, they cleaned the sacred area.

This was where a simple mosque was later built — the beginning of the Al-Aqsa Mosque we know today.

Umar also did something very fair: he allowed **Jews to return and live in Jerusalem** — after being expelled by the Romans for hundreds of years. So the Muslim conquest of Jerusalem brought good to everyone — Muslims, Christians, and Jews alike!
