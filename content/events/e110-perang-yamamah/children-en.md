# The Battle of Yamamah 📖⚔️

---

## The Liar from Yamamah

In a region called **Yamamah**, far to the east of Arabia, there was a man named **Musaylimah**. He claimed to be a prophet — but he was a great liar! That is why he was nicknamed **al-Kadhdhab**, which means "the liar."

Musaylimah once sent a letter to the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, claiming to also be a messenger of Allah. But the Prophet replied briefly: *"From Muhammad, messenger of Allah, to Musaylimah the liar."*

After the Prophet ﷺ passed away, Musaylimah became even bolder. He gathered **40,000 followers** and made up false rules — including allowing things that Allah had forbidden!

## Khalid ibn al-Walid Is Sent

Caliph **Abu Bakr** knew that Musaylimah was extremely dangerous. So he sent the greatest commander in Islam: **Khalid ibn al-Walid**, known as the *Sword of Allah*.

Khalid brought about **13,000 warriors**. Among them were many **Quran memorizers** — special people who had memorized every verse of the Quran in their hearts! In those days, the Quran had not yet been written into one book, so these memorizers were the ones keeping the Quran safe.

When they reached Yamamah, the Muslim army faced a very tough battle. Musaylimah's forces were far larger and were fighting on their own land. Three times the Muslim army was pushed back!

## The Companions' Battle Cry

Just when the Muslim army was close to defeat, the companions rallied each other. They shouted to one another:

*"O Ansar! Show your spirit!"*
*"O Muhajirin! Show your tenacity!"*

A companion named **Abu Hudhayfah** cried out: *"O people of the Quran! Adorn the Quran with your deeds!"*

And **Thabit ibn Qays** planted himself in the ground and declared: *"I will not move from this spot!"* He fought bravely until he fell as a martyr.

These companions showed that the Quran is not just for reading and memorizing — it is for living by, even at the cost of the greatest sacrifice.

## The Garden of Death

Musaylimah's forces finally retreated and hid behind the walls of a **large walled garden**. They thought the tall walls would protect them.

But a brave companion named **al-Bara' ibn Malik** shouted: *"Throw me into the garden!"* The companions lifted him on their shields and threw him over the wall! Al-Bara' fought alone inside the garden and managed to open the gate from within!

The Muslim army charged in. Inside that garden, **Musaylimah the liar** was finally killed by the spear of **Wahshi ibn Harb**.

The garden became known as **Hadiqat al-Mawt** — the Garden of Death.

## The Price of Victory

The Muslims won, but at a very heavy price. About **700 companions** fell in this battle, and among them were hundreds of **Quran memorizers**.

This loss deeply worried **Umar ibn al-Khattab**. If more and more Quran memorizers died in battle, what if the Quran was lost? Umar urged Abu Bakr: *"We must compile the Quran into one book immediately!"*

This became the beginning of the **compilation of the Quran** — so that Allah's revelation would be preserved forever, not only in people's memories, but also in writing.

## Lessons from Yamamah

The Battle of Yamamah teaches us:

- **The Quran is priceless** — the companions were willing to sacrifice their lives to protect it
- **True bravery** — al-Bara' let himself be thrown alone into the middle of the enemy for victory
- **Every crisis has wisdom** — the fall of the memorizers led to the Quran being compiled into one book
- **Truth always prevails** — even though Musaylimah had 40,000 followers, lies still lost

Allah says: **"Do not think of those who have been killed in the cause of Allah as dead. Rather, they are alive with their Lord."** (QS. Ali Imran: 169)
