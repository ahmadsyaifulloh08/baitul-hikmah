# The Second Pledge of Aqabah 🤝

---

## The Delegates from Madinah

A year earlier, twelve people from the city of Madinah had met Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and accepted Islam. They went home with a teacher named **Mus'ab ibn Umair** to teach Islam in Madinah.

Mus'ab was so good at sharing the message that Islam spread everywhere in Madinah. One by one, tribal leaders began to accept Islam. When the hajj season came, **75 Muslims from Madinah** — 73 men and 2 women — set out for Makkah to meet the Messenger of Allah ﷺ in person.

## A Secret Meeting on the Hill of Aqabah

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ arranged a very secret meeting. The place was the pass of the **Hill of Aqabah** near Mina, the time was midnight — so that the Quraysh would not find out.

The Muslims from Madinah crept out of their tents very carefully, "like birds creeping slowly," said Ka'b ibn Malik who was there. One by one they gathered at the dark mountain pass.

The Prophet came with his uncle **al-Abbas**, who had not yet accepted Islam but wanted to make sure his nephew was safe. Al-Abbas told the people of Madinah: "If you are sure you can protect Muhammad, go ahead. But if not, leave him now."

## A Brave Promise

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said: "I ask you to protect me as you protect your wives and children."

**Al-Bara' ibn Ma'rur**, the eldest leader, immediately reached out his hand: "We will protect you as we protect ourselves!"

Then someone asked: "O Messenger of Allah, if Allah gives you victory later, will you leave us?" The Prophet smiled and answered: "No. My blood is your blood. I am part of you."

Another man stood up and reminded his : "this means? It means we are ready to fight everyone who opposes Muhammad. If you cannot handle that, step back now!"

They answered together: "We are ready! What do we get, O Messenger of Allah?"

The Prophet answered: **"Paradise."**

One by one, all 75 people came forward and shook hands with the Prophet as a sign of their loyal pledge.

## Twelve Leaders

After the pledge, the Prophet chose **12 leaders** called *naqib* — 9 from the Khazraj tribe and 3 from the Aws tribe. Their job was to lead and look after the Muslims in Madinah.

The Prophet told them: "You are responsible for your people, just like the loyal followers of Prophet Isa (Jesus)."

The Second Pledge of Aqabah was incredibly important because it was the **beginning of the hijrah** — the migration of Muslims from Makkah to Madinah. Without the loyal promise of these 75 brave people, the hijrah would never have happened!

**An important lesson:** The courage and loyalty of the people of Madinah teach us that any great cause needs **bravery, honesty, and commitment**. They were not promised wealth or power — only Paradise. And that was enough for them. 🌟
