## 🧑 Who Was He?

Abu Hanifah Nu'man ibn Thabit was born in 699 CE in Kufa, Iraq. His grandfather was a Persian who converted to Islam and became a successful merchant. Abu Hanifah's family was known for their honesty in the silk and textile business.

Kufa was a bustling city filled with scholars and companions of the Prophet. Abu Hanifah grew up in an environment that loved knowledge. Initially, he focused on the family business and was known as a very trustworthy merchant.

Only at age 20 did Abu Hanifah begin seriously studying religion under Hammad ibn Abi Sulayman, a great scholar in Kufa. He studied for 18 years until he became a renowned expert in Islamic law.

## ⭐ What Did He Do?

Abu Hanifah developed very intelligent and flexible methods of Islamic legal thinking. He used the qiyas (analogy) method to solve legal problems that had no direct text in the Quran and Hadith.

He led scholarly assemblies at Kufa Mosque attended by dozens of brilliant students. They discussed and debated various legal cases using systematic methods. Abu Yusuf and Muhammad al-Shaybani were his best students.

When Khalifah al-Mansur offered him a judge position, Abu Hanifah refused to maintain independence in giving fatwas. This refusal led to his imprisonment, but he continued teaching and helping people from within prison.

## 💡 Why Was He Extraordinary?

Abu Hanifah successfully created an Islamic legal system that could adapt to various times and places. His Hanafi school became the most widely followed by Muslims worldwide, especially in Turkey, Central Asia, and India.

His courage in refusing a position from the ruler showed that scholars must be independent and not be influenced by politics. He chose to die in prison rather than compromise his principles.

The methodology he developed allowed Islamic law to remain relevant without losing its spiritual values. The concept of istihsan (choosing the better opinion) provided space to consider the welfare of the community.

## 📖 Verse

Allah SWT says about the importance of knowledge and wisdom:

> وَمَن يُؤْتَ ٱلْحِكْمَةَ فَقَدْ أُوتِىَ خَيْرًا كَثِيرًا ﴿٢٦٩﴾

> *"Whoever is given wisdom has been given much good."* ﴾269﴿
> — QS. Al-Baqarah (2): 269

Abu Hanifah showed that wisdom in understanding religion does not mean ignoring the Quran and Hadith, but rather a way to capture the spirit and purpose of sharia more deeply.

Let us pray: "O Allah, grant us wisdom and courage like Imam Abu Hanifah in learning and practicing Your religion correctly."
