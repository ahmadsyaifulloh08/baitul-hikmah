# Conquest of Constantinople & Hagia Sophia

## 🏰 A Very Important City

Constantinople was a very special city. This city was located between the continents of Asia and Europe, like a bridge connecting two worlds. For 1000 years, this city was ruled by the Byzantine Empire, which was Christian. They built very high and thick walls to protect the city from enemy attacks.

In the center of the city stood a very beautiful building called Hagia Sophia. This building was the largest church in the world with a giant dome that soared high. The Byzantine people were very proud of this building.

## ⚔️ The Brave Young Sultan

Sultan Mehmed II became the leader of the Ottoman Turkish Empire when he was only 21 years old. Although still young, he had a big dream: to conquer Constantinople. Many sultans before him had tried, but always failed because the city walls were too strong.

Mehmed II was different from the others. He was very smart and liked learning new technology. He knew that to conquer a strong city, he needed weapons stronger than those walls. So he ordered giant cannons that could destroy thick walls.

## 🔥 Giant Cannons and Warfare

The cannon made for Sultan Mehmed was very big and frightening. Its length was like two elephants lined up! The sound of this cannon was so loud it could be heard from 16 kilometers away. The cannonball it fired weighed six hundred kilograms - heavier than five cows!

The war lasted for 53 days. Every day, the giant cannon fired at the city walls until they cracked and collapsed. Ottoman forces attacked from both land and sea. Finally, on May 29, 1453 CE, the city walls broke through and Ottoman forces successfully entered Constantinople.

## 🕌 Hagia Sophia Becomes a Mosque

After successfully conquering the city, the first thing Sultan Mehmed did was go to Hagia Sophia. He was amazed by the beauty of this building. Its dome was so high and wide, like the sky covering the earth.

Sultan Mehmed decided to convert Hagia Sophia into a mosque. He did not destroy the beauty of the building, but added tall towers and changed its decorations to Islamic calligraphy. The city's name was also changed to Istanbul. Since then, Hagia Sophia has become the most famous mosque in the world.

## 📖 Lessons and Wisdom

>
> *"Allah has promised those who believe and do righteous deeds that He will grant them succession on earth."* ﴾55﴿

The conquest of Constantinople teaches us that with strong determination, good preparation, and Allah's help, things that seem impossible can be achieved. Sultan Mehmed proved that young age is not a barrier to achieving great dreams.

The way Sultan Mehmed treated the defeated city residents is also exemplary. He did not destroy the existing beauty, but added Islamic touches. This shows that Islam teaches tolerance and appreciation of beauty.
