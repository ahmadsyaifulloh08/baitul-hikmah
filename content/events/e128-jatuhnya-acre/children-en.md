# The Fall of Acre — End of the Crusader Kingdom

## 🌍 What Happened?

Acre was the last and most important city still controlled by the Crusaders in the Holy Land in 1291 CE. After Jerusalem was liberated by Salahuddin, Acre became the capital of the remaining Kingdom of Jerusalem. This port city was also an international trading center connecting Europe with Asia.

Sultan al-Ashraf Khalil of the Mamluks was determined to complete the mission of completely expelling the Crusaders from the Holy Land. He prepared the largest army ever assembled by the Mamluks: 160,000 soldiers! While Acre only had about 40,000 inhabitants and was internally divided among various knightly orders competing with each other.

## 📖 The Story

Sultan Khalil began the siege on April 5, 1291 CE by setting up camp on a hill directly facing Acre. The sight of thousands of tents and Mamluk banners stretching out struck fear into Acre's inhabitants. The Sultan sent an ultimatum to surrender, but Crusader leaders refused because they knew this meant the end of Christian presence in the Holy Land.

The battle began with massive bombardment using 92 giant trebuchets, including one called "al-Mansouri" that could hurl stones weighing 100 kg! The sound of trebuchets thundered day and night, large stones pounded the walls, and flaming projectiles created fires in various city districts.

Acre's strongest tower called "Accursed Tower" became the main target. After weeks of bombardment, this tower cracked and part of the wall collapsed on May 8, opening a large gap for attack.

## 🌟 The Lesson

On May 18, 1291 CE, Sultan Khalil launched a general assault. Thousands of Mamluk soldiers stormed through gaps in the walls while shouting takbir. Guillaume de Beaujeu, Grand Master of the Knights Templar, was killed while leading the heroic last resistance. Acre's defense collapsed within hours.

What happened after that was a great massacre. Sultan Khalil was angry because of the fierce resistance given, so he ordered mass killings. Thousands of civilians were killed, only those who managed to flee to ships in the harbor survived to Cyprus.

After the massacre, Khalil ordered total destruction of Acre: walls were demolished, the harbor was blocked with stones, buildings were razed. St. Andrew's Church was converted to a mosque by replacing bells with the call to prayer and crosses with Arabic calligraphy.

## 🤲 Prayer and Verses

The fall of Acre marked the end of the Crusader Kingdom era in the Middle East after 200 years. Thousands of refugees fled to Cyprus and Europe with traumatic stories. This news shocked Europe, but kings were no longer interested in Crusades because they focused on their domestic problems.

Importantly, the fall of Acre changed the world trade map. Asian spice trade routes controlled by Crusaders were now entirely in Muslim hands, forcing European merchants to seek new routes - this later drove the Age of Exploration and the discovery of the American continent.
