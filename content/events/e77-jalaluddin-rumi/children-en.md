# Jalaluddin Rumi - Poet of Divine Love

## 🧑 Who was he?

Jalaluddin Rumi was an Islamic poet and spiritual teacher who lived 800 years ago. He was born in Balkh, Afghanistan, from an honored scholarly family. His father was a very smart preacher nicknamed "King of Scholars".

When Rumi was young, his family had to move from Balkh to Konya, Turkey, because of Mongol attacks. This long journey made Rumi meet many different Islamic cultures and traditions.

## ⭐ What did he do?

Rumi initially was an ordinary teacher who taught religion at the mosque. But his life changed completely when he met Shams-i Tabrizi, a mysterious dervish who taught love of Allah in a different way.

After meeting Shams, Rumi began writing beautiful poetry about love for Allah. He wrote thousands of very captivating poems. His greatest work was the Masnavi book containing 25,700 lines of wisdom-filled poetry.

Rumi also founded the Mevlevi order famous for whirling dance. His followers spin while remembering Allah, like planets rotating in the sky. This dance is a way to feel Allah's presence.

## 💡 Why was it extraordinary?

What made Rumi special was how he taught that love is the fastest path to be close to Allah. He used very beautiful stories and metaphors to explain spiritual things that are difficult to understand.

Rumi said all creatures are mirrors of Allah's beauty. He compared humans to bamboo reeds longing for their origins, or water drops wanting to return to the sea. His poetry made people feel longing for Allah.

What's great is that Rumi's teachings could be accepted by everyone, not just Muslims. His poetry was translated into many languages and read by millions of people worldwide. He proved that Islam has extraordinary spiritual richness.

## 📖 Verses

Rumi taught that the Quran is the source of all knowledge and wisdom. All his poetry and teachings were based on the Quran and Hadith. He never detached himself from Islamic sharia, but instead deepened its meaning.

In the Masnavi book, Rumi explained that sharia is the skin, tariqa is the flesh, haqiqa is the bone, and ma'rifa is the marrow. All complement each other, cannot be separated.

> وَنَزَّلْنَا عَلَيْكَ الْكِتَابَ تِبْيَانًا لِّكُلِّ شَيْءٍ وَهُدًى وَرَحْمَةً وَبُشْرَىٰ لِلْمُسْلِمِينَ ﴿٨٩﴾
>
> *"And We have sent down to you the Book as clarification for all things and as guidance and mercy and good tidings for the Muslims."* ﴾89﴿

Subhanallah, Rumi showed how rich and beautiful Islam is when understood with hearts full of love for Allah.
