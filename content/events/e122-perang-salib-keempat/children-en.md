# The Fourth Crusade - Deviation to Constantinople

## 🌍 What happened?

In 1202 CE, Crusader soldiers from Europe prepared to go to the Holy Land for war. But they didn't have enough money to rent ships from Venetian merchants.

Doge Enrico Dandolo, the old and blind leader of Venice, had a cunning idea. He said, "You can board my ships, but help me recapture the city of Zara that rebelled." The Crusaders agreed because they had no other choice.

## 📖 The story

After capturing Zara, the Crusaders met Prince Alexius from Constantinople. This prince said his uncle had unfairly driven his father from the throne. He asked for help from the Crusaders and promised to give lots of gold.

The Crusaders who still needed money agreed to help. They sailed to Constantinople, the largest city in the world at that time. This city had stood for 900 years as the center of Orthodox Christianity.

The first attack succeeded. Alexius's father became emperor again. But they couldn't fulfill their promise to give gold. The people of Constantinople became angry and killed Prince Alexius.

## 🌟 The lesson

The Crusaders became very angry because they felt deceived. In 1204 CE, they attacked Constantinople again and this time succeeded in conquering it. What happened next was very sad.

They looted everything in the city. The beautiful Hagia Sophia church was damaged. Ancient books and priceless artworks were destroyed. Many people died in this tragedy.

This was one of the saddest events in Christian religious history. Fellow Christians fought each other and destroyed their own holy city. The Orthodox and Catholic churches became increasingly hostile.

## 🤲 Prayer & Verses

This tragedy teaches how important unity is among fellow believers. When fellow brothers fight, they themselves suffer the loss. Outside enemies actually benefit.

Allah SWT teaches to always unite and not separate. Internal disputes are far more dangerous than external threats. Leaders should maintain unity, not seek personal gain.

> وَاعْتَصِمُوا بِحَبْلِ اللَّهِ جَمِيعًا وَلَا تَفَرَّقُوا ﴿١٠٣﴾
>
> *"And hold fast, all of you together, to the rope of Allah, and do not separate."* ﴾103﴿

Subhanallah, how valuable unity is and how dangerous division caused by greed.
