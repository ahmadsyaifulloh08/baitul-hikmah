# Uniting the Quran's Recitation 📖

---

## A Big Problem Among Muslims

After Islam spread to many lands, Muslims in different places were reading the Quran in slightly different ways. The words and meaning were the same — but the way people pronounced them was different.

Soldiers from Iraq and Syria gathered in one army camp. When they recited the Quran together, they were shocked that their readings sounded different! Each group said: *"Our way is the correct one!"*

A companion named **Hudhayfah ibn al-Yaman** saw this argument and became very worried. He rushed to Caliph **Uthman ibn Affan** in Madinah and said: *"Save this nation before they fight over the Quran!"*

## The Quran Writing Team

Caliph Uthman acted right away! He formed a special team led by **Zayd ibn Thabit** — the companion who had already written the Quran during Caliph Abu Bakr's time.

The team worked very carefully:
- 📜 They used the original *mushaf* kept by **Hafsah bint Umar** (Caliph Umar's daughter)
- 🧠 They checked every verse against the memorization of Quran memorizers
- ✍️ They copied the *mushaf* with great care

Uthman gave them an important instruction: if there was any difference in how to write a word, use the language of the Quraysh tribe — because the Quran was first revealed in their language.

## Copies Sent Across the Lands

When the work was done, the Quran was copied into several identical versions. Uthman sent one copy to each major city: **Makkah**, **Basra**, **Kufa**, **Syria**, and one was kept in **Madinah**.

Here's the special part — each copy was sent with an **expert Quran teacher**! So it wasn't just a book that was sent, but also a teacher to go with it.

Then, all the old copies that were different were ordered to be destroyed. This was a tough decision, but it was very important so that all Muslims around the world would read the **exact same Quran**.

## The Quran We Read Today

Did you know? The Quran we read today — anywhere in the world — comes from Caliph Uthman's project in 650 CE! That's why the Quran is often called the **Uthmanic Mushaf**.

Allah says in the Quran: **"Indeed, it is We who sent down the Reminder, and indeed, We will be its guardian."** (QS Al-Hijr: 9)

Thanks to Uthman's courage and the hard work of the writing team, the Quran has remained perfectly preserved for over **1,400 years**. From Madinah to Jakarta, from Cairo to Tokyo — all Muslims read the same Quran. Subhanallah! 🌍
