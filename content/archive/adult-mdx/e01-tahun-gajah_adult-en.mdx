# The Year of the Elephant and the Birth of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ (570 CE)

## Historical and Geographical Context

### The Arabian Peninsula in the 6th Century CE

In the mid-6th century CE, the Arabian Peninsula was a politically fragmented region. The two superpowers of the era — the Byzantine Empire (Eastern Rome) to the north and the Sassanid Empire (Persia) to the east — competed for influence over the area. In the south, the Himyarite Kingdom of Yemen had fallen to the Kingdom of Aksum (Ethiopia/Abyssinia), a powerful Christian kingdom in East Africa.

Makkah, located in the arid valley of the Hijaz, held a position of unique spiritual and commercial significance. The city derived its special status from the Ka'bah — the house of worship built by Prophet Ibrahim (Abraham) AS and his son Isma'il (Ishmael) AS. The Ka'bah served as the central pilgrimage site for all Arab tribes, making Makkah both a sacred city and a thriving trade hub. The Quraysh tribe, descendants of Isma'il AS, held custodianship over the Ka'bah and leadership of Makkah.

### Abrahah and His Political Ambitions

Abrahah al-Ashram (أبرهة الأشرم) was an Abyssinian (Ethiopian) general who became the governor of Yemen on behalf of the Aksumite Negus. After overthrowing his predecessor Aryat, Abrahah established himself as the de facto ruler of Yemen. He built a magnificent cathedral in Sana'a called al-Qullays (القُلَّيْس), aiming to divert the Arabs' pilgrimage from the Ka'bah in Makkah to his church.

According to the well-known accounts in the books of sirah, when this plan failed — the Arabs remained loyal to the Ka'bah — Abrahah resolved to destroy the Ka'bah directly. He mobilized a large army equipped with war elephants, including a great elephant named Mahmud.

## The Event of the Year of the Elephant ('Ām al-Fīl)

### The March of the Elephant Army

Abrahah departed from Yemen with a formidable army heading for Makkah. Along the way, several Arab tribes attempted to resist him but were defeated. When Abrahah's forces reached the outskirts of Makkah, they seized the livestock of the Makkan people, including 200 camels belonging to Abdul Muttalib ibn Hashim, the leader of Banu Hashim and custodian of the Ka'bah.

### The Dialogue Between Abdul Muttalib and Abrahah

Abdul Muttalib (عبد المطلب بن هاشم) went to meet Abrahah to request the return of his camels. Abrahah was astonished — he had expected Abdul Muttalib to negotiate about the Ka'bah, not about camels. Abdul Muttalib responded with his famous statement:

> "I am the owner of the camels, and I am claiming them. As for the Ka'bah, it has a Lord who will protect it."
>
> (أنا رب الإبل، وللبيت رب يحميه)

This statement reflects Abdul Muttalib's profound conviction in Allah's protection of the Ka'bah. This account is recorded in various books of sirah, including Ibn Hisham's *al-Sirah al-Nabawiyyah* and al-Mubarakfuri's *al-Rahiq al-Makhtum*.

### The Destruction of the Elephant Army

As Abrahah's forces prepared to enter Makkah, their lead elephant, Mahmud, refused to advance toward the Ka'bah. Every time it was directed toward Makkah, the elephant knelt down and would not move. Yet when directed in any other direction, it walked normally.

Then Allah SWT sent flocks of birds (ṭayran abābīl — طير أبابيل) carrying stones of baked clay (ḥijāratin min sijjīl — حجارة من سجيل). These stones rained down upon Abrahah's army, destroying them until they became like chewed-up leaves (ka'aṣfin ma'kūl — كعصف مأكول). Abrahah himself was struck and died on his return journey to Yemen, his body deteriorating gradually.

This event took place in the month of Muharram, 570 CE (or 571 CE according to some opinions), and the year became known as *'Ām al-Fīl* (the Year of the Elephant) — a landmark date for the Arabs.

## Qur'anic Evidence: Surah Al-Fil (QS. 105:1-5)

Allah SWT immortalized this event in the Qur'an:

### Verse 1
**أَلَمْ تَرَ كَيْفَ فَعَلَ رَبُّكَ بِأَصْحَابِ الْفِيلِ**

*Alam tara kayfa fa'ala Rabbuka bi aṣḥāb al-fīl*

"Have you not seen how your Lord dealt with the People of the Elephant?"

### Verse 2
**أَلَمْ يَجْعَلْ كَيْدَهُمْ فِي تَضْلِيلٍ**

*Alam yaj'al kaydahum fī taḍlīl*

"Did He not make their treacherous plan go astray?"

### Verse 3
**وَأَرْسَلَ عَلَيْهِمْ طَيْرًا أَبَابِيلَ**

*Wa arsala 'alayhim ṭayran abābīl*

"And He sent against them birds in flocks,"

### Verse 4
**تَرْمِيهِمْ بِحِجَارَةٍ مِنْ سِجِّيلٍ**

*Tarmīhim bi ḥijāratin min sijjīl*

"striking them with stones of baked clay,"

### Verse 5
**فَجَعَلَهُمْ كَعَصْفٍ مَأْكُولٍ**

*Faja'alahum ka'aṣfin ma'kūl*

"and He made them like eaten-up straw."

### Brief Tafsir

Imam Ibn Kathir in *Tafsir al-Qur'an al-'Azhim* explains that this surah serves as a reminder to Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and the Quraysh of Allah's favor in protecting the Ka'bah. The event was a divine miracle witnessed directly by the people of Makkah and all the Arabs, standing as clear proof of Allah's power.

Imam al-Tabari narrates from Ibn Abbas RA that each of the Ababil birds carried three stones — one in its beak and one in each claw — and every stone that struck a person killed him.

## The Birth of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

### Date and Year of Birth

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was born in the Year of the Elephant, agreed upon by the majority of scholars as 570 CE. There is scholarly difference regarding the exact date:

- **12th Rabi' al-Awwal** — the majority opinion, supported by Ibn Ishaq and Ibn Hisham
- **8th Rabi' al-Awwal** — an alternative view, supported by some Muslim astronomers
- **9th Rabi' al-Awwal** — the view strengthened by al-Mubarakfuri in *al-Rahiq al-Makhtum* based on modern astronomical calculations

His birth coincided with a Monday. This is confirmed by an authentic hadith:

> It was narrated from Abu Qatadah al-Ansari RA that the Messenger of Allah ﷺ was asked about fasting on Mondays, and he said:
>
> **ذَاكَ يَوْمٌ وُلِدْتُ فِيهِ وَيَوْمٌ بُعِثْتُ أَوْ أُنْزِلَ عَلَيَّ فِيهِ**
>
> *Dhāka yawmun wulidtu fīhi wa yawmun bu'ithtu aw unzila 'alayya fīhi*
>
> "That is the day on which I was born and the day on which I was sent — or revelation was sent down to me."
>
> (Sahih Muslim, no. 1162)

### Lineage (Nasab) of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

Muhammad ﷺ ibn Abdullah ibn Abdul Muttalib ibn Hashim ibn Abd Manaf ibn Qusayy ibn Kilab ibn Murrah ibn Ka'b ibn Lu'ayy ibn Ghalib ibn Fihr ibn Malik ibn al-Nadr ibn Kinanah ibn Khuzaymah ibn Mudrikah ibn Ilyas ibn Mudar ibn Nizar ibn Ma'add ibn Adnan.

This lineage is narrated through mutawatir (mass-transmitted) chains and is agreed upon by all scholars of sirah and genealogy.

### Father: Abdullah ibn Abdul Muttalib

Abdullah ibn Abdul Muttalib (عبد الله بن عبد المطلب) was the youngest and most beloved son of Abdul Muttalib. He married Aminah bint Wahb of Banu Zuhrah. Abdullah passed away before the birth of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ — according to the stronger opinion, he died while Aminah was pregnant, during a trading journey to al-Sham (Syria). He was buried in Madinah (then known as Yathrib).

### Mother: Aminah bint Wahb

Aminah bint Wahb (آمنة بنت وهب) was from Banu Zuhrah, one of the most prominent families in Makkah. She carried and gave birth to Prophet Muhammad ﷺ as a young widow. Aminah passed away when the Prophet was approximately 6 years old, on the return journey from Madinah after visiting Abdullah's grave. She was buried at al-Abwa', between Makkah and Madinah.

### Abdul Muttalib: Grandfather and Guardian

After the deaths of Abdullah and then Aminah, Abdul Muttalib assumed guardianship of his grandson Muhammad. Abdul Muttalib was the chief of Banu Hashim and the custodian of the Ka'bah and Zamzam well. He deeply loved his grandson and would always bring him to the assemblies of the Quraysh. Abdul Muttalib passed away when the Prophet was 8 years old, after which guardianship passed to Abu Talib ibn Abdul Muttalib.

## Significance of the Event

### 1. Divine Protection of the Ka'bah

The Year of the Elephant demonstrates that Allah SWT protected the Ka'bah directly through His own power, not through human strength. The people of Makkah at the time were unable to resist Abrahah's army — they evacuated to the surrounding hills. The protection came purely from Allah SWT.

### 2. Divine Preparation for the Prophet's Birth

Many scholars observe the correlation between the Year of the Elephant and the birth of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in the same year. Ibn Kathir notes that this event constituted an *irhāṣ* (إرهاص) — a precursory sign — of the prophethood of Muhammad ﷺ. Allah cleansed the Ka'bah from the threat of destruction, preparing it for the mission of the final Prophet.

### 3. A Chronological Landmark for the Arabs

The Arabs did not have a unified dating system before the Hijri calendar. The Year of the Elephant became a widely used chronological reference — Arabs would refer to events as occurring "so many years before/after the Year of the Elephant."

### 4. Proof of Allah's Power Over Military Might

Abrahah's army brought the most advanced military technology of the time — war elephants — never before encountered by the Arabs. Yet Allah destroyed them with small creatures: birds carrying stones. This stands as a lesson that material power can never overcome the will of Allah SWT.

## Related Hadiths

### Birth on a Monday
The hadith of Abu Qatadah RA about the Prophet's birth on Monday (Sahih Muslim, no. 1162) — **Sahih (Authentic)**.

### Nursing by Halimah al-Sa'diyyah
It is narrated that after birth, Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was first nursed by Thuwaybah (a bondwoman of Abu Lahab), then fostered and nursed by Halimah bint Abi Dhu'ayb al-Sa'diyyah in the desert of Banu Sa'd. The account of Halimah's fosterage is detailed at length in Ibn Hisham's *al-Sirah al-Nabawiyyah*.

**Methodological note**: Accounts of miraculous signs at the time of the Prophet's birth (the palace of Chosroes cracking, the fire of the Magians being extinguished, Lake Sawah drying up) are mentioned in various books of sirah but **their chains of narration are disputed**. Some hadith scholars grade them as da'if (weak). Therefore, we do not present them as established facts and confine ourselves to what is thabit (firmly established) from authentic sources. *Wallahu a'lam*.

## Elaborative Perspectives

### Synthesis (Methodology of al-Ghazali)
The Year of the Elephant is not merely a military-historical event but a manifestation of *'ināyah ilāhiyyah* (divine providence) demonstrating that human history operates under Allah's governance. Al-Ghazali in *Ihya' Ulum al-Din* emphasizes that understanding the signs of Allah's power in history is part of *tafakkur* (contemplation) that draws the servant closer to his Lord.

### Rationalism (Methodology of Ibn Rushd)
From a rational perspective, this event can be understood as a supernatural intervention transcending material cause-and-effect. Ibn Rushd in *Tahāfut al-Tahāfut* acknowledges that miracles and extraordinary occurrences (*khawāriq al-'ādah*) are possible within the framework of divine will, while emphasizing the importance of not dismissing secondary causes.

### Critical Analysis and Evidence (Methodology of Ibn Taymiyyah)
Ibn Taymiyyah in *Minhāj al-Sunnah* and *Iqtiḍā' al-Ṣirāṭ al-Mustaqīm* affirms that the Year of the Elephant is a historical fact confirmed by the Qur'an itself (QS. Al-Fil), thus requiring no external validation. He also cautions against adding weak narrations to events that are already clearly established through the Qur'an and authentic hadiths.
